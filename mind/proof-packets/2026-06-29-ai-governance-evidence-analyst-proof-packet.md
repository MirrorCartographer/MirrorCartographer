# Proof Packet — AI Governance Evidence Analyst

Date: 2026-06-29  
Status: first public-safe proof packet  
Constraint: does not rely on Mirror Cartographer novelty language.

## Role-relevant skill claim

Can convert ambiguous AI product and research claims into structured evidence-governance artifacts that separate fact, inference, uncertainty, evaluation criteria, falsification paths, and lifecycle status.

## Work sample set

1. Claim registry / claim graph  
   Demonstrates ability to prevent isolated evidence documents from becoming ungoverned claims.

2. Negative result ledger  
   Demonstrates ability to preserve failed or inconclusive findings and avoid confirmation-only reporting.

3. Provenance retrofit queue  
   Demonstrates ability to distinguish repository storage from auditable evidence.

4. Combined next-proof test plan  
   Demonstrates ability to translate broad claims into scored tests and falsification conditions.

5. Architecture pruning audit  
   Demonstrates ability to classify active, experimental, superseded, archived, and retired artifacts.

## What this proves

- Evidence organization skill.
- Risk-boundary thinking.
- Claim-status discipline.
- Ability to turn vague AI claims into evaluation-ready structures.
- Awareness of overclaim, external validity, construct validity, causal boundary, and provenance limitations.

## What this does not prove

- Formal legal compliance authority.
- Clinical safety validation.
- That any AI product is effective.
- That the artifacts improve real-world decisions yet.
- That the work generalizes across organizations without adaptation.

## Best-fit role language

Evidence governance analyst for human-AI systems, focused on claim evaluation, safety-boundary documentation, provenance, falsification planning, and audit-ready research synthesis.

## Hiring-reader test

A reader should be able to answer:

1. What skill is demonstrated?
2. Which artifact demonstrates it?
3. What risk does the artifact reduce?
4. What claim remains unproven?

If the reader cannot answer those four questions without being persuaded by branding, the packet fails.
