## Offer name

Living ViewDiff: AI Output Autopsy for Teams

## Problem

Teams are using AI outputs without seeing how private notes, vague prompts, source material, assumptions, and generated claims were transformed into final artifacts.

This creates risks:

- uncritical adoption
- hidden claim inflation
- source confusion
- privacy leakage
- loss of human interpretive control
- inability to teach better use

## Public-safe MC wedge

A short workshop or demo that takes a fictional or customer-provided non-sensitive example and produces:

1. source boundary map
2. claim boundary map
3. privacy boundary map
4. ViewDiff between source and output
5. improvement plan for safer AI use

## Why this is realistic

AI literacy demand is moving from generic tool training toward workflow-specific judgment, validation, uncertainty, provenance, and critical evaluation. That aligns with MC’s current strongest public-safe capability: making transformation visible.

## Audience

Best early audiences:

- educators designing AI literacy lessons
- nonprofits adopting AI carefully
- small teams using AI for reports, grants, or communications
- care-adjacent organizations that need strong privacy hygiene
- creative teams documenting human-AI co-creation without losing authorship clarity

## Minimum viable proof

Build one fictional demo:

Input: synthetic source packet
Output: polished public artifact
Middle: Living ViewDiff showing what changed
Score: privacy, claim-boundary, source-boundary, evidence-fit, audience-fit

## Revenue shape

Potential formats:

- paid workshop
- audit package
- template pack
- custom documentation system
- internal AI-literacy training
- public demo leading to consulting

## Boundary

Do not market this as medical advice, legal compliance, therapy, certification, or guaranteed risk elimination. Market it as AI literacy, provenance hygiene, and transformation documentation.
