## Offer name
**Consent Gradient Audit for AI Notes**

## Plain description
A short-form audit that checks whether an AI-generated note, summary, artifact, or knowledge-base entry safely separates:

- private source material;
- publishable method;
- observation;
- interpretation;
- hypothesis;
- claim;
- professional-review requirement;
- missingness;
- revision history.

## Buyer problem
Many people and small teams are using AI to summarize sensitive material, then publishing or sharing outputs without knowing what leaked, what was transformed, what claim strength was implied, or what should have stayed private.

## Public-safe deliverable
One-page audit containing:

1. source boundary table;
2. claim boundary table;
3. privacy boundary table;
4. ViewDiff summary;
5. missingness list;
6. risk flags;
7. rewrite recommendation;
8. public-safe version if appropriate.

## Likely buyer segments
- AI-heavy creators turning private thinking into public artifacts.
- Coaches and consultants handling sensitive client notes.
- Research-adjacent groups using AI summaries.
- Patient/caregiver advocacy projects needing communication clarity without clinical claims.
- AI literacy workshops needing a concrete exercise.
- Small teams building knowledge bases from messy human context.

- Not therapy.

- Not privacy guarantee.

## Smallest paid test
Create a fictional before/after demo:

- Input: fictional messy private note.
- Output A: unsafe public summary.

## Evaluation criteria
The offer is worth keeping only if a buyer can understand within five minutes:

- what was unsafe;
- what was preserved;
- what was transformed;
- what was blocked;
- what can safely be published;
- what still needs human or expert review.
