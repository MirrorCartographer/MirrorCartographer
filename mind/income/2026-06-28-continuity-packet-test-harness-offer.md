# Income Lane: Continuity Packet Test Harness Audit

Date: 2026-06-28
Attractor: Discovery
Privacy status: public-safe offer design
Claim status: realistic adjacent opportunity, not revenue proof

## Offer
A compact audit for teams using AI to summarize or transform complex human input.

## Buyer problem
Teams are adopting AI summaries faster than they can inspect source boundaries, consent limits, claim strength, missing data, and view-specific transformations.

## Deliverable
For one fictional, public, or scrubbed workflow:
1. Minimum Complete Continuity Packet map
2. permissioned view set
3. ViewDiff ledger
4. claim-boundary failure list
5. privacy and reconstruction-risk notes
6. scoring rubric
7. next implementation test

## Likely buyer lanes
- AI literacy workshops
- responsible AI documentation teams
- care-navigation startups
- patient-education or social-support documentation teams
- creator/research knowledge-base teams
- nonprofits using AI to summarize sensitive stories
- small teams preparing grant, evaluation, or governance materials

## Pricing hypothesis
Start as a low-friction paid audit or workshop deliverable, not a platform sale. The first paid version should be narrow enough to finish quickly and concrete enough to show before/after value.

## Public-safe demo requirement
Use only fictional fixtures or public-domain examples. Do not use personal histories, raw chats, household details, health records, animal-care records, financial records, or private user examples as sales material.

## Evaluation criteria
- Buyer can see where AI changed meaning.
- Buyer can see where claims became too strong.
- Buyer can see privacy leakage risk before publication.
- Buyer receives a usable scoring rubric, not just advice.
- Buyer can repeat the method on another workflow.

## Missingness
No market validation, no buyer interviews, no price testing, no case study, no legal review, no security review.

## Revision reason
Created to turn the continuity-packet test harness into a realistic service wedge rather than another abstract MC concept.
