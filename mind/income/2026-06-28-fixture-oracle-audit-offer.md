# Income Lane: Fixture Oracle Audit Offer

## Source status
Public-safe business synthesis from Mirror Cartographer's boundary architecture and current AI provenance / literacy / documentation-risk research.

## Claim status
Market hypothesis. Not validated revenue.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. No client, personal, or private examples.

## Missingness

## Revision reason
The fixture runner offer becomes stronger when it includes expected-output oracles. Buyers need proof that the audit is not merely subjective review.

## Offer name
AI Public Artifact Boundary Audit

## Buyer problem
Teams using AI to generate public-facing documents need to know whether outputs preserve source boundaries, privacy boundaries, claim strength, audience intent, and review requirements.

## Narrow deliverable
A short audit report that runs 5 to 10 synthetic fixtures against a team's AI-assisted publication workflow.

## What the audit checks
- Does the workflow label sources?
- Does it prevent overclaiming?
- Does it preserve uncertainty?
- Does it route sensitive material to abstraction or review?
- Does it distinguish symbolic, operational, product, research, governance, and support claims?
- Does it produce a release decision with a revision reason?

## Why this is realistic
This is easier to sell than the whole Mirror Cartographer system because the buyer gets a concrete artifact: a boundary-readiness scorecard and a short list of failure modes.

## First public-safe demo
Create a synthetic demo with three fixture classes:
1. a product note with uncertain source status,
2. a support observation that requires review,
3. a public explainer that risks overclaiming.

Run each through:
fixture -> oracle -> output -> scorecard -> release decision.

## Pricing hypothesis
- Starter audit: low fixed price for 5 fixtures and one report.
- Standard audit: 10 to 20 fixtures, scorecard, and revision plan.
- Retainer: monthly regression checks for teams publishing AI-assisted artifacts.

## Key phrase
The first product is not the mirror. The first product is proof that the mirror knows when not to publish.
