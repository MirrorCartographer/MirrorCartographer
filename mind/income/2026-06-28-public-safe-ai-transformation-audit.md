# Public-Safe AI Transformation Audit

## Source status
Public-safe product requirement derived from the Mirror Compiler test matrix and current market pressure around AI literacy, provenance, and responsible AI use.

## Claim status
Business hypothesis. Not validated by sales, user interviews, or paid pilots.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. No private user, client, medical, financial, or household details.

## Missingness
- Needs a one-page demo.
- Needs pricing validation.
- Needs target-customer interviews.
- Needs before/after examples built from fictional data only.
- Needs clear non-medical, non-legal positioning.

## Revision reason
Previous income lanes identified AI literacy, boundary-visible design, and continuity-packet audits. This pass narrows the offer into a concrete, realistic service that can generate income without exposing private context or making clinical claims.

## Offer name
Public-Safe AI Transformation Audit

## Buyer problem
Teams are using AI to summarize, rewrite, transform, and publish sensitive or high-context material, but many cannot clearly show:

- what source material was used;
- what changed during AI transformation;
- what claims were upgraded or weakened;
- what was removed or generalized for privacy;
- what remains uncertain;
- what requires human review before publication or action.

## Offer promise
A short audit that turns one AI-assisted artifact into a transparent transformation packet with source, claim, privacy, missingness, ViewDiff, and revision labels.

## Deliverables
1. One public-safe transformation map.
2. One ViewDiff ledger.
3. One claim-boundary table.
4. One privacy-risk note.
5. One revision recommendation list.
6. One reusable template for future AI-assisted outputs.

## Best-fit customers
- small research teams using AI summaries;
- creators publishing AI-assisted essays, decks, or websites;
- nonprofits turning lived-experience material into public-safe reports;
- consultants using AI to transform client notes into deliverables;
- educators teaching AI literacy and critical evaluation.

## Explicit exclusions
This is not:

- legal review;
- clinical review;
- therapy;
- medical documentation;
- veterinary guidance;
- cybersecurity certification;
- guaranteed compliance approval.

## Pricing hypothesis
- Starter audit: fixed-fee review of one artifact.
- Template package: audit plus reusable schema.
- Workshop: teach a small team how to label source, claim, privacy, missingness, and revision boundaries.

## Proof object needed next
A fictional before/after demo showing:

private-style input → AI draft → compiler-labeled public-safe output → ViewDiff → final publish/revise/block decision.

## Evaluation metric
A buyer should be able to answer within two minutes:

“What changed, what can be trusted, what is missing, and what should not be published yet?”
