# Income Lane: Artifact Boundary Bill Audit Offer

## Offer

A practical Mirror Cartographer-adjacent service:

**Public Artifact Boundary Audit** — review AI-assisted documents, websites, research notes, product claims, or public knowledge-base entries and produce an Artifact Bill of Boundaries.

## Buyer problem

Organizations increasingly need to publish AI-assisted work without exposing private input, overstating claims, hiding uncertainty, or losing provenance.

## Public-safe deliverable

For each artifact, deliver:

1. source status,
2. claim status,
3. privacy status,
4. audience contract,
5. missingness register,
6. revision reason,
7. release recommendation,
8. plain-language risk summary.

## Why this is realistic

This is smaller and more sellable than the full Mirror Cartographer system. It can be offered as documentation governance, release-readiness review, AI provenance support, or public-knowledge transformation review.

## Likely customers

- small AI product teams,
- researchers using AI-assisted drafting,
- health-adjacent digital support tools,
- nonprofits publishing sensitive knowledge,
- creators turning private notes into public artifacts,
- consultants managing client knowledge bases.

## Evidence fit

2026 AI governance research is moving toward structured provenance, lifecycle assurance, inspectable workflow objects, and disclosure beyond simple AI labels. This supports a documentation-audit wedge.

## First product shape

A one-page audit card plus an optional deeper report.

### Basic card

- artifact title
- can publish / revise / hold private / discard
- top boundary risk
- top missing evidence
- claim limit
- audience limit
- recommended next action

### Deep report

- Artifact Bill of Boundaries
- ViewDiff-style transformation summary
- fixture test recommendation
- release checklist
