# Income Lane — ViewDiff AI Insight Audit

Status labels

- Source status: derived from public-safe MC product direction and current market pressure around AI provenance, semantic drift, and reviewability.
- Claim status: business hypothesis; not validated revenue.
- Privacy status: public-safe; no client or private user material.
- Missingness: no price testing, landing page, testimonials, sample audit, or buyer interviews yet.
- Revision reason: added practical income lane for the ViewDiff Discovery Engine.

## Offer

A paid review of an AI-generated or AI-assisted artifact that answers one practical question:

Did this artifact create real new usable value, or did it mostly repackage the source with confident language?

## Buyer types

- solo founders
- consultants
- researchers
- grant writers
- small teams using AI for strategy or documentation
- care-support organizations that need non-diagnostic communication clarity
- creative teams using AI to transform concepts across media

## Deliverable

A concise report with:

- source status
- claim status
- privacy status
- missingness
- transformation map
- preserved invariant
- semantic drift / distortion notes
- overclaim warnings
- discovery score
- next-test plan
- release recommendation

## Pricing hypothesis

Low-friction first product:

- Mini audit: fixed-scope review of one artifact.
- Standard audit: one artifact plus two transformation lenses.
- Workshop: team brings artifacts and learns ViewDiff scoring.

No revenue claim is made here.

This is a testable offer hypothesis.

## Why this is realistic

The market already has pressure around AI provenance, content authenticity, semantic drift, and governance.

MC's angle is narrower and more usable:

not `is this AI?`

but `what did AI change, and is the change worth trusting?`

## First public-safe demo

Use a synthetic AI-generated strategy memo.

Run it through the ViewDiff Discovery Scorecard.

Publish:

- before source
- transformed artifact
- ViewDiff report
- discovery score
- release recommendation

## Failure condition

The offer fails if buyers do not understand the difference between an ordinary edit and a ViewDiff audit, or if the report does not change their next action.

## Key phrase

Most AI audits ask whether a machine touched the artifact. MC asks whether the artifact survived the touch.
