## Offer name

Care View Adapter: turn messy notes into bounded observation packets.

## Public-safe pitch

A compact AI-literacy and communication demo showing how one private fictional record becomes:

1. a personal view,
2. a care-helper view,
3. a professional-facing observation packet,
4. a public-safe method view,
5. a research-safe aggregate,

with a visible ViewDiff showing what changed, what was removed, what claim strength is allowed, and what remains missing.

## Realistic customer lanes

### 1. AI literacy workshops

Audience: educators, nonprofits, community programs, workforce boards, small teams.
Pain: people use AI summaries without knowing what changed between source and output.
Demo value: teaches source status, claim status, privacy status, missingness, and revision reason using a concrete care-adjacent example.

### 2. Patient / caregiver communication education

Audience: patient advocates, care navigators, chronic-condition support communities, veterinary-client education groups, elder-care coordinators.
Pain: lived observations become scattered, emotional, too long, or too private to share cleanly.
Demo value: converts observation into questions and timelines without claiming to diagnose.

### 3. Documentation boundary consulting

Audience: small health-adjacent or wellness-adjacent teams that need safer AI summary workflows.
Pain: teams risk overclaiming, leaking sensitive details, or producing summaries whose transformations are invisible.
Demo value: provides a reviewable summary boundary rubric.

A one-page fictional demo packet:

## Why this is adjacent to MC

The offer does not sell medical capability. It sells the MC method: permissioned meaning transformation with visible source, claim, privacy, missingness, and revision labels.

## Evidence signals to cite in public-facing material

- AI literacy frameworks now emphasize critical evaluation, validation, and progression beyond uncritical use.
- AI transparency and provenance research is moving toward architecture-level disclosure and cross-layer verification.
- Ambient clinical documentation adoption shows demand for structured summaries, while consent and privacy concerns show why bounded transformations matter.
- Health-data leak reporting shows that even partial or supposedly anonymized records can create privacy risk when exposed.

## Validation plan

1. Build one fictional Care View Adapter demo.
2. Ask five non-expert users whether they can identify what changed between views.
3. Ask two domain-adjacent reviewers whether the professional-facing view avoids diagnosis and preserves useful questions.
4. Score using privacy safety, claim boundary, audience fit, and missingness visibility.
5. Iterate the card before building software.

## Boundary

Do not sell this as clinical documentation, triage, diagnosis, therapy, veterinary care, legal compliance, or emergency support. Sell it as AI literacy, communication hygiene, and provenance-aware summarization.
