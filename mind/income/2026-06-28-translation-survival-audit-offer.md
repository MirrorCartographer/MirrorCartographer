# Income Lane — Translation Survival Audit Offer

Status labels

- Source status: derived from public-safe MC architecture, current GitHub mind direction, and public research on semantic invariance/provenance.
- Claim status: product hypothesis, not validated revenue.
- Privacy status: public-safe; no personal or private financial details.
- Missingness: no customer interviews, landing page, pricing test, or case study yet.
- Revision reason: created to keep the practical income lane attached to the dangerous-question research.

## Offer name

Translation Survival Audit

## One-line offer

We test whether your idea survives translation across formats without losing its core meaning, claim status, source boundary, or release integrity.

## Buyer types

Potential early buyers:

- AI product teams releasing public claims
- researchers translating work for non-experts
- founders preparing pitch decks
- therapists/coaches/educators using AI-generated public content
- artists or writers turning private material into public artifacts
- nonprofits converting lived experience into policy or education material

## Pain

People use AI to transform ideas into posts, decks, papers, websites, protocols, and pitches.

The transformed artifact may sound better while quietly changing the meaning.

Common failures:

- speculation becomes claim
- uncertainty disappears
- privacy boundary weakens
- source lineage disappears
- metaphor becomes false authority
- product copy overpromises
- clinical/care language becomes too strong
- the beautiful version is less true than the ugly version

## Product

A fixed-scope audit of one artifact and its source-safe abstract.

Deliverables:

1. Translation Survival Score
2. ViewDiff report
3. Claim-status correction
4. Privacy-boundary note
5. Missingness label
6. Revision recommendations
7. Optional public-safe release note

## Evidence alignment

Public research on semantic invariance and paraphrase robustness supports the problem: meaning can drift across equivalent-looking formulations.

Provenance/AIBOM work supports the need for lifecycle records and transformation traceability.

## What not to claim

Do not claim:

- guaranteed truth
- legal compliance
- clinical safety certification
- investment readiness
- medical validity
- objective understanding

Allowed claim:

This audit helps identify meaning drift, boundary drift, claim drift, and release-risk before publication.
