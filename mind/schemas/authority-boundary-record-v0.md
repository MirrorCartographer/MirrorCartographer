# AuthorityBoundaryRecord v0

Status labels

- Source status: derived from MC public-safe architecture and current research pass.
- Claim status: schema proposal; not yet implemented or validated.
- Privacy status: public-safe; contains no private source material.
- Missingness: needs runtime integration, UI display rules, and test fixtures.
- Revision reason: created to prevent reflective artifacts from being misread as diagnostic, therapeutic, legal, financial, veterinary, credential, safety, or objective-truth authority.

## Purpose

An `AuthorityBoundaryRecord` names the permitted authority level of an MC artifact.

It answers:

What is this artifact allowed to be?

What is it not allowed to be?

What would make it dangerous if misread?

## Required fields

### artifact_id

Stable identifier for the artifact being bounded.

### artifact_type

Examples:

- reflection card
- field map
- ViewDiff ledger
- contradiction panel
- public-safe research note
- preparation packet
- product requirement
- audit report

### source_status

Where the structure came from.

Use one or more:

- user-provided public material
- private-context-derived abstraction
- uploaded-file-derived abstraction
- GitHub-derived artifact
- external-source-derived research
- synthetic fixture
- model-generated hypothesis

### claim_status

Use one:

- observation
- interpretation
- symbolic reading
- hypothesis
- design requirement
- evaluation criterion
- implementation plan
- source-boundary note
- unsupported speculation
- externally sourced claim

### privacy_status

Use one:

- public-safe
- private-only
- publishable with redaction
- synthetic-only
- blocked from publication

### authority_class

Use one:

- 0_orientation
- 1_reflective_hypothesis
- 2_preparation_artifact
- 3_action_support_with_external_anchor
- 4_prohibited_authority

### allowed_uses

What the artifact may support.

### forbidden_uses

What the artifact must not be used for.

### high_stakes_domains_detected

Boolean flags:

- medical
- veterinary
- mental_health
- legal
- financial
- credential
- safety
- relationship
- employment
- housing
- identity_claim

### downgrade_rule

How the artifact is reduced when it touches a high-stakes area.

Examples:

- Convert to question list.
- Convert to observation organizer.
- Convert to source checklist.
- Convert to expert-review packet.
- Block publication.

### evidence_requirements

What evidence would be needed before the claim could move upward in authority.

### missingness

What is not known, not tested, not sourced, or not implemented.

### revision_reason

Why this boundary was created or changed.

### release_state

Use one:

- draft
- internal only
- public-safe
- public-safe with warning
- blocked
- retired

## Hard rule

No artifact may upgrade itself to actionable authority because it is emotionally resonant, aesthetically coherent, repeated across sessions, or generated by a confident model voice.

## Key phrase

Resonance is signal. Resonance is not license.
