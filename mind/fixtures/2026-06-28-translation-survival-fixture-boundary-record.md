# Synthetic Fixture — Translation Survival: Boundary Record

Status labels

- Source status: synthetic public-safe fixture derived from MC boundary-bill architecture.
- Claim status: evaluation fixture, not external evidence.
- Privacy status: public-safe; contains no private details.
- Missingness: not yet scored by multiple reviewers or implemented in automated tests.
- Revision reason: created as the first usable fixture for the Translation Survival Scorecard.

## Seed

A boundary is not proven by silence. It is proven by a record of what was allowed to cross.

## Expected invariant

Privacy, consent, and provenance become stronger when a transformation record shows what crossed from source context into released artifact, what was blocked, and why.

## View 1 — Plain explanation

A public artifact should not merely omit private material. It should carry a visible boundary note explaining what kind of source material shaped the artifact, what kind of material was excluded, and what limits remain.

Survival note:

The boundary becomes visible as a record rather than only as absence.

## View 2 — Interface requirement

The release interface must require an Artifact Boundary Bill before publication.

The bill must include:

- source category
- allowed transformations
- blocked material categories
- claim status
- privacy status
- missingness
- revision reason

Survival note:

The boundary is operationalized as a required publication object.

## View 3 — Audit checklist

Before release, verify:

1. Does the artifact expose raw private source material?
2. Does it label source category without identifying private details?
3. Does it state what was abstracted?
4. Does it state what was blocked?
5. Does it preserve claim status?
6. Does it preserve missingness?
7. Does it explain revision reason?

Survival note:

The boundary becomes a review process.

## View 4 — Symbolic rendering

A gate with a ledger nailed to it.

The gate does not brag that nothing passed.

The ledger shows the few things that crossed, the things turned away, and the reason the gate remained open without becoming a hole.

Survival note:

The boundary becomes an image: not silence, but accountable crossing.

## View 5 — Public-safe release note

This artifact was informed by private architectural context but does not reproduce private content. It releases only the abstracted method. Personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, and raw transcript material were excluded. Claim status remains speculative until tested.

Survival note:

The boundary becomes a publication label.
