# Care / Social-Support Lane — Translation Survival for Support Communication

Status labels

- Source status: derived from public-safe MC architecture and public research direction; uses no private care details.
- Claim status: support-method hypothesis, not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or validated intervention.
- Privacy status: public-safe; no personal, household, health, animal-care, location, relationship, financial, credential, or transcript details.
- Missingness: no clinician review, no user study, no outcome evidence, no safety protocol beyond conceptual boundary labels.
- Revision reason: created to connect the dangerous-question research to evidence-based care/social-support communication without overstating authority.

## Core question

Can support-seeking become more effective when an observation survives translation from lived signal into professional-facing question without losing uncertainty?

## Why this matters

Many support failures occur during translation:

- lived experience becomes vague
- metaphor becomes dismissed
- uncertainty becomes overclaim
- concern becomes diagnosis
- pattern becomes anecdote
- professional question becomes too broad

MC's public-safe role should not be to diagnose or prescribe.

Its stronger role is to preserve structure during translation.

## Safe use case

Synthetic workflow:

1. A person records a non-identifying observation.
2. MC translates it into a structured observation packet.
3. MC labels uncertainty and missingness.
4. MC generates questions to ask a qualified professional or support provider.
5. MC preserves what changed during translation.

## Allowed outputs

- observation organization
- question generation
- uncertainty labels
- missingness labels
- appointment-preparation structure
- social-support communication structure
- source-boundary note

## Forbidden outputs

- diagnosis
- treatment recommendation
- medication advice
- prognosis claim
- emergency triage replacement
- claim that symbolic interpretation identifies a medical cause
- exposure of private specifics in public examples

## Translation survival dimensions for care support

A support translation succeeds when these survive:

1. The original concern remains recognizable.
2. The uncertainty remains visible.
3. The question is answerable by an appropriate professional/support person.
4. No private detail is exposed beyond the intended audience.
5. No metaphor is converted into fact.
6. No care authority is falsely claimed.

## Evidence direction

Research on semantic interoperability and synthetic medical records supports the broad idea that structured translation and privacy-safe simulation matter in health-information contexts.

For MC, this points toward synthetic fixtures and communication support, not clinical authority.
