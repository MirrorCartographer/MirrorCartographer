# Support Lane: Risk Ladder Care-Support Boundary

## Core finding

For medical or social-care adjacent work, the Risk Ladder should help MC distinguish communication support from clinical authority.

The useful lane is not diagnosis, treatment, or replacement care. The useful lane is structured communication, observation organization, question preparation, source-bound uncertainty, and review-ready summaries.

## Care-support ladder

| Level | Allowed support form | Boundary |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Metaphor for experience | Not evidence |
| 2 | Communication hypothesis | Not diagnosis |
| 3 | Synthetic fixture | Not real case proof |
| 4 | Professional-facing packet template | Requires user review before use |
| 5 | Review-ready support workflow | Requires domain review |
| 6 | Evidence-informed support pattern | Must cite evidence and limits |
| 7 | Diagnosis, treatment, medication, safety-critical authority | Block unless qualified professional owns it |

## Evidence-based direction

Current public reporting and research point toward caution: AI can improve access and structure, but mental-health and care-related AI outputs can also mislead, create overreliance, or provide dangerous advice when not properly reviewed.

The strongest public-safe MC support opportunity is therefore:

> transform messy observations into clearer questions, summaries, and uncertainty labels for qualified human review.

## Related intervention patterns to study

- Structured communication tools that help a person prepare for a professional encounter.
- Decision aids that improve question-asking and shared understanding.
- Video or interaction-guidance models that use reviewable evidence rather than hidden interpretation.
- Digital mental-health tools with safeguards, escalation boundaries, and explicit non-replacement framing.
- AI literacy interventions that increase critical evaluation rather than passive trust.

## Product implication

MC should create care-support artifacts only when they include:

1. scope boundary,
2. uncertainty label,
3. professional-review prompt,
4. source status,
5. claim status,
6. privacy status,
7. missingness,
8. revision reason,
9. and no unsupported treatment instruction.

## Key phrase

**Support may organize the signal. Authority must stay with qualified review.**

## Source status

Fresh public research/reporting and public-safe architectural synthesis.

## Claim status

Care-support boundary and research direction.

## Privacy status

Public-safe; no personal, household, health, animal-care, location, relationship, credential, financial, or raw transcript content included.

## Missingness

Needs systematic review of structured communication interventions and a public-safe support-template fixture set.

## Revision reason

Persistent support lane: adapt the Risk Ladder to evidence-based medical and social-care adjacent support without overclaiming clinical authority.
