# Public-Safe Risk Ladder

## Core discovery

Mirror Cartographer needs a **Risk Ladder**: a public-safe method for taking larger intellectual and product risks without confusing risk-taking with exposure, authority inflation, or unreviewed intervention.

The prior GitHub mind created a Public-Safe Speculation Lab. That lab established that MC should distinguish risk of thought from risk of exposure. The missing step is a graded ladder that tells the system what kind of risk is being taken, what evidence is required to climb, and what must remain blocked.

## Why this is needed

A single label such as "speculative" is too flat. Some ideas are harmless metaphors. Some are product hypotheses. Some are market claims. Some are care-support hypotheses. Some could drift into unsafe medical, legal, or financial authority if released incorrectly.

The Risk Ladder prevents two failure modes:

1. **Timidity**: the system only writes governance documents and never discovers anything surprising.
2. **Overreach**: the system treats a vivid pattern as evidence, advice, validation, or clinical authority.

## Ladder levels

| Level | Name | Allowed public output | Required control |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0 | Private spark | None | Hold private or abstract first |
| 1 | Symbolic conjecture | Metaphor, model, question | Mark as symbolic / analogical |
| 2 | Structural hypothesis | Architecture, invariant, method | Define falsification condition |
| 3 | Fixture-ready claim | Synthetic case, expected result | Public-safe fixture and oracle |
| 4 | Prototype-ready requirement | Product requirement, interface plan | Measurable acceptance criteria |
| 5 | Review-ready application | Market, care-support, education, governance use case | Domain review and boundary bill |
| 6 | Evidence-bearing claim | Published result or operational claim | Source-bound evidence and reproducibility record |
| 7 | Restricted authority claim | Medical, legal, financial, credentialed, safety-critical claim | Qualified human authority required; otherwise blocked |

## Operating rule

MC may climb the ladder only by adding evidence, testability, reviewer status, and boundary records. It may not climb by sounding more confident.

## Relationship to existing MC architecture

The Speculation Lab says risky ideas must name how they could be wrong. The Risk Ladder adds movement control: every risky idea gets a level, evidence threshold, and allowed release form.

The Artifact Bill of Boundaries records what crossed from private context into public artifact. The Risk Ladder records how far a claim is allowed to travel after crossing.

## Product implication

MC should expose the ladder level inside every research, museum, support, income, and product artifact. A public reader should be able to tell whether an artifact is a metaphor, a hypothesis, a requirement, an evidence-backed result, or a blocked authority claim.

## Evaluation implication

A claim fails the ladder if it:

- moves up a level without new evidence,
- removes uncertainty to sound more powerful,
- turns analogy into evidence,
- turns support into diagnosis or treatment,
- claims market validation without market test results,
- or hides reviewer status.

## Key phrase

**Risk is not the absence of gates. Risk is the climb that shows which rung it is standing on.**
