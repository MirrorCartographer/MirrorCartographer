## Core finding
Mirror Cartographer should not publish more abstract vocabulary until it can show a full loop:

private meaning-state → minimum continuity packet → mirror compiler → permissioned views → ViewDiff ledger → consent/risk gate → review outcome → revised packet

## Operating loop v0
1. Intake a fictional or consent-cleared record.
2. Split content into source units.
3. Assign claim status to every unit.
4. Assign privacy status to every unit.
5. Compile the minimum complete packet.
6. Generate permissioned views for distinct audiences.
7. Produce a ViewDiff ledger showing copied, generalized, removed, added, and blocked material.
8. Run consent/risk gate.
9. Score the output against evaluation criteria.
10. Feed the outcome back into the packet as a revision.

## Evaluation criteria
A loop pass is successful only if:
- every non-obvious claim has a source label;
- every audience view is narrower than the private/internal record;
- every transformation is visible;
- missingness is explicit;
- uncertainty remains visible in the final presentation;
- care-adjacent outputs become observation/question packets, not clinical conclusions;
- public artifacts contain no private raw details.

## Persistent practical lane: income
The realistic paid wedge is a narrow, non-clinical audit/demo service:

**Public-Safe AI Transformation Audit**
- Input: a team’s AI-assisted note, policy, product artifact, or research summary.
- Output: source/claim/privacy/missingness/revision labels + ViewDiff-style transformation notes.
- Buyer fit: teams that use AI but need clearer provenance, disclosure, or review boundaries.
- Boundary: not therapy, diagnosis, legal advice, or regulated compliance certification.

## Persistent practical lane: care/social support
The evidence-aligned care use is not treatment. It is safer communication:

**Observation-to-Question Support Loop**
- Convert messy lived observations into reviewable questions.
- Preserve uncertainty and missingness.
- Keep professional review explicit.
- Avoid turning metaphors or generated interpretations into medical facts.
