## Strongest attractor

Continuity.

The strongest current pattern in the mind is not merely private/public separation. It is continuity-preserving transformation: one underlying record can become multiple audience-specific views while retaining a visible trail of what changed, why it changed, what was removed, and what claim strength is allowed.

## Core discovery

Mirror Cartographer needs a Living ViewDiff layer.

A ViewDiff is the difference between two permissioned views of the same underlying meaning-state record. It is not a generic redaction log. It records transformation.

Example abstract flow:

Private expressive record
→ claim-boundary compiler
→ professional handoff view
→ public-safe method view
→ research-safe aggregate view

The Living ViewDiff answers:

1. What survived the translation?
2. What was removed for privacy or scope?
3. What became more precise?
4. What became less emotionally rich?
5. What claim category changed?
6. What evidence was added, missing, or downgraded?
7. Who is allowed to see which view?
8. What next test would reduce ambiguity?

## Why this matters

A simple summary hides transformation. A Living ViewDiff makes transformation auditable.

That matters for AI literacy, care communication, research translation, and co-created knowledge because the user, professional, reviewer, or public audience can see that the public artifact is not pretending to be the raw private record.

## Proposed protocol

Each continuity object should contain:

- record_id: stable non-identifying identifier
- source_boundary: private, public, professional, research-safe, synthetic, external
- source_status: direct, derived, synthesized, fictional, missing, unverifiable
- claim_status: observation, interpretation, hypothesis, requirement, evaluation criterion, research question, implementation plan
- privacy_status: private-only, permissioned, public-safe, aggregate-safe, blocked
- transformation_reason: why this view exists
- removed_fields: categories only, not sensitive content
- preserved_invariants: underlying meanings that survived
- added_constraints: evidence, safety, consent, audience, scope
- lost_information: what the new view no longer captures
- viewdiff_score: how faithfully the transformed view preserves the source structure
- next_test: smallest test that would improve confidence

## Evaluation criteria

A good Living ViewDiff should be:

1. Traceable: the transformed output can be linked to its source class without exposing private details.
2. Honest: it labels what changed instead of pretending transformation is neutral.
3. Useful: each view serves a real audience need.
4. Minimal: it avoids carrying sensitive information when category-level structure is enough.
5. Reversible only by permission: the system remembers the transformation path, but public artifacts cannot reconstruct private facts.
6. Evidence-aware: claims cannot exceed their source status.
7. Beautiful enough to read: the interface should compress complexity without concealing boundaries.

## Research alignment

Fresh public signals support the direction:

- AI literacy work increasingly emphasizes provenance, validation, uncertainty, and critical evaluation rather than tool use alone.
- Human-AI co-creation research warns against epistemic alienation: humans may endorse outputs they no longer understand.
- Health-data infrastructure work emphasizes interoperable, permissioned, and secondary-use boundaries.
- Patient-generated health data and personal health record systems show a real need for consumer-to-professional translation, but also raise privacy and overreliance risks.
