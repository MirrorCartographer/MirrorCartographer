# Compression Loss Ledger

## Public-safe finding

Public-safe transformation is not only a redaction problem. It is also a compression problem.

Mirror Cartographer often begins from dense, private, multimodal, emotionally salient, temporally layered context. A public artifact cannot carry that raw source. But when the source is abstracted into a method, requirement, research question, or evaluation criterion, the artifact should record what kind of signal was intentionally removed, preserved, generalized, or downgraded.

## Strongest attractor

**Compression Loss**

## Core phrase

**A safe summary should not pretend nothing was lost.**

## Why this matters

A public-safe artifact can become misleading in two opposite ways:

1. It can expose too much source material.
2. It can hide so much source material that the remaining claim appears cleaner, more universal, more authoritative, or more complete than it really is.

The Compression Loss Ledger protects against the second failure.

## Revision reason

Prior MC mind runs built layers for source boundary, claim transport, influence, context admission, quarantine, temporal validity, lineage, contestability, and context distillation. Those layers describe whether context may influence an artifact and how claims survive boundary crossings. This revision adds a missing accounting layer: what was lost when private context became public-safe structure.

## Method requirement

Every public-safe MC artifact generated from private or mixed context should include a compression-loss receipt with:

1. Input category, not raw input.
2. Preserved structure.
3. Removed structure.
4. Generalized structure.
5. Downgraded claim.
6. Missing evidence.
7. Possible distortion introduced by compression.
8. Human review need.

## Product implication

MC should not only label source status and claim status. It should also label **compression status**:

- `lossless_public`: source was already public-safe and minimal transformation was required.
- `abstracted_private`: private context shaped the method but no source details moved forward.
- `high_loss`: public artifact keeps only a small structural trace of the source context.
- `claim_downgraded`: a stronger internal hypothesis was reduced to a weaker public-safe claim.
- `structure_preserved`: the source details were removed but the relational pattern survived.
- `insufficient_context`: the public artifact may be too thin to support the intended claim.

## Evaluation question

Can an outside reviewer understand what kind of context shaped an MC artifact, what was intentionally omitted, and how much confidence remains after compression, without seeing the protected source?
