# Representational Fidelity

Status labels

- Source status: derived from public-safe synthesis of available Mirror Cartographer architecture materials, file-library specifications, recent GitHub mind direction, and current public research on human-centered AI, provenance, disclosure, and representational auditing.
- Claim status: architecture hypothesis and product-method proposal; not evidence of deployed runtime behavior.
- Privacy status: public-safe; excludes personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, and raw transcript details.
- Missingness: does not yet include a working MC runtime test, participant study, repository-wide file inventory, or measured artifact-loss audit.
- Revision reason: added after prior runs established public-safe compilation, influence graphs, release gates, symbolic translucence, and agency boundaries; this note adds a preservation test for what gets lost during abstraction.

## Core finding

Mirror Cartographer needs a RepresentationalFidelity layer.

Privacy-safe abstraction is necessary, but it can create a new failure mode: the public artifact may become safe by becoming too smooth.

If the compiler removes private details, it must still preserve the shape of the underlying architecture:

- dissent
- contradiction
- minority signals
- uncertainty
- emotional register
- symbolic force
- edge cases
- missingness
- non-consensus claims
- unresolved questions

The goal is not to preserve private content.

The goal is to preserve the public-safe topology of meaning.

## Problem

A public-safe compiler can pass a privacy gate and still fail the system.

Failure examples:

- Private source details are removed, but the artifact also removes the contradiction that made the question useful.
- A symbolic claim is rewritten as a bland product statement, erasing the interpretive lens.
- A minority or dissenting signal becomes grouped into a generic category.
- Uncertainty is softened into marketable confidence.
- Missingness is hidden because the artifact needs to sound complete.

This is not leakage.

It is representational loss.

## Research alignment

Current public research reinforces the need for this layer.

- Human-centered AI work describes a capability-comprehension gap and argues users need enough retained understanding to verify, intervene, and preserve autonomy.
- Generative AI disclosure research shows that simple labels are insufficient; useful disclosure needs method, role, rationale, and boundary clarity.
- Participatory provenance work argues that AI summaries can exclude dissenting or semantically isolated inputs even when summaries appear coherent.
- Human-AI collaboration disclosure work shows that visual disclosure format changes perceived human/AI contribution, meaning disclosure itself can distort interpretation.

MC should treat abstraction as a lossy transform requiring audit, not a neutral cleanup step.

## MC interpretation

MC already asks for source status, claim status, privacy status, missingness, and revision reason.

RepresentationalFidelity adds another question:

Did the public-safe artifact preserve the structure of what mattered?

This includes preserving:

- claim mode: fact, inference, symbolic interpretation, speculation, design requirement
- emotional-symbolic register: not sentiment, but tone-shape and attention force
- contradiction shape: what remains unresolved
- minority signal: what should not be averaged away
- boundary bill: what private materials influenced the artifact without being exposed
- missingness: what is absent and why that matters

## Non-goals

RepresentationalFidelity must not:

- publish private source material
- imply access to all chats or files
- claim a private detail is publicly verified
- preserve identifying context merely because it is structurally interesting
- treat emotional intensity as proof
- turn a symbolic map into a diagnosis, legal claim, financial claim, or factual authority

## Public-safe method

For every compiled artifact, create a transform audit:

1. Identify source classes, not raw sources.
2. Extract public-safe invariants.
3. Mark removed private classes.
4. Preserve contradiction as abstract structure.
5. Preserve minority or dissenting signals as labeled alternatives.
6. Label claim mode.
7. Label missingness.
8. Compare source topology to artifact topology.
9. Record what was compressed, generalized, or intentionally omitted.
10. Decide whether the artifact should be released, revised, or held.

## Research question

Can MC preserve interpretive structure across privacy-safe abstraction without leaking private data or flattening the original signal into generic product language?

## Key phrase

A public-safe artifact should not expose the source, but it should preserve the shape of what the source made visible.
