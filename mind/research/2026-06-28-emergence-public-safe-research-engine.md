## Core finding

Mirror Cartographer is emerging as a public-safe research engine.

private signal -> abstracted structure -> source-boundary note -> claim rung -> public artifact -> future test.

## Why this matters

AI-mediated research has a transparency problem. Fresh work on early-stage AI research use reports that confident AI tone can hide uncertainty, opaque retrieval can weaken provenance, and researchers often need compensatory strategies to restore accountability and trust.

MC's answer should not be `trust the generated interpretation.`

MC's answer should be:

`show the transformation record.`

## Research alignment

Current research directions support this lane:

- Reliability-aware evaluation of human-AI co-creation benefits from schema-constrained judging, trajectory-level analysis, and revision-churn measures.
- Human-AI collaborative uncertainty work emphasizes complementarity and avoiding AI degradation of correct human judgments.
- Provenance-first conversational systems show the value of source traceability, structured synthesis, and workflow alignment in high-stakes knowledge work.
- AI content provenance and authenticity work is moving toward metadata, watermarking, content credentials, and public verification pathways.
