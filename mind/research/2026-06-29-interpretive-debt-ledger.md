## Core finding

Mirror Cartographer needs an Interpretive Debt Ledger.

Interpretive debt is the accumulated risk created when a symbolic, reflective, or aesthetic interpretation is useful enough to shape an artifact but not yet verified enough to stabilize into a product claim, research claim, user-facing label, or public narrative.

## Why this matters

MC deliberately works with symbolic, emotional, embodied, nonlinear, and aesthetic material. That gives it unusual representational power, but it also creates a specific failure mode: a compelling interpretation can become infrastructure before its source status, claim status, missingness, and revision path are visible.

The ledger prevents useful interpretation from hardening into hidden authority.

## External research fit

Recent public research supports this direction:

1. `Audit Trails for Accountability in Large Language Models` argues for chronological, durable, context-rich audit trails that link technical provenance with governance records so changes and authorizations can be reconstructed.
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20727
   - Relevance to MC: Interpretive changes need reviewable lineage, not just polished output.

2. `Responsible Agentic AI Requires Explicit Provenance` argues that agentic systems require explicit, computable provenance across lifecycle layers to make responsibility traceable and interventionable.
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17169
   - Relevance to MC: Reflection systems need visible responsibility boundaries whenever outputs shape downstream artifacts.

3. `Human-Centered Design: The Disclosure of Generative Artificial Intelligence for Emerging Professionals` argues that simple AI-use disclosures are insufficient without methodology, rationale, human feedback, and justified boundaries.
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24136
   - Relevance to MC: MC should disclose not only that AI helped, but what role AI played in interpretation, synthesis, compression, and release.

4. `From Trust to Appropriate Reliance: Measurement Constructs in Human-AI Decision-Making` distinguishes trust from appropriate reliance and argues that reliance must be measured in context.
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23896
   - Relevance to MC: A resonant interpretation should not invite reliance unless its claim mode and verification status support that reliance.

## MC architecture fit

Available MC materials already define Mirror Cartographer as a recursive symbolic cognition interface with a core ENTRY -> FIELD -> RECURSION -> RETURN flow. The Atlas materials also emphasize epistemic honesty, non-coercion, continuity with accountability, explicit epistemic labels, and the separation of confirmed material, inference, and bounded speculation.

The Interpretive Debt Ledger operationalizes that principle at the level of individual interpretive moves.

## Definition

Interpretive debt exists when any of the following are true:

- An interpretation is emotionally or aesthetically strong but source-thin.
- A symbolic pattern appears repeatedly but has not been checked against counterexamples.
- A private-context insight has been abstracted into public-safe language but its structural fidelity has not been tested.
- A product requirement is derived from repeated conversation patterns but has not been validated with non-private users or external evidence.
- An output is useful, beautiful, or resonant enough that future work might treat it as settled.

## Ledger fields

Each interpretive debt record should track:

- debt_id
- artifact_path
- interpretive move
- source boundary class
- claim mode: fact, inference, symbolic interpretation, speculation, product requirement, research question, evaluation criterion
- confidence level
- reliance risk
- privacy risk
- missing evidence
- counterexample needed
- owner or review lane
- allowed use before repayment
- disallowed use before repayment
- repayment action
- revision reason
- status: open, reduced, repaid, retired, escalated

## Use rules

- Interpretation may guide exploration before repayment.
- Interpretation may not become an authority claim before repayment.
- Interpretation may appear in public artifacts only if its claim mode and missingness are visible.
- Symbolic or aesthetic strength increases the need for review; it does not reduce it.
- Public-safe compression must preserve the debt marker when source evidence is hidden.

## Product implication

The MC interface should include a visible debt layer for generated artifacts:

- What did this output assume?
- What did it infer?
- What did it symbolize?
- What evidence is missing?
- What would change this?
- What is this not allowed to decide?

## Evaluation implication

An MC artifact should fail release readiness if it contains unpaid interpretive debt that could reasonably be mistaken for diagnosis, instruction, proof, identity labeling, financial guidance, legal guidance, or authoritative fact.

## Key phrase

Interpretation can open the map. Unpaid interpretation cannot own the road.
