# Dual-Lane Continuity Kernel

## Core finding

Mirror Cartographer needs a **Dual-Lane Continuity Kernel** that explains why its symbolic-reflection work and governance/provenance work belong to the same system without collapsing them into the same claim.

Operating line:

> MC has two public lanes, but one continuity problem: meaning changes over time unless its source, state, authority, and revision path remain inspectable.

## Claim status

- **Claim type:** product architecture / public positioning requirement.
- **Evidence level:** architecture-derived synthesis, not empirical validation.
- **Allowed public claim:** MC can be described as a continuity system with at least two distinct public lanes:
  - **Human reflection lane:** symbolic, emotional, somatic, narrative, and meaning-making state mapping.
  - **Governance/provenance lane:** reasoning trajectory, contradiction, source custody, evaluator interpretation, and revision tracking.
- **Not allowed public claim:** symbolic reflection outputs and governance/provenance outputs have the same authority, proof standard, or validation pathway.

## Revision reason

Prior notes established export gating, authority preservation, claim taxonomy, missingness, provenance packets, consent gradients, and mode handoff. This note adds a higher-level definition resolver: MC should present its public identity as a shared continuity kernel with separate authority lanes, rather than as one blurred product category.

## Method

Use a two-step definition test before publishing any MC explanation:

1. **Kernel test:** Does this artifact describe the shared continuity problem?
   - What state is being preserved?
   - What changes over time?
   - What source boundary controls interpretation?
   - What revision path changes the map?

2. **Lane test:** Which authority lane is speaking?
   - Reflection lane: subjective/symbolic/meaning-making support.
   - Product lane: interface requirement or workflow design.
   - Research lane: hypothesis, open question, or evaluation target.
   - Governance lane: provenance, oversight, reasoning trace, or auditability.
   - Public-value lane: demo, offer, collaboration, or implementation plan.

If an artifact passes the kernel test but fails the lane test, it should be rewritten before publication.

## Product requirement

Every durable MC artifact should expose a visible `continuity_lane` field.

Suggested initial values:

- `reflection`
- `product_requirement`
- `research_question`
- `governance_provenance`
- `evaluation_criterion`
- `public_value`
- `implementation_plan`
- `mixed_requires_split`

## Evaluation criteria

A public MC explanation passes if an outside reader can tell:

1. what continuity problem is being addressed;
2. which authority lane the artifact belongs to;
3. what the artifact is allowed to claim;
4. what it is not allowed to prove;
5. what would revise, split, or retire the artifact.

A public MC explanation fails if it makes symbolic resonance, product ambition, governance architecture, and empirical proof sound interchangeable.
