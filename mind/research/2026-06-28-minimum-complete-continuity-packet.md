## Core compression
A Minimum Complete Continuity Packet should contain:

1. **Record identity**: fictional or anonymized record ID only.
2. **Input class**: symbol, sensation, narrative, observation, question, or correction.
3. **Source status**: user-stated, AI-inferred, externally sourced, clinician/professional reviewed, unknown.
4. **Claim status**: expression, observation, hypothesis, question, decision support, verified fact, contraindicated claim.
5. **Privacy status**: private-only, shareable summary, professional handoff, public-safe method, aggregate-only.
6. **View target**: self, coach, clinician, veterinarian, social-care worker, researcher, product team, public viewer.
7. **Transformation note**: what was removed, compressed, translated, generalized, or preserved.
8. **Missingness note**: what is unknown, unverified, old, incomplete, or outside system authority.
9. **Revision reason**: safety boundary, evidence update, user correction, professional review, new source, clearer language.
10. **Next test**: the smallest action that could falsify, improve, or clarify the packet.

## Why this matters
Previous MC artifacts define many components: consent layers, ViewDiff, claim boundaries, care adapters, source labels, and public-safe indexing. The compression problem is that the system can become too large to inspect. A minimum packet gives every future module a shared atomic form.

## Care/social-support lane
Potential support use: convert a personal narrative into an observation/question packet for a professional conversation. Boundary: communication support only. No diagnosis, triage, treatment recommendation, urgency assessment, or replacement for licensed care.

## Evaluation criteria
- Can a reader identify the source of each claim?
- Can a reader see what changed between views?
- Can private meaning be preserved without exposure?
- Can a professional distinguish observation from interpretation?
- Can missing information be seen without shame or overreach?
- Can the record be revised without erasing prior state?
