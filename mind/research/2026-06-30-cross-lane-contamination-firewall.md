## Core finding

A connected graph needs a proof firewall.

Mirror Cartographer can allow ideas to merge at the meaning, routing, dashboard, action-planning, and symbolic-architecture layers while requiring proof to remain lane-specific. A cross-lane contamination firewall prevents one lane's evidence from being silently reused as authority for another lane.

## Firewall rule

Everything can connect in the operating graph. Nothing can prove outside its proof lane without an explicit bridge record.

## Bridge record requirement

Any cross-lane use must declare:

1. source lane
2. destination lane
3. transferred object
4. transfer type: metaphor, requirement, signal, evidence, test, implementation, or policy
5. authority allowed after transfer
6. authority forbidden after transfer
7. required proof standard in destination lane
8. privacy class
9. expiry or review trigger

## Product implication

MC should treat each generated output as a routed object with two separate maps:

- connection map: what this resembles, relates to, or helps organize
- proof map: what this is allowed to support

The interface should never show only the connection map. It should show the proof map whenever an output could influence action, belief, publication, or external claims.
