## Core finding

A private signal is not publishable because it is meaningful. It becomes publishable only after it is distilled into a rule that still works when the private source is removed.

## Distillation rule

Before any private-derived idea becomes a public artifact, it must pass five gates:

1. Source removal: the claim still makes sense without naming or exposing the private source.
2. Domain neutralization: the artifact does not reveal whether the source came from health, household, animal care, money, location, relationship, identity, credentials, or raw transcript material.
3. Method survival: the public output is a method, schema, requirement, evaluation criterion, research question, or implementation plan.
4. Claim downgrade: resonance, recurrence, memory, or pattern is not allowed to become evidence, diagnosis, causality, or authority.
5. Revision trace: any redaction, abstraction, downgrade, or omission has a labeled reason.
