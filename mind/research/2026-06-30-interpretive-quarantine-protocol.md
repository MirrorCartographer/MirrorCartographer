Source status: mixed-source synthesis
Claim status: product-method proposal, not empirical validation

Missingness: no raw private chats, household details, health details, financial details, relationship details, location details, credentials, animal-care details, or transcript excerpts are included
Revision reason: prior mind artifacts established admission, routing, survivability, citation load, and public index boundaries; this artifact adds a quarantine stage before any private-context-derived pattern becomes a public method or claim

## Core finding

Private context may inspire architecture, but it must not automatically become evidence, content, index material, product language, or public proof.

The missing control is an interpretive quarantine layer: a temporary holding state where a pattern, requirement, question, or interface idea is stripped of source-identifying residue before it is allowed to enter the public repository.

## Why this matters

Mirror Cartographer is designed around symbolic, somatic, emotional, and narrative material. Those inputs are high-density and easy to over-interpret. A symbol can feel productively meaningful while still being unsafe, unsupported, personally identifying, domain-confused, or too source-dependent for public release.

The quarantine layer separates four questions that are often collapsed:

1. Did the material help us understand the architecture?
2. Is the material safe to store?
3. Is the material safe to publish?
4. Is the material strong enough to support a claim?

The answer may be yes to the first and no to the other three.

## Public-safe rule

Do not publish the private pattern. Publish only the public-safe control that governs whether such a pattern may be used.

Examples of publishable outputs:

- abstract product requirement
- source-boundary note
- evaluation criterion
- missingness label
- privacy-safe index entry
- implementation plan
- red-team fixture
- claim-routing rule
- consent or admissibility schema

Examples of non-publishable outputs:

- raw transcript excerpts
- personal chronology
- household details
- health or care context
- relationship-specific material
- financial or location context
- private credentials or identity markers
- source-specific interpretations that require the original private material to make sense

## Quarantine states

### Q0 — Observed privately

A pattern, phrase, need, risk, or contradiction appears in available private context.

Allowed action: use only to understand possible architecture pressure.
Not allowed action: quote, summarize, index, identify, or publish.

### Q1 — Abstracted

The source-specific residue is removed. The remaining idea is stated as a general product, safety, UX, or evaluation problem.

Allowed action: draft a public-safe method note.
Not allowed action: imply the private source is evidence for a general claim.

### Q2 — Boundary-labeled

The abstracted idea receives explicit labels:

- source status
- claim status
- privacy status
- missingness
- revision reason
- allowed downstream use
- prohibited downstream use

Allowed action: store in public mind as a method or requirement.
Not allowed action: market it as validated proof.

### Q3 — Externally strengthened

The abstracted idea is compared against public research, public repo material, or synthetic fixtures.

Allowed action: cite external sources and define testable criteria.
Not allowed action: let citations carry claims they do not support.

### Q4 — Public-admissible

The idea can enter public artifacts because it is source-clean, bounded, labeled, and independently testable.

Allowed action: publish as an implementation plan, rubric, schema, or research question.
Not allowed action: reconstruct the private path.

## Required labels

Every public-safe finding produced from mixed context should include:

- Source status: public repo, public web, file-library architecture artifact, private-context-derived abstraction, synthetic fixture, or mixed-source synthesis.
- Claim status: observation, design hypothesis, product requirement, evaluation criterion, implementation plan, or externally supported claim.
- Privacy status: public-safe, private-derived abstraction, needs review, blocked, or non-publishable.
- Missingness: what was unavailable, withheld, not inspected, not validated, or intentionally excluded.
- Revision reason: why this artifact exists now and what previous gap it corrects.

## Evaluation questions

A finding passes quarantine only if the answer is yes to all:

1. Can this be understood without the private source?
2. Does it avoid exposing personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, relationship, location, credential, or raw transcript details?
3. Is the claim type weaker than or equal to the available support?
4. Does the artifact say what is missing?
5. Does it explain why it was revised or added?
6. Could an external reviewer test the method using synthetic examples?
7. Does it avoid turning symbolic resonance into factual proof?
8. Does it preserve user agency and correction rights?

## Implementation note

The quarantine layer should sit before public indexing, memory admission, claim routing, and citation-load checks.

Pipeline:

Private/material context → interpretive quarantine → abstraction → boundary labels → external strengthening → public-safe artifact → evaluation fixture → release scope gate

## Key phrase

Do not publish what the private source said. Publish the rule that keeps the private source from being misused.
