# Public-Safe Speculation Lab

## Core discovery

Mirror Cartographer needs a **Public-Safe Speculation Lab**: a controlled research lane where the system can take intellectual risks without taking privacy, consent, source, or medical-authority risks.

The prior GitHub mind has been converging toward boundary control: source labels, claim labels, privacy labels, audience contracts, fixture runners, oracle records, drift ledgers, regression harnesses, and the Artifact Bill of Boundaries. This pass adds the missing counterpart: a place where the system is explicitly allowed to generate bold hypotheses, strange mappings, and cross-disciplinary invariants as long as every speculative move is labeled, bounded, testable, and reversible.

## Why this is needed

If MC only becomes a release-control system, it becomes safe but dull. If MC becomes only symbolic speculation, it becomes expressive but weak. The useful tension is:

- **Speculation layer**: generate risky conceptual hypotheses.
- **Boundary layer**: prevent those hypotheses from pretending to be facts.
- **Test layer**: convert interesting hypotheses into falsifiable questions, fixtures, demos, or review tasks.

## Public-safe thesis

MC should distinguish **risk of thought** from **risk of exposure**.

- Risk of thought: acceptable and necessary.
- Risk of wrongness: acceptable if labeled and testable.
- Risk of exposing private material: not acceptable for public artifacts.
- Risk of medical, legal, or financial overclaiming: not acceptable without qualified review.
- Risk of pretending a speculative pattern is evidence: not acceptable.

## Relationship to existing MC architecture

Existing public-safe MC materials define MC as a recursive symbolic cognition interface with ENTRY -> FIELD -> RECURSION -> RETURN, plus Canonical, Reflective, and Mythopoetic modes. The current mind also defines an Artifact Bill of Boundaries as the portable record of what was allowed to cross from private context into public artifact.

The Speculation Lab becomes a controlled extension of Mythopoetic and Reflective modes, but it must pass through Canonical review before any public release.

## Proposed operating loop

1. **Generate** a bold hypothesis, invariant, metaphor, or system relation.
2. **Classify** it as speculative, analogical, architectural, research question, product hypothesis, or care-support hypothesis.
3. **Detach** it from private examples before publication.
4. **Translate** it into a testable form.
5. **Attach** a falsification condition.
6. **Run** public-safe synthetic fixtures when possible.
7. **Record** what survived, what failed, what narrowed, and what remains unknown.
8. **Release** only the abstracted method, requirement, question, scorecard, or implementation plan.

## Example public-safe speculation formats

### Invariant hypothesis

Understanding may be measured by how many faithful transformations an idea survives across audiences, modalities, and evidence lanes.

### Product hypothesis

A user-facing MC interface should expose not just the reflection, but the transformation path that generated it.

### Research question

Can symbolic inputs improve user recall, self-explanation, or emotional orientation compared with plain text journaling when evaluated with non-clinical, consented, low-risk tasks?

### Evaluation question

Does an output preserve source boundaries after being transformed from private note to public artifact, clinician-facing packet, product requirement, and museum entry?

## Risk taxonomy

| Risk type | Allowed? | Control |
|---|---:|---|
| Conceptual wrongness | Yes | Label as speculation and define falsifier |
| Weird metaphor | Yes | Separate symbolic meaning from factual claim |
| Cross-disciplinary analogy | Yes | Mark analogy, not evidence |
| Product overreach | Limited | Convert to hypothesis and validation plan |
| Clinical overclaim | No | Keep as care-support boundary only |
| Private-context leakage | No | Abstract before publication |
| Authority inflation | No | Preserve claim status and reviewer status |
