# Artifact Bill of Boundaries

## Core discovery

Mirror Cartographer needs an **Artifact Bill of Boundaries**: a compact public-safe record that travels with every transformed artifact and declares what crossed the boundary, what did not cross, why the transformation was allowed, and what remains unverified.

This extends prior gate, router, fixture, oracle, and drift concepts into a single portable record.

## Public-safe principle

A public artifact should not merely say that private material was removed. It should state the boundary logic that made the artifact publishable.

## Research basis

- Inspectable AI governance research argues that AI-assisted work can be treated as structured, accountable process objects rather than vague tool use. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11261
- AIBOM research frames AI provenance as lifecycle metadata, lineage, disclosure, reproducibility, and assurance. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19755
- Effect-transparent governance research distinguishes structural governance of workflow effects from content-level filtering. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01030
- Privacy-sensitive agent research increasingly uses synthetic data and targeted sanitization to test boundaries without exposing private records. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03183
- Mental-health AI reporting in 2026 reinforces the need for cautious boundaries, review, evidence status, and safeguards when AI mediates support or advice. Sources: https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/feb/20/mind-inquiry-google-ai-overviews-mental-health-guardian-investigation and https://www.theguardian.com/society/2026/jun/19/ico-watchdog-inquiry-cameras-mental-health-patients-bedrooms

The Artifact Bill of Boundaries compresses these into one object that can accompany any public artifact.

## Proposed boundary bill sections

1. **Source boundary** — what kind of material informed the artifact.
2. **Private material exclusion** — what categories were intentionally not published.
3. **Claim boundary** — what the artifact is allowed to assert.
4. **Audience boundary** — who the artifact is for and who it is not for.
5. **Evidence lane** — symbolic, operational, product, research, care-support, or legal/policy-adjacent.
6. **Transformation record** — how private or messy input became public-safe output.
7. **Review status** — unchecked, self-reviewed, peer-reviewed, expert-reviewed, or blocked.
8. **Missingness** — what is unknown, unverified, stale, inaccessible, or intentionally withheld.
9. **Revision reason** — why this artifact exists or changed.
10. **Release state** — private, internal review, public-safe draft, or public release.
