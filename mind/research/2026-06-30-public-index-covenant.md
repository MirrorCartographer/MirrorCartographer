## Revision reason

Prior mind layers established quarantine, contestability, distillation, compression loss, deployment boundary, evidence admission, revision provenance, release scope, proof packets, boundary stacks, interpretation admissibility, instruction-source collision, claim routing, cross-lane contamination, and recall conditions. The remaining gap is the public-facing index itself: how to make a safe inventory of evidence, constraints, and requirements without leaking the sensitive origins of the work.

## Core finding

A public index is not a table of secrets. It is a covenant about what may be known, what may be claimed, and what must remain unexposed.

## Product implication

Mirror Cartographer should maintain a Public Index Covenant for every outward-facing release. The covenant records:

1. what source classes informed the artifact;
2. which source classes are publishable, abstract-only, or excluded;
3. which claim lanes are permitted;
4. which private details were intentionally withheld;
5. what missingness remains;
6. what revision reason explains the artifact's current form.

## Boundary rule

Do not publish a private source inventory. Publish a source-boundary inventory.

## Evaluation question

Can an external reviewer understand the artifact's evidence boundaries, intended use, exclusions, and uncertainty without receiving private context or being asked to trust hidden personal history?

## Research alignment

Current AI-memory research supports treating memory retrieval and long-term personalization as trust-boundary problems rather than neutral recall. Relevant public themes include task-conditioned memory admission, ground-truth preservation, memory poisoning, provenance, temporal validity, and the limits of similarity-only retrieval.
