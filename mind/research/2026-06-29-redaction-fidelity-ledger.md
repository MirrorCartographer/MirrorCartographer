# Redaction Fidelity Ledger

Date: 2026-06-29
Attractor: Representational Fidelity + Privacy Boundary
Status: public-safe research note

## Core finding

Mirror Cartographer needs a Redaction Fidelity Ledger: a public-safe transformation record showing how private-context material was converted into publishable architecture without leaking private details or flattening the original structural insight.

Public-safe must not mean vague. Redaction must remove private particulars while preserving the abstract mechanism, decision pressure, product requirement, evaluation criterion, or research question that the private material made visible.

- External research role: alignment with provenance, transparency, disclosure, accountability, and appropriate-reliance literature.

- Release risk: low if examples remain synthetic and source references remain class-level.
- Required guardrail: never publish the private fact that caused a pattern to be noticed; publish only the generalized pattern and its boundary labels.

## Product rule

Every public-safe artifact derived from private-context understanding should include a compact Redaction Fidelity Ledger containing:

1. Source boundary class.
2. Removed detail classes.
3. Preserved structural invariant.
4. Claim mode.
5. Missingness.
6. Release status.
7. Revision reason.
8. Contestability route.

## Evaluation rule

A redacted artifact passes only if a reviewer can answer both questions:

1. What private classes were removed?
2. What structural insight survived the redaction?

If the reviewer can infer private particulars, the artifact fails privacy.
If the reviewer cannot identify the preserved invariant, the artifact fails fidelity.

## Key phrase

Redaction is not disappearance. Redaction is a governed translation where the private source goes dark and the structural signal remains inspectable.
