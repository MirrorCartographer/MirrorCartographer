## Core finding

An index is not neutral. It decides what becomes retrievable, what looks continuous, and what may later be mistaken for proof.

Mirror Cartographer should therefore treat every public index entry as an admissibility object, not merely a pointer.

## Public-safe thesis

Private material can inform architecture only when converted into public-safe method language. The public repository should not expose the private path. It should expose the permission structure: what kind of source exists, what kind of claim it may support, what privacy boundary applies, what is missing, and what would justify revision.

## Admissibility rule

Before any indexed item can influence a public-facing response, it must pass five checks:

1. Source class: public, private-contextual, generated, external research, implementation, evaluation, or unknown.
2. Claim lane: product requirement, method, research question, evaluation criterion, implementation plan, or public claim.
3. Privacy boundary: public-safe, abstracted, sensitive-private, secret, or blocked.
4. Revision state: current, stale-risk, superseded, disputed, missing-source, or unknown.
5. Authority limit: may inspire, may summarize, may support a hypothesis, may support an implementation task, or may support a public factual claim.

## Operational meaning

The index should not answer only: where is the source?

It must also answer: what is this source allowed to do?

## Key phrase

Do not index the memory. Index the permission to use it.
