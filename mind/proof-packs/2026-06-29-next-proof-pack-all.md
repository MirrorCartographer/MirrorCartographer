Status: implementation scaffold + first-pass proof artifacts.  
Scope: converts the open “next proof needed” items from the evidence-map series into concrete reviewable objects.  
Certainty: low-to-moderate. These artifacts do not prove MC works. They prove the GitHub mind now has a testable structure for proving or falsifying the claims.

## What this pack does

This pack creates a single proof layer above the evidence maps. It does not upgrade claims automatically. It forces each claim to show:

- what decision or reasoning behavior it is supposed to improve;
- what evidence would count as support;
- what evidence would count against it;
- whether the proof is direct, indirect, prototype-only, or untested;
- what artifact in the repo carries the next test.

## Claim-status rule for this pack

No claim is upgraded to “validated.”

Allowed status changes from this pack:

- implicit assumption → testable claim;
- unstructured next step → implemented proof object;
- unsupported claim → still unsupported but now falsifiable;
- design principle → still design principle unless direct MC test exists.

## First-pass results

The proof pack found that the strongest current MC evidence is methodological, not outcome-based.

Supported as design/process principles:

- provenance is needed for auditability;
- claim graphs are needed to prevent isolated evidence maps;
- negative results should be preserved;
- construct validity must be checked;
- human agency and clinical/memory/relational boundaries are necessary safety constraints.

Still unproven as MC effects:

- MC improves decision quality;
- symbolic mapping improves meaning-making without memory distortion;
- role pluralism improves reasoning rather than theater;
- Discovery/Fossil objects improve architectural learning;
- Influence Scope improves calibrated trust;
- MC artifacts convert into real employment opportunity.
