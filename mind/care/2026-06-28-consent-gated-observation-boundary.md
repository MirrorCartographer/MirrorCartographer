# Consent-Gated Observation Boundary

## Source status
- Public-safe care/social-support synthesis from MC architecture materials and public research on patient-generated data, personal health records, ambient documentation, and privacy risk.
- Private-context material used only to understand architecture; no private details included.

## Claim status
- Strong boundary claim: MC care-adjacent outputs must remain observation/question support unless reviewed by an appropriate professional.
- Moderate product claim: consent-gated observation packets may improve communication quality.
- Missing evidence: no controlled outcome study yet.

## Privacy status
- Public-safe.
- No personal, household, animal-care, health, financial, location, relationship, credential, or raw transcript details.

## Missingness
- No professional review protocol.
- No emergency escalation protocol.
- No field test against ordinary notes.
- No validated form for dependent beings, shared households, or multi-observer contexts.

## Revision reason
The Biocultural View Loop established how private meaning could move toward reviewed action. This note tightens the care boundary: before observation leaves private space, consent and risk must be evaluated.

## Boundary principle
A care-adjacent MC packet must be useful without becoming authoritative.

It may help structure:

- what was observed;
- when it happened;
- what changed;
- what remains unknown;
- what question should be brought to a professional;
- what source or observer confidence exists.

It must not decide:

- diagnosis;
- treatment;
- urgency;
- medication changes;
- prognosis;
- whether professional care is unnecessary.

## Consent-gated care packet fields
- intended professional or support audience;
- consent scope and revocation status;
- observer role;
- time pattern;
- recurrence;
- functional impact;
- context or trigger;
- response to prior reviewed action;
- uncertainty and missingness;
- question list;
- review-needed flag;
- ViewDiff from private meaning-state.

## Safety rule
Any symbolic, emotional, or intuitive material must be converted into neutral observation or question form before professional sharing.

Example transformation pattern:

Private layer: symbolic or embodied language.
Professional layer: observable timing, intensity, recurrence, functional impact, and uncertainty.
ViewDiff: records what metaphor or personal meaning was omitted, generalized, or transformed.

## Evaluation criteria
A consent-gated observation packet succeeds if:

1. a professional can read it quickly;
2. private meaning is not exposed unnecessarily;
3. unsupported causality is removed;
4. the packet improves questions rather than replacing judgment;
5. the user can see what changed between private and shared views;
6. consent can be withdrawn or narrowed.

## Public-safe phrase
**The care view should carry the signal, not the whole private world.**
