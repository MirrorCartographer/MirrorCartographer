# Release Readiness Support Boundary

Date: 2026-06-28
Status: public-safe care-support boundary
Attractor: Compression

## Boundary
Mirror Cartographer can help transform lived observations into clearer packets, but it must not present the packet as diagnosis, treatment, legal advice, financial advice, or proof of truth.

## Support-safe use
The Release Readiness Card can help a person or team decide whether a support-related artifact is ready to share with a reviewer, helper, clinician, advocate, educator, or trusted collaborator.

## Required transformation
Private experience should become:
- neutral observation
- dated question
- missing-information list
- review request
- decision boundary
- next evidence action

## Prohibited transformation
Private experience must not become:
- unsupported certainty
- public exposure
- identity claim about another person
- clinical conclusion without clinician review
- crisis instruction
- pressure tactic

## Evidence-based lane
Current health-support AI and documentation research points toward usefulness when AI assists summarization, but also toward review, consent, privacy, and error risks. MC should therefore treat support packets as preparation tools, not authorities.

## Claim status
Care-support design boundary, not clinical advice.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. No private records included.

## Missingness
Needs fictional fixture testing and review by appropriate domain experts before any care-facing deployment.

## Revision reason
Created to keep public MC support artifacts useful without overstating authority.
