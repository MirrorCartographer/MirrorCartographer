# Support Observation Compiler Boundary

## Source status
Public-safe care/support architecture note. Private-context material was used only to identify the need for observation-to-question translation; no private details are included.

## Claim status
Design hypothesis. Evidence-aligned but not clinically validated.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. Contains no personal, animal-care, medical, household, or location details.

## Missingness
- Needs fictional fixtures.
- Needs review by clinicians/social-care professionals before professional use.
- Needs consent language.
- Needs safeguards for crisis, coercion, and over-interpretation.

## Revision reason
The compiler concept needs a care-adjacent boundary that explains how it may support communication without becoming a diagnostic or treatment system.

## Boundary statement
Mirror Cartographer does not heal by assertion, diagnose by pattern, or replace professional care. Its care-adjacent role is narrower:

It can help transform lived observations into clearer, safer questions for qualified humans.

## Safe transformation pattern

1. Private meaning stays private unless explicitly shared.
2. Symbolic or emotional language may be preserved for personal reflection.
3. Professional-facing packets should prioritize observable facts, timelines, functional impact, uncertainty, and questions.
4. Unsupported causality claims must be downgraded to hypotheses or blocked.
5. Missing evidence must be visible.
6. Any health, veterinary, legal, or crisis action must remain human/professional-reviewed.

## Allowed outputs
- observation timeline;
- question list;
- uncertainty map;
- appointment-prep packet;
- record-request checklist;
- support-conversation summary;
- follow-up tracking template.

## Disallowed outputs
- diagnosis;
- treatment plan;
- medication instruction;
- prognosis certainty;
- claims that symbolic recurrence proves biological cause;
- pressure to accept AI documentation, AI advice, or AI interpretation without consent and review.

## Evidence-based alignment
Current health-AI and ambient documentation debates reinforce the need for consent, review, privacy, error awareness, and human oversight. The compiler should therefore treat care support as a communication and continuity layer, not an authority layer.
