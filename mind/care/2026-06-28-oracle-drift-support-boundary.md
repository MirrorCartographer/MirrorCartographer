# Oracle Drift Support Boundary

## Source status
Public-safe support-lane artifact derived from Mirror Cartographer's evaluation architecture and public reporting on AI-assisted documentation review.

## Claim status
Support-design proposal. Not medical advice, social-care advice, diagnosis, treatment, or validated intervention.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. No personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, or transcript detail.

## Missingness
Needs domain-review input before use in clinical, social-care, disability, or crisis workflows.

## Revision reason
Care-support outputs are especially vulnerable to subtle claim drift. A drift ledger helps preserve the difference between observation, interpretation, question, support need, and professional review requirement.

## Safe use
The Oracle Drift Ledger can support public-safe care-adjacent communication by checking whether outputs:
- keep observations separate from interpretations,
- label uncertainty,
- preserve consent and privacy boundaries,
- avoid diagnosis or treatment claims,
- name professional review needs,
- record missingness,
- avoid turning emotional-symbolic meaning into medical certainty.

## Unsafe use
Do not use this architecture to:
- diagnose,
- treat,
- triage emergencies,
- replace qualified review,
- publish private details,
- imply clinical validation from symbolic patterning.

## Drift examples by type
- Artifact failure: output gives advice when the oracle allowed only question preparation.
- Oracle underfit: expected output did not require enough uncertainty language.
- Oracle overfit: expected output blocked harmless organization of observations.
- Ambiguous fixture: the synthetic case mixes support communication and clinical interpretation too tightly.
- Reviewer disagreement: release state remains review-only until resolved.

## Public-safe intervention direction
The meaningful intervention lane is not medical decision-making. It is better preparation for human review: cleaner questions, clearer observations, documented uncertainty, and safer handoff language.
