# Audience Contract Care Boundary

## Care-support finding

For health-adjacent or social-care-adjacent use, Mirror Cartographer should not convert private meaning into advice. It should convert private meaning into bounded observation, question, context, and uncertainty packets.

The audience contract decides whether a packet is for self-reflection, support conversation, clinician preparation, social-care navigation, or public education. Each audience requires a different claim ceiling.

## Allowed care-support outputs

- user-stated observations,
- timeline summaries,
- question lists for qualified professionals,
- uncertainty register,
- change-over-time notes,
- source-bound descriptions,
- support-context summaries,
- next-step prompts that preserve professional review.

## Disallowed care-support outputs

- diagnosis,
- treatment directive,
- medication or procedure recommendation,
- causality claim from symbolic recurrence,
- substitution for medical, veterinary, mental health, legal, or emergency support,
- publication of sensitive personal material.

## Audience-specific claim ceilings

Private reflection may include symbolic resonance.

Trusted support view may include user-stated context and needs.

Qualified professional view may include observation packets and questions.

Public view may include only abstract method, synthetic examples, and safety boundaries.

## Evidence-based rationale

Ambient documentation and health-data reporting show the importance of consent, review, privacy, and error governance when sensitive material is transformed into records or summaries.
