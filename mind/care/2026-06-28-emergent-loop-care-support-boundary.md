# Emergent Loop Care Support Boundary

## Source status
- Public-safe MC architecture source.
- Fresh public context: ambient clinical documentation, patient-generated data, AI literacy, and consent/privacy concerns.
- Private-context material used only to understand why care-adjacent boundaries matter. No private details included.

## Claim status
Support-communication design note. Not a clinical, therapeutic, diagnostic, veterinary, legal, or emergency-care protocol.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. Abstract care-support method only.

## Missingness
- No clinician validation.
- No patient/caregiver study.
- No measured outcome effect.
- No emergency workflow.
- No integration with health records.

## Revision reason
The operating loop must include a care-adjacent lane without overclaiming medical authority.

## Core boundary
MC may help convert confusing lived material into better questions, but it must not convert uncertainty into diagnosis.

## Care-support loop
1. Capture observation in plain language.
2. Separate observation, feeling, metaphor, interpretation, and outside source.
3. Convert unsupported claims into questions.
4. Preserve missingness.
5. Create a professional-review packet only when appropriate.
6. Record what changed after professional review.

## Safe output shape
A care-adjacent output should look like:
- observed pattern;
- timeframe if known;
- severity/impact if known;
- what is missing;
- questions for a qualified professional;
- source status;
- review boundary.

## Unsafe output shape
The system fails if it:
- asserts diagnosis;
- recommends treatment as authority;
- treats metaphor as evidence;
- hides missingness;
- implies biological effect without evidence;
- bypasses professional review in high-stakes contexts.

## Evidence-aligned opportunity
The practical value is in communication quality, continuity, consent, and reviewability. Any biological or social-care benefit would be indirect and would require separate evidence.
