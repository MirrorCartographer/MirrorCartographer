# Contract Execution Care Boundary

## Summary

Mirror Cartographer can support care-adjacent communication only when observation, uncertainty, question, review status, and privacy boundary remain visible through the full transformation path.

## Source status

Public-safe synthesis from the Mirror Cartographer repository plus public reporting and research on ambient clinical documentation, consent, review, privacy, and AI-generated documentation errors.

## Claim status

Support-communication boundary. Not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment recommendation, clinical decision support, veterinary advice, legal advice, or emergency guidance.

## Privacy status

Public-safe. No private health, animal-care, household, location, financial, relationship, credential, or transcript details.

## Boundary rule

Care-adjacent outputs may compile observations into better questions.

They must not compile symbolic resonance into diagnosis, treatment direction, causality, certainty, or urgency claims without qualified review.

## Contract execution requirements for care-adjacent artifacts

A care-support artifact should show:

- source type: user observation, public source, fictional fixture, or reviewed source,
- claim status: observation, question, hypothesis, or reviewed finding,
- missingness: what is not known,
- reviewer burden: what kind of qualified review is needed,
- privacy state: what was removed, generalized, or withheld,
- transformation note: how the private source became the shareable packet,
- next action type: observe, document, ask, review, seek care, or emergency pathway where appropriate.

## Evidence-based lane

Public reporting on ambient clinical documentation shows real adoption and possible workflow benefits. It also shows persistent concerns: consent, privacy, transcription errors, hallucinated content, liability, and the need for clinician review.

MC should learn from that boundary rather than imitate clinical authority.

## Product implication

The care lane should produce reviewed question packets and longitudinal observation summaries, not answers that present themselves as clinical conclusions.

## Missingness

- This note does not evaluate a specific health or social-care intervention.
- This note does not establish clinical benefit.
- This note does not define an emergency protocol.
- This note does not replace professional judgment.

Added to align the Contract Execution Ledger with the persistent care-support lane while preserving strict non-diagnostic boundaries.
