# Care/Social-Support Lane: Minimum Continuity Care Boundary

Date: 2026-06-28
Attractor: Compression
Privacy status: public-safe
Claim status: communication-support hypothesis only

## Finding
MC's care-adjacent value should be framed as observation-to-question continuity, not diagnosis or treatment. The useful object is a packet that helps a person or caregiver communicate clearly with an appropriate professional while preserving uncertainty and consent.

## Allowed support functions
- organize observations
- separate observation from interpretation
- preserve timelines
- mark unknowns
- prepare questions
- summarize changes since last review
- identify what needs professional confirmation
- record outcomes after professional review

## Disallowed functions
- diagnose
- triage emergencies
- recommend treatment changes
- replace medical, veterinary, legal, or social-care professionals
- present symbolic interpretation as factual cause
- claim biological effect without evidence

## Required packet labels
- Source status
- Claim status
- Privacy status
- Missingness
- Revision reason
- Professional-review need

## Evidence boundary
Public reporting in 2026 continues to show both the promise and risk of AI-generated health/social-care notes. The safest design direction is reviewed support, not autonomous authority.

## Practical protocol
1. Capture private narrative.
2. Convert to neutral observations.
3. Preserve user concerns as questions.
4. Mark missing data.
5. Remove private irrelevant detail.
6. Produce professional-facing handoff.
7. Record professional feedback as a separate source layer.
8. Revise the continuity record with explicit reason.

## Missingness
No clinical study, no veterinary study, no social-care deployment, no professional review board, no HIPAA/security analysis, no emergency safety validation.

## Next test
Build one fictional example packet and ask whether a professional-facing reader can identify: what happened, what is uncertain, what is being asked, and what should not be inferred.
