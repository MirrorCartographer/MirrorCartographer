# Care / Support Lane: Artifact Boundary Bill for Support Communication

## Public-safe finding

Mirror Cartographer should not convert support-related material into conclusions. It should convert it into reviewable communication objects with explicit boundaries.

## Support-use pattern

The Artifact Bill of Boundaries can be used to prepare:

1. observation summaries,
2. question lists,
3. change-over-time notes,
4. uncertainty registers,
5. care-team handoff packets,
6. digital-support app evaluations.

## Boundary requirement

Any health, mental-health, social-care, or animal-care-adjacent artifact must carry a visible label:

- not a diagnosis,
- not treatment instruction,
- not a substitute for qualified review,
- intended to improve communication and record quality.

## Evidence basis

Public reporting in 2026 shows continuing concern about AI-generated health and mental-health information, including misleading advice, insufficient safeguards, surveillance/privacy concerns, and lack of credible evidence for many support apps.

## Design requirement

The system should separate:

- what was observed,
- who observed it,
- when it was observed,
- what is uncertain,
- what question should be asked next,
- what requires professional review.

## Safe intervention target

The intervention is not clinical decision-making. The intervention is **better boundary-labeled communication**.

## Claim status

Care-support boundary and product requirement.

## Privacy status

Public-safe. Contains no personal, household, medical, animal-care, location, financial, relationship, credential, or transcript detail.

## Missingness

Needs clinical, veterinary, legal, accessibility, and social-care review before any real-world support deployment.

## Revision reason

Translate the Artifact Bill of Boundaries into a safe support-communication lane without making medical or social-care claims.
