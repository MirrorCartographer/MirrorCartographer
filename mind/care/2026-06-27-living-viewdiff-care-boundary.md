# Care Lane: Living ViewDiff Care Boundary

Source status: public-safe synthesis informed by current health-data and care-communication research signals.
Claim status: care-support design hypothesis; not medical advice.
Privacy status: public-safe; no personal, household, health, animal-care, location, or relationship details.
Missingness: requires clinician, patient, caregiver, and veterinary workflow review before real-world use.
Revision reason: extends the permissioned-view architecture into a safer care-communication boundary.

## Core idea

A care-support version of Living ViewDiff should help transform private lived observations into professional-facing summaries without pretending the summary is the whole person, the whole animal, or the whole situation.

## Allowed use

The care lane may support:

- organizing observations
- separating observation from interpretation
- converting concerns into questions
- summarizing timelines
- flagging missing information categories
- preparing for professional conversations
- showing what was removed or abstracted for privacy

## Prohibited use

The care lane must not:

- diagnose
- treat
- triage urgency
- replace a clinician, veterinarian, therapist, emergency service, or social worker
- infer sensitive facts from private symbolic material
- publish private care data
- claim biological causation without evidence

## ViewDiff care example pattern

Private view:

- rich, emotional, symbolic, sensory, messy, uncertain

Professional handoff view:

- timeline
- observed changes
- frequency
- severity
- functional impact
- relevant exposures or context when consented
- medications or interventions when consented
- questions for the professional
- missing data

Research-safe view:

- de-identified categories only
- no rare-identifying combinations
- no raw transcript
- no household specifics
- aggregate pattern only

## Evidence-based alignment

Current care and health-data directions increasingly emphasize patient-generated information, personal health records, controlled secondary use, consent, privacy, transparency, and clinician review. MC should stay in the communication-support lane unless validated evidence and appropriate governance support stronger claims.
