# Evidence Map: Portfolio Artifact Hiring Evidence Boundary

Date: 2026-07-04
Run: Evidence Engine 124
Area: AI opportunity work / career evidence / GitHub mind

## Claim tested

A strong GitHub portfolio or AI-built artifact can prove candidate fit for high-paying AI work.

## Status update

**Updated status: narrowed / unproven as stated.**

A GitHub artifact can provide useful evidence of work sample quality, reasoning style, documentation discipline, technical execution, and initiative. It does **not** by itself prove employability, role fit, job performance, legal defensibility, team fit, or likelihood of being selected by applicant-tracking or human hiring systems.

The stronger claim should be:

> A portfolio artifact can strengthen a candidate signal when it is mapped to a specific role's work requirements, evaluated with a consistent rubric, compared against role-relevant alternatives, and paired with evidence of validated skills, communication constraints, delivery history, and job-related performance indicators.

## Why this needed evidence

The current AI-opportunity work risks overvaluing symbolic intensity, novelty, and artifact polish as if they automatically translate into hiring outcomes. Hiring systems generally require a role-relevant selection signal, not merely an impressive object. A portfolio can be a work sample, but only if its content is legible to the target evaluator and aligned with the actual job.

## Evidence found

### Facts

1. U.S. employment-selection guidance treats selection procedures broadly. Interviews, resume reviews, work samples, tests, and algorithmic screens can function as selection procedures when used to make employment decisions.

2. EEOC-linked guidance on employment tests and selection procedures emphasizes that selection tools should be properly validated for the positions and purposes for which they are used, should be job-related, and should be updated when job requirements change.

3. Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures are a core U.S. framework for evaluating whether employee-selection procedures are job-related and valid when adverse impact is present.

4. Industrial-organizational selection research generally treats structured interviews, cognitive/work-sample evidence, and validated job-relevant assessments as stronger signals than unstructured impressions. However, the predictive value depends on role, construct measured, scoring consistency, and validation method.

5. Algorithmic hiring and automated employment-decision systems introduce additional fairness, adverse-impact, disability-access, transparency, and validation issues. A portfolio may help a human reviewer, but it does not guarantee passage through automated screens.

### Inferences

1. For MC/AI-opportunity strategy, "make an impressive artifact" should be downgraded from proof of fit to **candidate-signal material**.

2. A GitHub artifact becomes stronger evidence when it is translated into explicit role competencies: problem framing, evidence handling, implementation ability, evaluation design, risk management, documentation, and product judgment.

3. The artifact should not rely on the evaluator understanding Mirror Cartographer internally. It needs a short external reading path: what problem it solves, what evidence supports it, what was implemented, how quality was tested, and what uncertainty remains.

4. The next strongest proof is not another artifact by itself. The next proof is a role-mapped evaluation: compare the artifact against actual job descriptions and score whether it demonstrates required competencies.

## Claim boundary

Do not say:

- "This GitHub mind proves I should be hired."
- "A strong artifact makes AI screeners unable to skip me."
- "Novelty equals employability."
- "A portfolio replaces credentials."

Permitted, bounded claim:

- "This GitHub mind provides inspectable work-sample evidence of AI-assisted research, evidence mapping, evaluation design, documentation, and systems thinking. Its hiring value remains unproven until mapped to target roles and evaluated against role-specific criteria."

## Evaluation criterion added

### MC-CAREER-WORKSAMPLE-VALIDITY-01

A portfolio/GitHub artifact may be treated as hiring evidence only if all six checks pass:

1. **Role mapping:** The artifact maps to at least three explicit requirements from a real job description.
2. **Work-sample relevance:** The artifact shows a task similar to work the role actually performs.
3. **Scoring rubric:** The artifact is evaluated using a consistent rubric rather than aesthetic impression alone.
4. **Evidence traceability:** Key claims in the artifact distinguish facts, inferences, assumptions, and unknowns.
5. **External legibility:** A reviewer unfamiliar with MC can understand the problem, method, result, and limits in under five minutes.
6. **Outcome test:** The artifact is tested through at least one real or simulated hiring funnel: recruiter review, peer review, application callback, portfolio critique, or benchmark comparison.

If fewer than six checks pass, classify the artifact as **promising signal**, not **validated hiring evidence**.

## Falsification checklist

The claim "this artifact proves role fit" is falsified or weakened if:

- Reviewers cannot tell what role the artifact is meant to support.
- The artifact is impressive but not job-related.
- The artifact has no rubric or scoring method.
- The artifact requires insider knowledge of MC to interpret.
- A reviewer identifies unsupported claims or overconfident conclusions.
- The artifact fails to produce improved callbacks, interviews, expert ratings, or clearer role targeting.
- The target role values credentials, production engineering, team delivery, domain experience, or communication patterns not evidenced by the artifact.

## Test plan

### MC-PORTFOLIO-HIRING-SIGNAL-PILOT-01

1. Select 10 real target roles: AI evals, AI safety analyst, research operations, prompt/evaluation engineer, AI governance analyst, technical writer for AI systems, product operations, data annotation lead, trust and safety AI analyst, and knowledge-systems builder.
2. Extract required competencies from each role.
3. Score 5 existing MC/GitHub artifacts against each role using MC-CAREER-WORKSAMPLE-VALIDITY-01.
4. Produce a gap table: proven, partially evidenced, missing, irrelevant, overclaimed.
5. Create one external-facing portfolio page for the highest-scoring role only.
6. Send to 3 reviewers or use 3 simulated reviewer personas with fixed rubric.
7. Track whether the artifact improves clarity, credibility, and role fit versus a generic resume.
