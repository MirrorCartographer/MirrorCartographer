## Attractor

Representational Fidelity

## Pull

MC is being pulled toward a sharper distinction:

Privacy safety asks: Did we avoid exposing what must not be exposed?

Representational fidelity asks: Did we preserve what mattered after abstraction?

The first protects the source.

The second protects the meaning.

Both are required.

## Risk if absent

Without this attractor, MC may become safe by becoming generic.

It may produce artifacts that are clean, compliant, and useless.

It may preserve privacy while destroying the thing that made the architecture worth preserving.

## Design pressure

Every public-safe artifact should carry a small structural bill:

- what shaped it
- what survived
- what was removed
- what was generalized
- what remains uncertain
- what cannot be claimed

## Boundary

This attractor must not be used as an excuse to leak private details.

When private details are structurally important, the compiler should translate them into source classes, invariants, and missingness notes.

## Phrase

Do not publish the private source.

Do not flatten the signal into fog.

Preserve the shape without exposing the origin.
