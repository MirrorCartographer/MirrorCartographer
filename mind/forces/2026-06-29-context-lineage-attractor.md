# Context Lineage Attractor

## Attractor
Context Lineage

## Core phrase
Do not publish the source. Publish the path the claim survived.

## Source status
Public-safe synthesis from available MC architecture context, repository publication constraints, and current public research on AI memory/provenance.

## Claim status
Design principle and product requirement.

## Privacy status
Public-safe. No private details included.

## Missingness
No automated implementation yet. No empirical reviewer study yet. No CI enforcement yet.

## Meaning
Mirror Cartographer is not only a reflection system. It is a crossing system: private or semi-private material may shape understanding, but only inspected public-safe claims should cross into publication.

A Context Lineage Ledger makes that crossing visible. It does not reveal the protected source. It reveals the path of abstraction, redaction, synthesis, claim classification, missingness, and release decision.

## Distinction from prior attractors
- Source Boundary asks what type of material shaped the artifact.
- Claim Transport asks what claim crossed.
- Claim Influence asks what shaped the claim.
- Temporal Validity asks whether context has expired, changed, or been superseded.
- Context Lineage asks how the artifact moved through all of those gates over time.

## Failure if absent
MC can become polished but uninspectable. The artifact may be safe in surface content while hiding how it was formed, what it inherited, what it revised, and what it failed to prove.

## Design imperative
Every public artifact should carry enough lineage for a reviewer to inspect the crossing without needing access to the protected source.
