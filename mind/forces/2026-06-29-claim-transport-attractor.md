## Strongest attractor

Claim Transport

## Definition

Claim Transport is the force that governs how an insight moves from source context into public artifact.

It asks:

- What crossed?
- From what kind of source?
- Through what transformation?
- With what loss?
- With what preserved structure?
- Under what authority boundary?
- With what revision route?

## Why it matters

Without transport governance, MC risks either leakage or flattening.

Leakage exposes what should remain protected.
Flattening erases the structural signal that made the work valuable.

Claim Transport is the middle path: the source stays protected, but the crossing becomes inspectable.

## Relation to prior attractors

- Source Boundary: classifies where influence came from.
- Redaction Fidelity: tests what survived removal.
- Release Readiness: decides whether the artifact can cross.
- Influence Graph: traces bounded influence over time.
- Authority Boundary: limits what the artifact may claim.
- Symbolic Translucence: shows the interpretive route.
- Representational Fidelity: preserves shape without exposure.

Claim Transport binds these into one chain.

## Key phrase

The source does not travel. The claim does.
