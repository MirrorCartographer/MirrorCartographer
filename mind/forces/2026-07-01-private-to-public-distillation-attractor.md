# Force Attractor: Private-to-Public Distillation

Date: 2026-07-01

## Attractor

Private-to-public distillation.

## Core phrase

The source may teach the rule, but the rule must survive without the source.

## What it pulls together

- source status
- claim status
- privacy status
- missingness
- revision reason
- evidence boundaries
- symbolic resonance boundaries
- public-safe implementation planning

## What it prevents

- private-source leakage
- pattern-to-proof collapse
- memory-as-authority collapse
- public artifacts that depend on hidden source material
- revision without reason
- missingness hidden behind confident language

## Product implication

Mirror Cartographer should not only have a memory layer. It should have a publication layer that asks what memory is allowed to become after privacy, evidence, and claim boundaries are applied.

## Research implication

Future MC research should test whether public-safe abstractions remain useful after all private examples are removed.

## Evaluation implication

A public artifact passes only if a reviewer can understand and evaluate the rule without needing the private source.
