# Source Survivability Attractor

## Attractor
Source Survivability

## Sentence
A source is not usable because it is present. It is usable only when it survives the claim it is asked to carry.

## Force pattern
Mirror Cartographer repeatedly faces a dangerous compression:

`retrieved = relevant = admissible = publishable = true`

The Source Survivability layer breaks that chain.

## Public-safe transformation
Private context may reveal the shape of a requirement. It may not become the public evidence for the requirement.

Generated interpretation may inspire language. It may not become discovered proof.

Repository text may prove public language. It may not prove runtime behavior.

Memory may point toward a verification path. It may not substitute for verification.

## System pressure
As MC grows, its risk is not only false claims. Its risk is source confusion: treating old, private, duplicate, generated, or remembered materials as if they were current public evidence.

## Design law
Every claim needs a source. Every source needs a boundary. Every boundary needs a survivability state.

## Key phrase
Do not let remembered relevance impersonate admissible evidence.
