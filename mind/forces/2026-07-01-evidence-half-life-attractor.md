# Attractor: Evidence Half-Life

## Name
Evidence Half-Life

## Compression
A claim is not safe because it was once admitted. It is safe only while its authority has not outlived its source, privacy boundary, and revision history.

## Why this belongs in MC
Mirror Cartographer is a continuity system. Continuity without decay becomes ghost accumulation. Decay without trace becomes amnesia. Evidence half-life gives MC a middle path: preserve history, downgrade authority, and keep meaning from pretending to be current proof.

## Public-safe doctrine

- Preserve the path, but do not let every path testify.
- Keep history visible as history.
- Mark stale claims before they become public claims.
- Refresh research before using it as current evidence.
- Verify product status before using it as built proof.
- Re-check private-derived abstractions every time they travel.
- Keep revision reasons attached to downgrades.

## Symbolic form
A lamp with a dimmer, not an on/off switch.

The old light is not evil. It is just no longer bright enough to guide a current step without being checked.

## Operational form

`claim + source + privacy + time + revision_reason -> current authority`

## Failure if absent

- old context keeps steering new claims
- roadmap becomes product proof
- private examples leak through public abstractions
- user corrections get overwritten by pattern confidence
- citations become decorative instead of load-bearing
- MC becomes an archive that remembers without governing what memory is allowed to do

## Key phrase
Do not only ask whether the claim was once true. Ask whether the claim is still allowed to speak.
