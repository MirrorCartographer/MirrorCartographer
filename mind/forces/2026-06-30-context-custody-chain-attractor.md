# Context Custody Chain Attractor

## Attractor

A context may shape the map without becoming part of the map.

## Compression

Private context is not cargo. It is weather. It can explain why the architecture needs a roof, but the rain itself does not belong inside the public blueprint.

## Operating phrase

Do not publish the private path. Publish the custody rule that prevents the path from being misused.

## Force pattern

Mirror Cartographer repeatedly encounters material that is meaningful, sensitive, symbolic, recursive, and continuity-heavy. The system’s public value depends on converting that material into safe architecture without pretending the private source is public evidence.

The force is custody:

- private context may influence design attention;
- abstraction must remove reconstructable detail;
- claim status must downgrade unsupported meaning;
- source status must stay visible;
- missingness must be named;
- revision reason must explain why the artifact changed.

## Failure mode

Redaction without custody creates false cleanliness. The artifact may contain no obvious private detail, but still smuggle private authority into a public claim.

## Stabilizer

Every public artifact gets a custody chain:

source -> privacy boundary -> transformation -> claim lane -> missingness -> revision reason.

## One-line law

A private source may illuminate the need for a boundary; it may not become the public proof that crosses it.
