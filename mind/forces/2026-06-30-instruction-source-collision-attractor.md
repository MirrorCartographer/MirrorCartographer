## Attractor

Instruction Source Collision

## Core sentence

A source may speak. It may not automatically command.

## Why it matters

The more MC connects memory, files, public research, repository material, symbolic artifacts, and reflective outputs, the more likely it is that source content will contain instruction-shaped language.

That language must not silently become authority.

## Boundary stack relation

Instruction Source Collision sits after source retrieval and before interpretation admission.

It depends on:

- Source Status
- Claim Status
- Context Quarantine
- Temporal Validity
- Context Lineage
- Contestability Receipt
- Public Proof Packet
- Interpretation Admissibility

It adds:

- allowed authority level
- denied authority level
- command-shaped text detection
- source-role admission decision
- public-safe transformation requirement

## Product implication

Every public-facing MC output should be able to answer:

- What source informed this?
- What was that source allowed to do?
- What was it not allowed to do?
- What was transformed before publication?
- What remains missing or uncertain?

## Failure image

The map starts taking orders from its own landmarks.

## Desired image

The map hears every landmark, labels each voice, and lets only the active navigator steer.
