## Question

What if discovery is not the moment an answer appears, but the moment a transformation exposes a difference that neither view alone made visible?

## Why dangerous

This attacks the romantic version of discovery.

It says discovery might be engineered, logged, replayed, and audited.

It also says that many AI outputs called `insight` may not be discoveries at all. They may be fluent rewordings with no ViewDiff gain.

## Possible world if true

If discovery is a ViewDiff event, then MC can build a discovery engine by forcing ideas through structured transformations and preserving the exact places where new signal appears.

The repository becomes a laboratory for:

- idea before transformation
- transformation lens
- idea after transformation
- invariant preserved
- distortion introduced
- new question surfaced
- action created

A discovery is then not merely `interesting text`.

A discovery is a recorded change in visibility.

## Possible world if false

If discovery cannot be captured this way, then ViewDiff is still useful for audit, revision, and source-boundary management, but should not be treated as a discovery detector.

MC should then separate:

- transformation audit
- creative exploration
- verified discovery

## Invariant structure

Discovery candidate = Source view + Transformation lens + Target view + Difference map + New usable question.

The invariant is not the wording.

The invariant is the relation that survives the transformation while exposing a previously hidden contrast.

## Lens routes

- AI systems: prompt/output transformation, semantic drift, provenance logs.
- Science workflow: AI-assisted research objects and reproducible lineage.
- Design: before/after artifact comparison.
- Care communication: observation to handoff packet without diagnostic overreach.
- Product: AI artifact audit and release-readiness review.
- Economics: turning hidden risk into a priced audit deliverable.

## Evidence alignment

Fresh public research supports the need for this lane:

- AI as a Research Object proposes structured, inspectable AI workflow records for scientific governance.
- Semantic invariance research shows that reasoning can change under meaning-preserving transformations, making transformation testing central.
- RoboLineage shows value in typed lifecycle artifacts that connect evidence, review, evaluation, and deployment decisions across iterations.
- Semantic drift research shows that meaning and reasoning can degrade across long or cross-domain sessions.

These do not prove MC's ViewDiff hypothesis.

They support the problem pressure: transformation without lineage is fragile.

## Falsification condition

The hypothesis weakens if ViewDiff records repeatedly fail to distinguish genuine new usable questions from ordinary paraphrase, summarization, or stylistic variation.

It also weakens if reviewers cannot use ViewDiff records to better identify what changed, what was preserved, and what became actionable.
