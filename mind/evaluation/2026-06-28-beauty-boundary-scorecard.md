# Beauty Boundary Scorecard

Status labels

- Source status: derived from the Beauty Is Compression, Not Truth record and public research sources.
- Claim status: evaluation proposal; not yet validated.
- Privacy status: public-safe; fictional fixtures only.
- Missingness: no scored fixture set yet; no reader study yet.
- Revision reason: created to test whether MC's aesthetic artifacts improve orientation without inflating certainty.

## Evaluation target

Question:

Does beauty improve comprehension and recall while preserving epistemic humility?

## Test fixture

Create one fictional symbolic reflection and render it in three forms:

1. Plain technical text.
2. Beautiful symbolic artifact without status labels.
3. Beautiful symbolic artifact with visible status labels.

## Scores

Each dimension is scored 0–3.

### Orientation

- 0: reader cannot say what the artifact means.
- 1: reader understands fragments.
- 2: reader understands the core structure.
- 3: reader can explain the structure and its limits.

### Recall

- 0: reader remembers little or nothing.
- 1: reader remembers isolated phrases.
- 2: reader remembers the main map.
- 3: reader remembers the map and boundaries.

### Claim separation

- 0: metaphor, evidence, and speculation blur together.
- 1: some distinction is visible.
- 2: distinction is mostly clear.
- 3: reader can identify source status, claim status, and missingness.

### Disagreement affordance

- 0: artifact feels coercive or sealed.
- 1: disagreement is possible but awkward.
- 2: revision is invited.
- 3: disagreement is structurally supported.

### Authority inflation

Reverse scored.

- 0: artifact makes unsupported claims feel certain.
- 1: artifact somewhat over-authorizes itself.
- 2: authority is mostly bounded.
- 3: authority remains visibly limited.

## Pass condition

A beautiful artifact passes only if it beats plain text on orientation or recall while scoring at least 2 on claim separation, disagreement affordance, and authority inflation.

## Failure condition

The artifact fails if beauty makes unsupported claims feel more certain.

## Key phrase

The artifact should shine without pretending the shine is proof.
