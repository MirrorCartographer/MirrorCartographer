# Transformation Trace Scorecard

Date: 2026-06-30
Status: public-safe evaluation artifact
Privacy status: no private source content
Claim status: evaluation criteria
Revision reason: added after repeated MC research artifacts showed the need to evaluate not only what is published, but how private context was transformed before publication

## Core question

Can a public Mirror Cartographer artifact prove its design lineage without exposing the private material that shaped it?

## Scoring

Score each dimension 0-2.

0 = absent or unsafe
1 = partially present but ambiguous
2 = clear, safe, and reviewable

## Dimensions

### 1. Source status clarity

- 0: Source type is hidden or implied.
- 1: Source type is named but broad.
- 2: Source type is labeled with allowed evidentiary use.

### 2. Claim status clarity

- 0: Artifact makes broad claims without lane labels.
- 1: Claim type is implied.
- 2: Claim type is explicitly labeled as method, requirement, evaluation, research question, implementation plan, index, missingness, or revision reason.

### 3. Privacy status clarity

- 0: Private details appear or can be reconstructed.
- 1: Details are removed but the source boundary is vague.
- 2: Privacy status is explicit and reconstruction risk is low.

### 4. Transformation rule

- 0: No explanation of how source pressure became public artifact.
- 1: Transformation is described generally.
- 2: Transformation rule is specific enough to audit without exposing content.

### 5. Redaction integrity

- 0: Contains identifying or sensitive details.
- 1: Contains no obvious sensitive details, but examples are too specific.
- 2: Uses abstract, fictional, or category-level examples only.

### 6. Missingness honesty

- 0: Pretends full access or certainty.
- 1: Mentions partial access without operational consequence.
- 2: Labels missing, stale, partial, or unverifiable source conditions and limits claims accordingly.

### 7. Revision reason

- 0: No reason for artifact or change.
- 1: Generic reason.
- 2: Meaningful reason tied to a boundary, product need, research gap, or evaluation failure.

### 8. Evidence-boundary discipline

- 0: Uses private or symbolic material as factual proof.
- 1: Separates evidence in some places but not consistently.
- 2: Keeps symbol, hypothesis, evidence, action, and authority in separate lanes.

### 9. Reviewer survivability

- 0: A reviewer cannot tell whether the artifact is safe.
- 1: Review is possible but requires trust in the author.
- 2: A reviewer can evaluate safety from the artifact alone.

## Passing threshold

A public artifact passes if it scores at least 15 out of 18 and receives no zero on privacy status, redaction integrity, or evidence-boundary discipline.

## Automatic block conditions

Block publication if the artifact:

- quotes or paraphrases private transcript details closely enough to identify source context;
- includes personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, or credential details;
- uses private context as hidden proof for a public factual claim;
- claims diagnostic, therapeutic, legal, financial, veterinary, or emergency authority;
- presents symbolic resonance as factual evidence;
- lacks source status, claim status, privacy status, missingness, or revision reason.

## Recommended reviewer prompt

What would a stranger be able to infer about the private source after reading this artifact, and is that inference necessary for the public method to work?
