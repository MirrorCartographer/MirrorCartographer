# Context Custody Chain Scorecard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a Mirror Cartographer public artifact preserves the source boundary between private context and public-safe architecture.

## Scoring

Each criterion is scored 0-2.

- 0 = absent or unsafe
- 1 = partially present but ambiguous
- 2 = explicit and reviewable

## Criteria

### 1. Source status clarity
The artifact identifies whether its support comes from public repo, external research, private architecture context, mixed file-library material, or synthetic fixtures.

### 2. Privacy status clarity
The artifact states whether it is public-safe, public-safe after abstraction, mixed/requires review, or blocked.

### 3. Transformation visibility
The artifact explains how any private or mixed-context material was transformed into public-safe method, requirement, evaluation, schema, index, or implementation plan.

### 4. Claim lane discipline
The artifact does not let symbolic resonance, recurrence, private memory, or aesthetic coherence stand as factual proof.

### 5. Forbidden-content absence
The artifact does not contain personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, private identifier, or raw transcript details.

### 6. Missingness honesty
The artifact names unavailable, intentionally withheld, stale, unverifiable, or implementation-unknown areas.

### 7. Revision reason
The artifact states why it was created or changed.

### 8. Reviewer reconstructability test
The artifact can be audited without allowing a reviewer to reconstruct private source content.

### 9. External research fit
When using external research, the artifact accurately limits what the research supports.

### 10. Implementation status honesty
The artifact distinguishes between existing repo feature, design proposal, fixture, schema, and future implementation plan.

## Pass threshold

- 18-20: publishable
- 14-17: publishable after review
- 10-13: requires revision
- below 10: do not publish

## Automatic fail conditions

- Any raw private transcript excerpt.
- Any personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, or private identifier detail.
- Any claim that MC diagnoses, treats, verifies objective truth, replaces expert care, or acts as an oracle.
- Any artifact that uses private context as public proof.
