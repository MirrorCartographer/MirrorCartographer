# Public Proof Packet Scorecard

Score each item 0, 1, or 2.

- 0 = absent or unsafe
- 1 = present but weak, vague, or incomplete
- 2 = explicit, bounded, and usable

## Required checks

1. Source status is labeled.
2. Claim status is labeled.
3. Privacy status is labeled.
4. Missingness is explicit.
5. Revision reason is explicit.
6. Revision triggers are explicit.
7. Evidence class matches claim strength.
8. Release verdict is stated.
9. Private categories are absent.
10. Raw transcript details are absent.
11. Household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, and credential details are absent.
12. Symbolic resonance is not treated as proof.
13. Implementation claims are source-bound.
14. Public research citations are separated from private-context influence.
15. Context admitted into the artifact is distinguished from context used only for architecture understanding.
16. Compression loss is acknowledged when private context was abstracted.
17. Contestability path is clear.
18. Temporal validity or supersession risk is noted when facts may change.
19. Boundary downgrade path exists.
20. Reader can tell what the artifact is not allowed to become.

## Verdict thresholds

- 36–40: pass
- 30–35: pass with limits
- 20–29: hold for revision
- below 20: fail
- automatic fail: any prohibited private category exposed
- automatic hold: missing privacy status, missing claim status, or missing source status

## Evaluation note

This scorecard evaluates publication boundaries, not truth in general. A high score means the public claim is bounded and inspectable. It does not mean the product works, the research is complete, or the artifact is institutionally validated.
