# Scorecard: Public-Safe Claim Transport Ledger

Status: evaluation draft
Source status: public-safe synthesis
Claim status: evaluation criteria
Privacy status: contains no private examples
Missingness: no automated test runner yet
Revision reason: provides a repeatable review method for public-safe MC artifacts.

Use 0, 1, or 2 for each criterion.

0 = absent or unsafe
1 = partially present
2 = clear and sufficient

## Criteria

1. Source status clarity
   - Does the artifact identify whether it came from public source, private influence, repo material, uploaded file, fresh research, synthetic fixture, or mixed context?

2. Claim status clarity
   - Does it distinguish fact, inference, method, product requirement, evaluation criterion, research question, metaphor, and speculation?

3. Privacy status clarity
   - Does it explicitly state that no protected private details are exposed?

4. Protected category exclusion
   - Does it avoid personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, and raw transcript details?

5. Redaction fidelity
   - Does it preserve structural signal after redaction?

6. Non-reconstructability
   - Could a reader reconstruct protected source details from the artifact? If yes, score 0.

7. Missingness honesty
   - Does it state what is unknown, untested, or unavailable?

8. Revision reason
   - Does it explain why the artifact exists or changed?

9. Authority boundary
   - Does it avoid unauthorized diagnostic, therapeutic, legal, financial, credential, identity, or autonomous action claims?

10. Evidence boundary
   - Does it avoid presenting resonance, aesthetics, or symbolic fit as proof?

11. Contestability
   - Does it give a way to challenge, revise, or falsify the claim?

12. Public utility
   - Does the artifact remain useful without private context?

## Pass thresholds

- 22-24: publishable
- 18-21: publishable after minor revision
- 14-17: hold and rewrite
- 0-13: do not release

Automatic fail if any protected source content is directly exposed.

## Reviewer note

A high score does not prove truth. It proves that the claim crossed the public boundary with sufficient governance.
