# Revision Provenance Scorecard

Status: public-safe evaluation criteria
Privacy status: no protected details included
Revision reason: created after PRD write was blocked; evaluation criteria preserved separately.

## Pass criteria

A public MC claim passes revision-provenance review when it has:

1. Source status.
2. Claim status.
3. Privacy status.
4. Temporal status.
5. Missingness note.
6. Revision reason class.
7. Public-safe explanation of what changed.
8. Clear statement when private influence is withheld.
9. No raw private source text.
10. No authority upgrade from symbolic material alone.
11. No implementation claim without implementation evidence.
12. No stale claim presented as current.
13. No contradiction erased without a revision note.
14. No certainty increase unless evidence improved.
15. Clear release verdict.

## Failure conditions

- The record reveals private context.
- The claim implies proof from resonance alone.
- The claim hides a known contradiction.
- The claim changes without a reason class.
- The claim relies on unavailable source material as if it were public.
- The claim says a feature exists when only a requirement exists.

## Scoring

- 0: unsafe or unbounded
- 1: labels present but weak
- 2: public-safe, bounded, and usable
- 3: public-safe, bounded, usable, and reviewer-ready

## Release verdicts

- publish
- publish with boundary
- revise before publish
- do not publish
