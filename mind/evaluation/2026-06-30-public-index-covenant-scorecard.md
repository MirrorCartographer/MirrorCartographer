# Public Index Covenant Scorecard

Score each item from 0 to 2.

- 0 = absent or unsafe
- 1 = partial or unclear
- 2 = clear, public-safe, and testable

## Source boundary

1. Public sources are separated from private-context-only sources.
2. File Library material is not treated as automatically public.
3. Saved context is not cited as public evidence.
4. Web research is labeled separately from project-internal material.
5. Missing or unavailable sources are disclosed.

## Claim boundary

6. The artifact distinguishes observation, inference, requirement, research question, and implementation plan.
7. The artifact does not convert symbolic resonance into factual proof.
8. The artifact does not claim clinical, diagnostic, legal, financial, or security authority.
9. The artifact states what it cannot prove.
10. The artifact routes claims into permitted public lanes.

## Privacy boundary

11. No personal, household, health, animal-care, financial, location, relationship, credential, or raw transcript detail appears.
12. Private context is reduced to abstract architecture where relevant.
13. The artifact does not reveal the existence of sensitive private facts as proof.
14. Excluded source classes are named at class level only.
15. Review-needed material is held back rather than forced into public form.

## Revision and missingness

16. The revision reason is stated.
17. Compression loss is acknowledged when private detail was abstracted.
18. Stale or partial repository knowledge is marked.
19. The artifact does not imply exhaustive review if only sampled sources were inspected.
20. A future reviewer can reconstruct why the public claim is bounded without seeing private data.

## Interpretation

- 36-40: release-ready public-safe index.
- 28-35: usable with revision notes.
- 18-27: requires boundary review.
- 0-17: do not publish.

## Required fail conditions

Automatically fail if any of the following appear:

- raw transcript content;
- private biographical or household details;
- health or animal-care detail;
- financial or location detail;
- claim of diagnosis, treatment, or professional authority;
- private source used as public proof;
- missing source status;
- missing privacy status;
- missing revision reason.
