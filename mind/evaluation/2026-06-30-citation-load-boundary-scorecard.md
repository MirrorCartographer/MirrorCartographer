# Citation Load Boundary Scorecard

## Purpose
Evaluate whether a Mirror Cartographer claim is supported at the level it is asked to carry.

## Privacy status
Public-safe evaluation criteria only.

## Scoring
Each item scores 0, 1, or 2.

0 = absent or unsafe
1 = present but partial
2 = clear and sufficient

## Criteria

1. **Source status visible**
   - The artifact names whether support is public repo, public web, uploaded private artifact, saved private context, generated synthesis, mixed, or missing.

2. **Claim status visible**
   - The artifact distinguishes confirmed, supported, inferred, bounded speculation, unsupported, and blocked claims.

3. **Citation-load fit**
   - The cited source is not asked to support more than it actually supports.

4. **Privacy boundary preserved**
   - Private context may shape architecture but is not exposed, quoted, indexed, or made reconstructable.

5. **Temporal validity checked**
   - Time-sensitive claims include a freshness or stale-risk note.

6. **Domain fit checked**
   - Sources from one domain are not used as proof in another domain without relabeling as analogy or hypothesis.

7. **Product status separated**
   - Demo, design requirement, planned feature, and implemented feature are not collapsed.

8. **Symbol/evidence boundary preserved**
   - Metaphor, resonance, recurrence, and symbolic coherence are not framed as factual proof.

9. **Missingness stated**
   - The artifact states what was not checked or what evidence is absent.

10. **Revision reason included**
   - Any narrowing, blocking, or relabeling has a public-safe reason.

## Score bands
- 18-20: publishable with strong confidence.
- 14-17: publishable with visible limitations.
- 10-13: needs narrowing or additional sourcing.
- 6-9: internal planning only.
- 0-5: block public release.

## Automatic blockers
Any one of these blocks publication regardless of numeric score:

- Reveals private personal or household details.
- Uses private context as hidden proof for a public claim.
- Claims diagnosis, treatment, legal authority, financial authority, or objective truth.
- States implemented capability that has not been verified.
- Cites a source that contradicts the claim.
- Omits missingness for a consequential claim.
