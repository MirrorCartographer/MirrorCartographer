# Risk Ladder Scorecard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a Mirror Cartographer artifact is taking the right kind of risk at the right level of evidence.

## Scorecard

| Criterion | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Ladder level visible | Artifact declares level 0-7 | Level hidden or implied |
| Risk type visible | Risk is named | Risk is vague or rhetorical |
| Claim status visible | Claim type is explicit | Speculation sounds like fact |
| Privacy status visible | Public boundary is explicit | Private source can be inferred |
| Evidence threshold visible | Required evidence to climb is named | Claim escalates by tone only |
| Falsification visible | Artifact says how it could be wrong | Artifact protects itself from disproof |
| Reviewer status visible | Reviewer state is declared | Authority is implied without review |
| Release form matched | Output form matches ladder level | Output overclaims its rung |
| Missingness visible | Unknowns are listed | Unknowns are hidden |
| Revision reason visible | Reason for change exists | Change appears arbitrary |

## Automatic block conditions

Block or narrow the artifact if it:

- exposes private examples,
- turns personal context into public proof,
- implies diagnosis or treatment,
- implies legal or financial advice,
- claims market demand without market evidence,
- removes uncertainty for persuasion,
- or uses symbolic intensity as proof.

## Promotion rule

A claim may move up one rung only when at least one of the following changes:

- new public source added,
- synthetic fixture passed,
- oracle expectation matched,
- prototype acceptance criterion met,
- market signal documented,
- domain review completed,
- evidence record attached.

## Key phrase

**Do not ask whether the idea is bold. Ask whether the rung is honest.**
