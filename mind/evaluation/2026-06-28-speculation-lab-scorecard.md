# Speculation Lab Scorecard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a Mirror Cartographer speculation is useful intellectual risk or just decorative overreach.

## Score dimensions

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Novelty | Repeats existing idea | Recombines known ideas | Produces a distinct hypothesis or invariant |
| Boundary clarity | No labels | Some labels | Source, claim, privacy, missingness, and revision labels complete |
| Falsifiability | Cannot be tested | Test is vague | Clear falsification or narrowing condition |
| Public safety | Contains risky details | Requires heavy redaction | Public-safe by design |
| Product relevance | Decorative only | Possible relevance | Converts to requirement, prototype, or user-facing behavior |
| Research relevance | No outside anchor | Weak analogy | Linked to literature, benchmark, or research question |
| Care-support safety | Implies treatment/diagnosis | Needs caution | Communication/support only, with review boundary |
| Transformation value | Static phrase | Can be summarized | Can become fixture, schema, rubric, interface behavior, or offer |

## Interpretation

- 0-5: Archive or rewrite.
- 6-9: Keep as bounded hypothesis.
- 10-13: Convert into fixture or product requirement.
- 14-16: Promote to active research lane.

## Required reviewer question

What would make this idea less true, less useful, or more dangerous than it currently appears?

## Example evaluation target

Hypothesis: Understanding is the survival of invariant structure through faithful transformations.

Potential falsifier: If users can repeat an idea across formats without improved prediction, recall, decision quality, or self-explanation, then transformation survival may measure stylistic consistency rather than understanding.
