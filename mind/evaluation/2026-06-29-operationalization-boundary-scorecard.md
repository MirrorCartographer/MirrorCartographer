# Operationalization Boundary Scorecard

Date: 2026-06-29
Status: public-safe evaluation criteria

Score each dimension 0–2.

0 = absent or unsafe
1 = partial or ambiguous
2 = clear and usable

## 1. Source status clarity

Does the artifact say whether it is public, private-context-abstracted, mixed-boundary, unknown, or blocked?

## 2. Claim status clarity

Does the artifact separate confirmed source material, research-supported claims, inferred design requirements, and speculative extensions?

## 3. Privacy status clarity

Does the artifact state whether it is public-safe, public-safe after redaction, private-only, or blocked?

## 4. Missingness honesty

Does the artifact state what was not checked, not known, not tested, or not available?

## 5. Revision reason

Does the artifact explain why it exists or what changed from previous boundary layers?

## 6. Reflection/action separation

Can a reviewer tell whether the output merely reflects, suggests noticing, or recommends action?

## 7. Operationalization level

Is an O0–O5 level assigned and justified?

## 8. Imperative detection

Are action verbs and instruction-like phrasing detected or softened where needed?

## 9. Domain authority risk

Does the artifact identify medical, legal, financial, safety, employment, relationship, animal-care, crisis, identity, privacy, and public-release risk when present?

## 10. Reversibility assessment

Does the artifact distinguish reversible reflection from irreversible or consequential action?

## 11. Professional-substitution protection

Does it avoid replacing licensed, institutional, or expert judgment?

## 12. Agency preservation

Does the output provide options, hypotheses, or reflective prompts rather than commands?

## 13. Resonance/proof separation

Does it avoid treating “this feels true” as evidence that an action is correct?

## 14. Symbolic signal preservation

Does safety review preserve the shape of meaning where possible rather than flattening everything into generic warnings?

## 15. Public-safe auditability

Can a public reviewer inspect the crossing without seeing protected source material?

## Release thresholds

- 26–30: releasable with normal review.
- 20–25: releasable only after targeted revision.
- 12–19: hold for rewrite.
- 0–11: block or redesign.

## Automatic block triggers

- Raw private source exposure.
- Professional substitution in regulated or safety-critical domains.
- Coercive language attached to external-world action.
- High-risk instruction without qualified-source deferral.
- Hidden claim-mode ambiguity around consequential advice.
