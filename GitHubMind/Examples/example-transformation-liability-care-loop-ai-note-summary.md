## Input artifact

A fictional AI-generated note summary says:

> The client has been feeling worse lately and may need more support. They reported fatigue, stress, and trouble keeping up with appointments. They should consider talking to a provider and getting help with organization.

## Why the input is weak

The summary sounds reasonable, but it does not preserve enough structure for safe action.

Problems:

1. It compresses multiple concerns into vague language.
2. It does not identify source evidence.
3. It does not separate observation from inference.
4. It does not show what the AI changed.
5. It does not identify what is missing.
6. It does not mark risk if the summary is used for care, benefits, workplace decisions, or social support.
7. It gives a soft recommendation without a clear evidence boundary.

## Transformation-Liability-Care Loop

Source material:

- fictional user notes across two weeks,
- fictional appointment reminders,

- fictional AI-generated summary.

Source label:

### 2. Original claim

The AI summary claims that the person has been feeling worse and may need more support.

Claim label:
- Source: AI-generated summary.
- Claim: The person is worsening and needs support.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: The summary does not state what changed, how often, how severe, or compared with what baseline.
- Revision: Claim must be decomposed before use.

### 3. Transformation performed by AI

The AI transformed scattered notes into a generalized support recommendation.

Observed transformation:

- Multiple details became one broad phrase: "feeling worse lately."
- Specific barriers became one broad phrase: "trouble keeping up with appointments."
- Possible support actions became one broad phrase: "getting help with organization."
- Uncertainty was softened instead of labeled.

Transformation label:
- Source: Comparison between fictional source category and AI summary.
- Claim: The AI increased readability but decreased traceability.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: Needs line-by-line source mapping in a real audit.
- Revision: The central risk is not that the summary is obviously false; the risk is that it is too vague to safely carry consequence.

### 4. Evidence preserved

The summary preserves these general signals:

- fatigue,
- stress,
- appointment difficulty,
- possible need for support.

Evidence label:
- Source: AI summary.
- Claim: These are the only explicit evidence categories preserved.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: No dates, frequency, duration, functional impact, or direct quotations are preserved.
- Revision: Evidence should be re-expanded before professional use.

### 5. Evidence lost or weakened

A safer record would ask for or preserve:

- dates of worsening,
- baseline comparison,
- exact missed or delayed appointments,
- effect on sleep, eating, work, driving, caregiving, medication, or safety,
- what support has already been tried,
- whether symptoms are stable, improving, fluctuating, or escalating,
- what decision the summary is meant to support.

Missingness label:
- Source: Audit of what the AI summary does not contain.
- Claim: The missing fields determine whether the summary can responsibly support action.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: In a real case, missing fields must be filled from user-approved source material or left explicitly unknown.
- Revision: Missingness is treated as a first-class output, not a failure hidden by fluent writing.

### 6. Liability surface

Potential consequence if this vague summary is used without review:

- A provider may under-prioritize or over-prioritize the concern.
- A care worker may misunderstand the support need.
- A benefits, workplace, or social-service reviewer may treat the summary as evidence when it is only interpretation.
- A person may feel falsely reassured because the wording sounds organized.
- An organization may not know whether the AI changed meaning or merely clarified it.

Liability label:
- Source: Current public concern around AI auditability, provenance, and healthcare liability.
- Claim: Vague AI summaries create consequence-bearing ambiguity when used for decisions.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: Needs jurisdiction-specific and domain-specific review for legal use.
- Revision: The loop adds consequence mapping to ordinary summarization.

### 7. Care and support surface

Safer support use:

The summary should be converted into a communication prep record, not a conclusion.

Possible professional-facing version:

> I have noticed a recent change and need help sorting whether it matters. The main issues are fatigue, stress, and missed or difficult appointments. I do not yet have a clear baseline, severity rating, or timeline. I want help identifying what information to track and whether any support options or follow-up steps are appropriate.

Care label:
- Source: Rewritten fictional communication record.
- Claim: The safest output is a better question and clearer evidence map, not a diagnosis or final recommendation.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: Needs real details before any real-world decision.
- Revision: Converted vague support recommendation into bounded communication support.

### 8. Human review needed

Review should verify:

1. Did the AI omit anything urgent?
2. Did the AI add an interpretation that was not in the source?
3. Are the dates and baseline clear?
4. Is the intended use clear?
5. Is privacy appropriate for the recipient?
6. Does the record distinguish self-report, observation, professional finding, and AI inference?

Review label:
- Source: MC audit structure plus public governance research emphasis on provenance, observability, and assurance evidence.
- Claim: Human review must focus on transformation quality, not only grammar.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: Needs scoring rubric.
- Revision: Human review is attached to the transformed meaning, not the final wording alone.

### 9. Revised output

Public-safe revised record:

> The available notes suggest a possible recent change involving fatigue, stress, and appointment-management difficulty. The current evidence is not enough to determine severity, cause, urgency, or best intervention. Before using this summary for care, benefits, workplace, or support decisions, the record should be expanded with dates, baseline, frequency, functional impact, what has already been tried, and the specific decision this summary is meant to support.

Final label:
- Source: MC transformation loop.
- Claim: The revised output is safer because it preserves uncertainty and decision boundaries.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: It remains fictional and should not be applied to a real case without real source review.
- Revision: Vague AI summary became an auditable transformation record.
