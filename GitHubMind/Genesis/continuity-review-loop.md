## Claim
A living reasoning repository needs a repeated review loop, not just a claim trace or exit boundary.

A claim can be well-sourced, bounded, and authorized at one time, then become unsafe, stale, over-applied, or commercially misleading when context changes. Continuity requires scheduled reinspection of claims, practical lanes, missingness, and action boundaries.

## Core primitive

Observation -> Claim -> Authority -> Use -> Outcome -> Review -> Continue / Revise / Exit / Museum

The loop asks five questions each cycle:

1. What changed since the claim was last used?
2. What evidence now weakens, strengthens, or redirects it?
3. Is the claim still allowed to support the same action?
4. Is the missingness still tolerable for the intended use?
5. Should the artifact continue, revise, exit, or become a Museum candidate?

## Practical lane 2: medical / social-care support
The loop supports continuity documentation without claiming clinical authority.

Allowed use:

- organize observations over time
- flag uncertainty and missing evidence
- preserve source fidelity
- document revision triggers
- prepare clearer questions for qualified professionals
- separate user observation from AI interpretation

Not allowed use:

- diagnosis
- treatment selection
- medication changes
- replacing clinician, therapist, veterinarian, legal, or emergency judgment
- converting symbolic resonance into medical proof
