# Genesis — Consent Authority Envelope

## Status

Public-safe governance primitive.

## Attractor

Contradiction.

## Core contradiction

A claim can be well-sourced, well-labeled, portable, inspectable, reversible, and still become unsafe if it acts outside the authority granted by the person, institution, workflow, or care role that receives it.

The existing GitHub Mind has been strengthening provenance, verification, recovery, deployment proof, claim cards, and carry-forward boundaries. The missing boundary is consent-to-act.

## Primitive

**Consent Authority Envelope**

A Consent Authority Envelope is the visible boundary around a claim, artifact, agent, workflow, or care-support summary that states what it is allowed to do, who authorized that allowance, what action ceiling applies, what data may be used, what reuse is prohibited, and what event revokes or narrows authority.

## Core upgrade

**Verification is not authorization.**

A verified claim may still be unauthorized to act. A useful summary may still be unauthorized to enter a record. A provenance-rich artifact may still be unauthorized to travel into a different context. A governance system must therefore attach an authority envelope to any claim or workflow that can influence action.

## Minimum fields

- Claim or artifact identifier.
- Authorized actor.
- Authorizing party or role.
- Permission scope.
- Prohibited uses.
- Data-use boundary.
- Reuse boundary.
- Action ceiling.
- Escalation trigger.
- Revocation trigger.
- Expiration or review date.
- Audit trail location.
- Human review requirement.
- Emergency or failover exception, if any.

## Relationship to existing GitHub Mind primitives

- **Claim Card Schema:** carries source, confidence, missingness, and correction path.
- **Verification Surface Protocol:** makes provenance visible at reuse.
- **Provenance Carry-Forward Ledger:** checks whether labels survive movement.
- **Governance State Machine:** tracks lifecycle transitions.
- **Deployment Proof Gate:** demands operational evidence before reliance.
- **Consent Authority Envelope:** states what the artifact is permitted to do after it is verified.

## Practical lane 1 — income

Potential offer: **AI Consent Boundary Review**.

Scope:

- map agent or AI workflow permissions;
- identify unbounded reuse paths;
- separate verification from authorization;
- define action ceilings;
- draft consent and revocation surfaces;
- produce a public-safe governance memo.

This can serve organizations using AI assistants, agentic workflows, AI scribes, customer-support bots, internal copilots, or automation tools where permission and liability boundaries are unclear.

## Practical lane 2 — medical / social care

Potential support artifact: **Care Consent Boundary Card**.

Purpose:

Help care-support notes distinguish between:

- what was observed;
- who said it;
- who reviewed it;
- whether the person consented to reuse;
- whether the note is a draft, reviewed record, or superseded record;
- whether it can be shared with a clinician, caregiver, insurer, school, social worker, or internal system.

This does not diagnose, treat, or replace clinical/social-care judgment. It supports safer documentation and continuity.

## Claim labels

- Claim type: governance design primitive.

- Authority: design proposal, not law, medicine, compliance certification, or clinical guidance.

## Privacy label

No private user data, medical history, client information, or non-public institutional detail is included.

## Missingness label

This primitive has not yet been tested against a live enterprise workflow, clinical workflow, social-care case system, or legal consent regime. Jurisdiction-specific law and organizational policy are missing.

## Revision label

Revise when new AI Act transparency obligations, healthcare AI governance standards, social-care AI guidance, or agentic-commerce authorization protocols materially change the permission landscape.
