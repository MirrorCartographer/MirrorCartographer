# Aesthetic Legibility Boundary

## Attractor

Beauty

## Core claim

A governance artifact is not operationally safe merely because it is technically complete. It must also be legible enough for the humans who inherit it, review it, challenge it, pause it, or act on it.

Beauty, in this repository, does not mean decoration. It means compression that preserves truth under stress.

## Definition

The **Aesthetic Legibility Boundary** is the point where a claim, trace, control, or care record becomes too dense, fragmented, ambiguous, ugly, or unstructured for a responsible human to reliably use.

When an artifact crosses this boundary, its governance state must be downgraded even if its underlying evidence remains valid.

## Invariant

A claim cannot safely govern action if its evidence, limits, authority, and revision path cannot be perceived quickly and accurately by the people responsible for it.

## Relation to existing GitHub Mind primitives

- **Minimum Viable Trace** says what must travel.
- **Trace Sufficiency Test** asks whether the trace is strong enough for the action.
- **Intervention Boundary Matrix** separates documentation, decision support, and intervention authority.
- **Exit Authority** says who can stop continuation.
- **Continuity Review Loop** keeps claims current.
- **Handoff Integrity** prevents orphaned claims.
- **Context Lease** limits permission by scope and time.
- **Claim Portability Test** asks whether a claim can move safely.
- **Aesthetic Legibility Boundary** asks whether the receiving human can actually read, feel, inspect, and use the artifact without distortion.

## Required fields for legibility

Every public-safe governance artifact should expose these fields near the surface:

1. Current claim
2. Evidence maturity
3. Source status
4. Missingness
5. Action ceiling
6. Review trigger
7. Reversal threshold
8. Privacy boundary
9. Owner or accountable role
10. Plain-language human reading

## Failure modes

- Dense artifacts that only their creator can interpret
- Beautiful diagrams with missing source claims
- Long records with no action ceiling
- Medical/care notes that sound authoritative but hide uncertainty
- AI summaries that appear clean while inserting false details
- Governance dashboards that report status but obscure who can intervene
- Public artifacts that accidentally expose private context

## Practical test

Before promotion or handoff, ask:

> Can a tired, responsible stranger understand what this claim means, what it does not mean, what it is allowed to support, what evidence would change it, and who can stop it?

If the answer is no, the artifact has crossed the Aesthetic Legibility Boundary.

## Practical lane 1: income

This supports a sellable Mirror Cartographer adjacent offering:

**AI Governance Legibility Review**

Possible deliverables:

- Convert AI governance records into readable governed traces
- Identify where claims become too dense for safe use
- Redesign evidence summaries, decision records, and review dashboards
- Produce executive-facing and frontline-facing versions of the same trace
- Mark public/private boundaries and missingness explicitly

This is a realistic service because organizations adopting agentic AI increasingly need audit-ready governance that humans can actually operate, not only static policy documents.

## Practical lane 2: medical / social care

This supports documentation quality, continuity, and review without acting as diagnosis or treatment.

A care-adjacent version could help a patient, family, social worker, clinician, or advocate distinguish:

- observation from interpretation,
- quote from summary,
- clinician judgment from AI-generated text,
- uncertainty from conclusion,
- documentation support from intervention authority.

This matters because AI-generated care records can introduce fluent but harmful distortions if humans do not have time, training, or structure to review them.

## Labels

- Source: Multi-source synthesis from current AI governance, healthcare-agent lifecycle, trace assurance, audit accountability, and social-care documentation risk.
- Claim: Legibility is a governance boundary, not an aesthetic afterthought.
- Privacy: Public-safe. No personal medical details, private names, or repository secrets included.
- Missingness: Needs testing against existing GitHub Mind artifacts and a real care-documentation example using fictional or fully de-identified data.
- Revision: Initial proposal.
- Confidence: Medium-high.
- Evidence maturity: Moderate.
- Action authority: Documentation and design-review support only.
- Validation status: Unvalidated repository primitive.
- Reversal threshold: Revise if practical tests show that dense but standardized artifacts are safer than visually/structurally legible artifacts in real handoffs.
