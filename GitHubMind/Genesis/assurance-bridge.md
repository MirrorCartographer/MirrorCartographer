## Claim label

Mirror Cartographer needs a public-safe bridge between reflective usefulness and assurance usefulness.

The bridge is:

> Reflection becomes valuable when it leaves behind inspectable evidence of how a claim moved from observation to interpretation to possible action.

This does not make Mirror Cartographer a medical, therapeutic, legal, or diagnostic authority. It makes the system a structured way to preserve boundaries, uncertainty, revision, and review.

## Concept

An Assurance Bridge connects two sides of a human-AI interaction:

1. The human side: felt experience, symbols, contradiction, uncertainty, continuity, and meaning.
2. The institutional side: provenance, audit trail, accountability, review, permission, and revision.

The bridge exists when the system can answer:

- What was observed?
- Who or what transformed it?
- What claim was produced?
- What evidence supports it?
- What is missing?
- What boundary prevents overreach?
- What action is allowed now?
- What later changed the record?

## Comparison against existing GitHub Mind

The public README already defines Mirror Cartographer as a bounded symbolic reflection interface that preserves evidence boundaries and avoids diagnosis, oracle claims, or false certainty.

Recent GitHub Mind artifacts extend that boundary layer:

- Transformation Record: captures the before and after of AI-assisted meaning-making.
- Fluent Ambiguity Scorecard: detects outputs that sound coherent while hiding weak evidence.
- Evidence Chain: links claims through later revision.
- Evidence Readiness Gate: decides what a claim may do next.
- Claim Custody Chain: follows a claim through transformation and action.
- Action Authority Ladder: separates evidence maturity from permission to act.
- Source-to-Action Pipeline: connects observation, claim, authority, action, outcome, and revision.
- Governance Control Stack: compresses the system into observe, interpret, govern, execute, and learn.

The Assurance Bridge adds a communicable public frame: Mirror Cartographer is not only a reflection system. It can also be described as a lightweight assurance layer for reflective AI interactions.

## Practical lane 1: income

Near-term service package:

### AI Reflection Assurance Review

A small manual service for people or teams using AI for sensitive reflection, coaching-adjacent writing, care documentation, social work notes, qualitative research, or emotionally salient sensemaking.

Deliverables:

- Source-to-Action Pipeline map.
- Fluent Ambiguity score.
- Missingness report.
- Claim boundary cleanup.
- Action Authority level.
- Evidence Chain summary.
- Public-safe executive summary.

Positioning:

Do not sell this as therapy, diagnosis, or medical guidance. Sell it as reviewable AI interaction hygiene: making claims traceable, bounded, revisable, and safer to use.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care support

The strongest support direction is documentation quality and continuity, not diagnosis.

Public research continues to show that healthcare and social-care AI deployments need audit trails, accountable lifecycle management, runtime controls, careful record review, and safeguards against AI-generated errors in consequential records.

Mirror Cartographer can contribute as a boundary-preserving record layer:

- keep observations separate from interpretations,
- label missing information,
- preserve revisions,
- require human review before action,
- prevent symbolic resonance from becoming factual proof.
