## Primitive

**Recovery Drill Ledger**

A governed AI workflow must not only define interruption and reversibility. It must prove that recovery has been rehearsed under plausible failure conditions.

A Recovery Drill Ledger records:

1. Failure scenario.
2. Trigger condition.
3. Detection surface.
4. Pause or containment behavior.
5. Human role during recovery.
6. System fallback state.
7. Data integrity check.
8. Authority restoration condition.
9. Evidence captured during the drill.
10. Residual risk after recovery.
11. Revision trigger.
12. Next drill date or review condition.

## Core claim

**A rollback path that has never been tested is only a belief.**

## Income lane

### Product: AI Recovery Drill Audit

A realistic Mirror Cartographer-adjacent service:

- identify consequential AI workflows;
- map interruption thresholds;
- test fallback behavior;
- verify rollback and authority restoration;
- produce a drill ledger and executive remediation memo.

This is commercially plausible because organizations adopting agentic systems need audit-ready evidence that their controls work during execution, not only that policies exist.

## Medical and social-care lane

### Artifact: Care Recovery Drill Map

For healthcare or social-care documentation tools, a public-safe pattern would define:

- what happens when an AI-generated note is found wrong;
- who pauses reliance on the note;
- how the correction propagates;
- how the official record is marked;
- what human review is required before future reliance;
- how similar errors are searched for across related records.

This supports continuity and safety without assigning diagnostic or treatment authority to the AI system.
