# Assurance Interface Contract

## Attractor

Continuity.

## Core claim

A governance primitive is incomplete until it defines the interface where a human, tool, reviewer, or institution can inspect its evidence, authority, missingness, risk, and revision path.

Mirror Cartographer already separates symbol, hypothesis, evidence, action, and feedback. The next compression is not another label. It is an interface contract: the minimum visible structure required before a claim or workflow can be trusted enough to travel, recur, or support a next step.

## Why this belongs in Genesis

Existing public repository language defines Mirror Cartographer as a bounded symbolic reflection interface, not a therapy product, diagnostic authority, medical tool, oracle, source database, or objective truth engine. The README also states that the system should preserve boundaries between feeling, metaphor, evidence, and reality.

This primitive turns that boundary into an inspectable surface.

## Minimum contract fields

Every public-safe governed artifact should expose:

1. **Claim** — what is being asserted, proposed, observed, or tested.
2. **Source** — where the supporting information came from.
3. **Evidence maturity** — raw observation, weak support, moderate support, strong support, or externally validated.
4. **Authority** — what the artifact is allowed to influence.
5. **Capability** — what the system or workflow can technically do.
6. **Action ceiling** — the strongest action permitted without additional human review.
7. **Privacy boundary** — public-safe, private-user, clinical, legal, financial, or restricted.
8. **Missingness** — what is unknown, unverified, or intentionally excluded.
9. **Revision trigger** — what would require correction, escalation, pause, retirement, or decommissioning.
10. **Reviewer interface** — where a human can inspect, correct, reject, or approve the claim.
11. **Trace link** — the artifact, log, source packet, or execution record that allows reconstruction.
12. **Exit path** — how the claim or workflow is stopped, downgraded, archived, or retired.

## State rule

A claim does not become safer because labels exist. It becomes safer when labels are placed at the point where a responsible human can actually see them before relying on the claim.

## Practical lane 1: income

Near-term service shape: **AI Assurance Interface Review**.

Deliverables:

- artifact inventory;
- claim surface audit;
- missingness visibility test;
- authority and capability separation review;
- action-ceiling review;
- revision-trigger map;
- executive remediation notes.

This is realistic because organizations adopting agentic systems need audit-ready, runtime-visible governance rather than static policy language alone.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care support

Public-safe contribution: **Care Continuity Interface Contract**.

It can structure:

- patient or caregiver observations;
- source-separated summaries;
- clinician-reviewed interpretations;
- missing information;
- revision history;
- next questions for care teams;
- explicit non-diagnostic boundary.

It must not diagnose, prescribe, triage emergencies, replace clinicians, or silently convert drafts into official records.

## Labels

- **Source:** Repository review plus fresh public research.
- **Claim:** Governance artifacts need visible assurance interfaces, not only internal labels.
- **Privacy:** Public-safe. No private user details included.
- **Missingness:** Requires testing against existing docs and future UI implementation.
- **Revision:** Initial Genesis primitive.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high.
- **Evidence maturity:** Moderate external support; implementation untested.
- **Authority:** Documentation and governance-design support only.
- **Action ceiling:** May guide artifact structure; may not authorize medical, legal, financial, or operational decisions without domain review.
- **Validation status:** Proposed; not promoted to Museum.
