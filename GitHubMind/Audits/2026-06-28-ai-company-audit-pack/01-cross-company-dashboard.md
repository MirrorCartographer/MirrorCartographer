## Executive signal

The six-company pattern is clear: the AI industry is moving from chat products toward delegated work systems. That creates a new audit market. Buyers need help understanding what an AI system changed, why it changed, whether the evidence supports the change, what risk remains, and what should not be claimed.

## Cross-company forces

### Force 1 — Agentic delegation

Claim label: INFERENCE
Confidence: HIGH
Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE

The market is moving from answer generation toward delegated tasks: coding, legal drafting, research, tool use, and workflow execution. This makes evaluation harder because risk shifts from wrong text to wrong action.

### Force 2 — Governance compression failure

Claim label: INFERENCE
Confidence: MEDIUM
Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE

Safety frameworks are increasingly public, but they remain hard for normal buyers to interpret. There is a gap between policy language and operational buyer questions: What can this system do? What can it break? Who reviewed it? What evidence exists?

### Force 3 — Enterprise proof hunger

Claim label: HYPOTHESIS
Confidence: MEDIUM
Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE

The next buyer need is not more AI enthusiasm. It is proof packaging: evidence maps, audit logs, risk registers, evaluation traces, and revision memory.

### Force 4 — Professional liability surface

Claim label: INFERENCE
Confidence: HIGH
Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE

Legal, medical, social-care, financial, and engineering workflows create liability because AI output becomes part of real-world decisions. The practical wedge is not diagnosis or final authority; it is better structured observation, review, escalation, and uncertainty labeling.

## Ranking by MC opportunity

1. Cursor / Anysphere — strongest near-term audit product fit because code changes can be logged, tested, diffed, and rolled back.
2. Harvey — high-value enterprise clients already understand audit trails and professional review.
3. Anthropic — strongest governance-drift and policy-to-practice audit fit.
4. OpenAI — strongest ecosystem reach but harder to sell into directly; better as public demonstration target.
5. Continue.dev — best open-source collaboration path.
6. Google DeepMind — high strategic importance but least accessible as a near-term buyer.

## Core product idea emerging from all six

Name: AI Transformation Audit

Definition: A public-safe or enterprise-safe report showing what an AI system changed, what evidence supported the change, what uncertainty remains, what risks were introduced, and what revision path is recommended.
