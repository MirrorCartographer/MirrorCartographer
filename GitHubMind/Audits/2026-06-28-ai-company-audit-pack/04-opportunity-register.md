## Primary commercial wedge

Name: AI Transformation Audit

Definition: A structured report that shows what an AI system changed, what evidence supports the change, what uncertainty remains, what risks were introduced, and what revision path is recommended.

Claim label: HYPOTHESIS
Confidence: HIGH as a service concept; LOW until validated by buyers.

## Why this wedge is stronger than selling full MC immediately

Full Mirror Cartographer is broad, symbolic, and hard to explain quickly. A transformation audit is concrete. It can be sold to developers, law firms, AI startups, creators, healthcare-adjacent organizations, nonprofits, and teams using AI to generate work products.

## Offer ladder

### Offer 1 — Public AI Artifact Audit

Buyer: founders, creators, researchers, job applicants, grant applicants.
Price hypothesis: low-ticket to mid-ticket.
Deliverable: 5–10 page report.
Inputs: one AI-generated artifact, prompt history if available, target audience, claims to verify.
Output: claim map, unsupported claims, evidence labels, revision plan, public-safe version.

### Offer 2 — AI Code Change Audit

Buyer: software teams using Cursor, Continue.dev, Copilot-like tools, internal coding agents.
Price hypothesis: mid-ticket per repo or pull request batch.
Deliverable: PR-level audit card.
Inputs: diff, tests, README, issue/ticket, agent transcript if available.
Output: changed files, intent match, risk level, test adequacy, rollback notes, reviewer checklist.

### Offer 3 — Legal / Professional Document Audit

Buyer: solo lawyers, legal ops, compliance teams, consultants.
Price hypothesis: higher-ticket due to professional liability.
Deliverable: document provenance and review trace.
Inputs: AI-generated memo, contract, policy, citation set.
Output: claim-by-claim evidence map, missing citations, jurisdiction flags, confidence labels, review queue.
Non-claim: Not legal advice.

### Offer 4 — Safety / Governance Framework Translation

Buyer: AI startups, nonprofits, advocacy groups, journalists, policy analysts.
Price hypothesis: project-based.
Deliverable: framework readability and enforceability report.
Inputs: public policy/safety document.
Output: commitments, loopholes, allowed actions, missing thresholds, revision history.

### Offer 5 — Medical / Social-Care Communication Pack

Buyer: patients, caregivers, clinics, social workers, disability advocates.
Price hypothesis: low-ticket / grant-funded / nonprofit channel.
Deliverable: structured observation summary.
Inputs: timeline, symptoms/events, appointments, medications, questions.
Output: clinician-facing brief, uncertainty labels, escalation questions, longitudinal tracker.
Non-claim: Not diagnosis, not treatment plan.

## Buyer pain map

| Pain | Buyer says | MC answer |
|---|---|---|
| AI output is impressive but untrusted | “Can I rely on this?” | Evidence and uncertainty map |
| AI changed too much too fast | “What actually changed?” | Transformation log |
| AI claims are hard to verify | “Where did this come from?” | Source-to-claim ledger |
| Team members do not review consistently | “How do we standardize review?” | Audit rubric |
| High-stakes users need clarity | “What do I ask next?” | Structured communication pack |

## Fastest validation path

1. Create three sample audits from public artifacts.
2. Package them as before/after PDFs or repo pages.
3. Offer fixed-price audits to developers, creators, and small organizations.
4. Track whether buyers pay for clarity, risk reduction, or presentation polish.
5. Convert repeated audit steps into templates/software.

## Income ranking

1. AI Code Change Audit — easiest to prove with diffs/tests.
2. Legal / Professional Document Audit — highest willingness to pay but stricter boundary language.
3. Public AI Artifact Audit — easiest to sell quickly to individuals.
4. Governance Framework Translation — strong thought-leadership value.
5. Medical/Social-Care Communication Pack — meaningful but must be careful, evidence-based, and likely lower-margin unless grant-supported.

## What not to sell yet

- Do not sell MC as therapy.
- Do not sell MC as medical diagnosis.
- Do not sell MC as a replacement lawyer, clinician, engineer, or safety team.
- Do not claim internal audits of companies without authorization.
- Do not imply private access when using public evidence.
