## New ruler set

### 1. Transformation Density

Definition: Number of meaningful state changes per artifact, task, or session.

Use: Identifies whether AI merely rephrased content or materially changed decisions, claims, structure, risk, or future action.

### 2. Claim Drift

Definition: Distance between source evidence and final stated claim.

Use: Flags when fluent output has moved beyond support.

### 3. Evidence Entropy

Definition: Degree of scatter, weakness, conflict, or incompleteness in the evidence supporting a conclusion.

Use: Helps reviewers separate strong synthesis from attractive uncertainty.

### 4. Revision Velocity

Definition: Rate at which contradictions, missingness, and failed assumptions are converted into better structure.

Use: Measures learning behavior rather than surface polish.

### 5. Orientation Gain

Definition: Increase in a human user's ability to understand what happened, what matters, what is uncertain, and what action is safe next.

Use: Directly measures whether the system helps humans think and act better.

### 6. Action Risk Delta

Definition: Difference in downstream risk before and after the AI-mediated transformation.

Use: Determines whether the artifact made the next action safer, unchanged, or riskier.

## Validation plan

Test:
- Collect 20 AI-generated artifacts.
- Audit all 20 with MC transformation cards.
- Have independent human reviewers mark unsupported claims, missing context, risks, and useful revisions.
- Compare MC against a normal AI summary.

Success criteria:
- Finds more unsupported claims.
- Identifies more missing context.
- Reduces reviewer time.
- Produces more actionable revision paths.
- Avoids overclaiming.
