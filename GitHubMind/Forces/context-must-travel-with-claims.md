# Force: Context Must Travel With Claims

## Status

Forces artifact. Public-safe. Initial version.

## Attractor

Contradiction.

## Force statement

Context must travel with claims.

If a claim moves but its source, uncertainty, authority, allowed use, and review threshold do not move with it, the receiving context may treat a bounded statement as stronger than it is.

## Pressure

AI-assisted systems make it easy to generate useful claims quickly. They also make it easy to detach those claims from the conditions that made them safe.

The danger is not only false content. The danger is true-or-plausible content with missing governance context.

## Direction of pressure

This force pushes Mirror Cartographer toward:

- shorter claim objects,
- stronger transfer labels,
- visible authority boundaries,
- explicit allowed and disallowed use,
- review triggers attached to claims,
- accountability holders that do not disappear during transfer,
- public-safe documentation patterns.

## Interaction with prior forces

- Review is a control surface: handoffs must preserve review triggers.
- Continuation must be earned: handoffs must preserve exit authority.
- Intervention authority must be earned: handoffs must not smuggle documentation support into action authority.
- Thresholds turn governance into control: handoffs must preserve advance, pause, escalation, and retirement thresholds.

## Income implication

This force supports a practical offer:

AI Claim Handoff Audit.

The buyer pain is simple: teams using AI do not always know which parts of a document are sourced, inferred, transformed, uncertain, allowed for downstream use, or ready for decision support.

Mirror Cartographer can provide a visible map of those boundaries.

## Support implication

For care-adjacent and social-support contexts, the force says:

Do not let an AI-assisted interpretation travel as an unlabeled fact.

Preserve the difference between observation, user report, professional record, symbolic reflection, hypothesis, and action.
