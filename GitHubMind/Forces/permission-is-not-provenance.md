# Force — Permission Is Not Provenance

## Force statement

**Permission is not provenance.**

A system can know where a claim came from and still not know whether that claim is allowed to act, travel, persist, or enter a record.

## Why this matters

The GitHub Mind has built a strong chain around source, claim, missingness, revision, verification, recovery, and continuity. That chain protects truth boundaries. It does not automatically protect permission boundaries.

Permission is a different force:

- provenance asks: where did this come from?
- verification asks: can this be inspected?
- confidence asks: how strong is this?
- recovery asks: can this be repaired?
- authority asks: who can decide?
- permission asks: what is allowed here, now, for this actor, under this consent boundary?

## Failure pattern

A claim becomes dangerous when it crosses any of these lines without an authority envelope:

- draft note becomes official record;
- user reflection becomes medical inference;
- care summary becomes assessment;
- AI suggestion becomes instruction;
- internal analysis becomes public claim;
- personal context becomes reusable memory;
- agent recommendation becomes purchase, message, database write, or workflow action.

## Design implication

Every mobile claim needs two containers:

1. **Claim Card** — what the claim says, where it came from, how strong it is, what is missing, and how it can be corrected.
2. **Consent Authority Envelope** — what the claim is allowed to do, who authorized that action, when that permission expires, and how it can be revoked.

## Practical lane 1 — income force

The marketable force is not abstract governance. It is concrete friction removal for organizations afraid of AI overreach:

**Show me where my AI is acting without a permission boundary.**

Deliverable shape:

- permission map;
- forbidden-use list;
- authority matrix;
- consent and revocation surface;
- action-ceiling table;
- reuse-risk register;
- remediation sequence.

## Practical lane 2 — care/social-care force

The care-support force is record humility:

A care-support note must show whether it is:

- a person statement;
- a caregiver observation;
- an AI draft;
- a clinician/social-worker reviewed record;
- an inference needing review;
- a superseded or corrected version.

This supports continuity without pretending to be diagnosis, treatment, or legal case determination.

## Source label

Grounded in current public research and reporting on agentic AI governance, healthcare agent lifecycle controls, trace-based assurance, graduated human oversight, healthcare zero-trust architectures, social-care AI summary errors, content provenance, and agentic commerce authorization risks.

## Claim label

Governance design claim. Moderate confidence. Not a legal rule, compliance certification, medical directive, or implementation proof.

## Privacy label

Public-safe. No private user, patient, client, animal, or institutional data.

## Missingness label

The force still needs examples, UI patterns, test cases, and domain-specific permission matrices.

## Revision label

Revise when the GitHub Mind adds tested consent-envelope examples, live UI implementation, or jurisdiction-specific care/social-care authorization guidance.
