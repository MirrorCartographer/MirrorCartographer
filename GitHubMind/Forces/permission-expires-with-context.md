# Force — Permission Expires With Context

## Core force
Permission is not a permanent property of a claim.

Permission depends on context.

When context changes, the permission may expire even if the claim text remains unchanged.

## Pressure pattern
AI-assisted work often fails by carrying a statement farther than its original authority allowed.

Examples of unsafe movement:

- reflection becomes explanation,
- explanation becomes evidence,
- evidence becomes recommendation,
- recommendation becomes action,
- draft becomes record,
- record becomes decision input,
- temporary permission becomes standing permission,
- read-only support becomes workflow authority.

## Governance rule
A claim must not travel without its Context Lease.

If the lease is missing, expired, or out of scope, the claim falls back to its safest state: observation or draft support only.

## MC design implication
Mirror Cartographer should preserve visible boundaries around claims, especially when content moves between modes, sessions, exports, care notes, evaluation reports, or income-facing artifacts.
