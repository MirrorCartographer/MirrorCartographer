# Force — Unfinished Duty Is a System Risk

**Attractor:** Continuity  
**Status:** Public-safe force artifact  
**Revision:** v1.0

## Force statement

**A system that records actions but not remaining obligations becomes falsely complete.**

The dangerous failure is not always the wrong decision. Sometimes it is the correct decision that creates a follow-up duty no one owns.

## Tension

Existing primitives can answer:

- What did we believe?
- Who permitted it?
- Why was it chosen?
- What happened?

They cannot fully answer:

- What remains unresolved?
- Who owns the next duty?
- What evidence is required to close the loop?
- What must be transferred if the original actor disappears?
- What obligation survives deletion, export, summary, or handoff?

## Risk pattern

1. A claim is generated.
2. A decision relies on it.
3. An action occurs.
4. A partial follow-up is implied.
5. The interface displays completion.
6. The unresolved duty disappears from view.

This is a continuity failure.

## Counterforce

The **Obligation Ledger** forces unresolved duty into a visible object.

It makes incompletion operational instead of atmospheric.

## Practical interpretation

For AI systems, this force appears as:

- unowned escalation;
- missing correction pathway;
- permission carried beyond its original scope;
- unclosed rollback duty;
- unresolved provenance after reuse;
- generated summaries that remove open loops.

For care systems, this force appears as:

- referral not followed;
- symptom log not reviewed;
- medication question not answered;
- caregiver concern summarized away;
- record correction not carried forward;
- discharge or handoff leaving no accountable next actor.

## Design law

**No consequential action should close without an obligation check.**

The check asks:

1. Did this create a duty?
2. Who owns it?
3. What evidence closes it?
4. What happens if it is not closed?
5. What must be preserved if the artifact is reused?
