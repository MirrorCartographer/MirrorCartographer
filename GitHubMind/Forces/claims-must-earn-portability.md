# Force: Claims Must Earn Portability

## Status
Forces artifact. Public-safe.

## Attractor
Discovery.

## Force statement
**Claims must earn portability.**

A claim is not automatically safe to reuse just because it was useful, accurate, or authorized in its original context.

## Why this force exists
AI-assisted systems increasingly create outputs that travel:

- from chat to document,
- from note to report,
- from report to decision,
- from one worker to another,
- from one system to another,
- from documentation to action,
- from private reflection to public artifact,
- from administrative summary to clinical or social-care record.

Each crossing can strip away the claim's source, uncertainty, missingness, purpose, authority, privacy boundary, and reversal criteria.

## Pressure created by the force
This force pushes the repository toward portable governance labels:

- source must travel,
- claim state must travel,
- uncertainty must travel,
- action ceiling must travel,
- Context Lease must travel,
- exit authority must travel,
- revision triggers must travel,
- privacy label must travel.

## Counterforce
The opposing force is convenience: people and systems prefer clean summaries, short reports, and confident language. That pressure makes claims easier to move but easier to misuse.

## MC response
The Claim Portability Test functions as a crossing gate. It asks whether a claim is still allowed to be used after the context changes.

## Practical lane: income
This force supports a concrete consulting offer: review where AI-generated claims enter documents, dashboards, tickets, care records, audits, marketing materials, or operational workflows without carrying their governance state.

## Practical lane: medical and social care
This force is especially relevant to care-adjacent documentation because downstream readers may treat an AI-assisted summary as if it were verified observation. Portability controls protect against that category error.

## Labels

- Source: Synthesis from current AI governance and documentation-risk literature.
- Claim: Claims must earn portability before reuse across context, authority, or action boundaries.
- Privacy: Public-safe. No private user details included.
- Missingness: Needs testing against repository artifacts and real handoff workflows.
- Revision: Initial Forces artifact.
- Confidence: Medium-high.
- Evidence maturity: Conceptual synthesis with current external support.
- Action authority: Governance-design support only.
