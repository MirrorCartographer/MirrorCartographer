# Force — Claims Need Portable Boundaries

## Status

Public-safe force note.

## Attractor

Compression.

## Force statement

**A claim that moves without its boundary becomes a new risk object.**

## Pressure

The GitHub Mind has repeatedly identified that AI-assisted outputs do not remain still. They are copied, summarized, exported, translated, polished, converted into plans, embedded into records, and reused by people who did not see the original context.

When this happens, the claim may keep its words while losing:

- source;
- uncertainty;
- missingness;
- privacy boundary;
- authority boundary;
- revision status;
- correction path;
- action ceiling.

This creates a force problem: the claim keeps influence while losing containment.

## Why this matters

A polished sentence can feel more certain than the evidence behind it. A summary can sound complete while hiding missingness. A care-adjacent note can appear official before review. A governance document can cite a control without showing whether that control is operational.

The boundary must therefore move with the claim, not remain in a separate document that the next actor may never inspect.

## Operational rule

Any claim that may influence action should carry a minimum portable boundary.

That boundary is implemented through the Claim Card Schema.

## Relation to existing primitives

- Provenance Carry-Forward Ledger: did the labels survive movement?
- Verification Surface Protocol: can the next actor inspect the labels where reliance occurs?
- Claim Card Schema: what minimum portable object carries those labels?
- Layer Drift Sentinel: did a new explanation layer change the claim?
- Reversibility Anchor: how can a claim be corrected or rolled back after reliance?

## Failure mode

A claim becomes unsafe when:

- it is detached from source;
- missingness is removed;
- uncertainty is polished away;
- authority boundary is implied rather than stated;
- a summary is treated as a reviewed record;
- a derivative artifact hides that it is derivative;
- the correction path is absent.

## Practical lane 1: income

Commercial force:

Many organizations do not need a philosophical essay about responsible AI. They need to know which claims in their AI-assisted outputs can safely travel to clients, leadership, auditors, users, or regulators.

Offering:

**Portable Claim Boundary Audit**

Core output:

A map of high-impact claims and the minimum Claim Cards required before those claims can be reused.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

Care force:

Medical and social-care systems already depend on summaries, handoffs, referrals, notes, and administrative records. AI-assisted summaries can help reduce burden, but they can also insert or distort claims.

Care-support boundary:

Any care-related claim should show whether it is:

- patient-reported;
- caregiver-observed;
- clinician-reviewed;
- system-generated;
- administratively inferred;
- corrected;
- superseded;
- unverified.

## Source labels

- Healthcare agent lifecycle governance literature supports operational control planes, audit logging, runtime policy, and decommissioning.
- Runtime guardrail literature supports translating governance goals into enforceable controls and assurance evidence.
- Agentic assurance literature supports provenance, dynamic authorization, interruptibility, and audit controls.
- Trace-based assurance literature supports message-action traces and governance metrics.
- Social-care AI reporting shows that AI-generated summaries can introduce harmful record errors.

## Claim labels

- Claim: claim boundaries must become portable when claims move between contexts.
- Confidence: high.
- Evidence maturity: emerging but convergent.
- Authority: design force, not legal, clinical, compliance, financial, or regulatory authority.

## Privacy label

Public-safe. No private health, household, client, patient, financial, or personal records included.

## Missingness label

This force note does not define a technical transport layer, validation engine, or cryptographic signature scheme. It names the containment pressure and links it to the Claim Card Schema.

## Revision label

Revise if:

- repository-wide claim IDs are created;
- automated export cards are implemented;
- care-support review states are formalized;
- regulatory provenance display requirements become more specific;
- a stronger portable-boundary primitive replaces the Claim Card Schema.
