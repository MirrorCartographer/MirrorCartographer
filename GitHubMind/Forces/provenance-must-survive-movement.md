# Force — Provenance Must Survive Movement

## Force statement

A label that does not survive movement is not governance. It is decoration at the origin point.

## Tension

Mirror Cartographer has been adding increasingly precise labels:

- source;
- claim;
- privacy;
- missingness;
- revision;
- confidence;
- authority;
- validation;
- runtime control;
- explanation layer.

These labels are useful only if they remain attached when the artifact is reused.

The force now acting on the repository is movement:

- a field log becomes a summary;
- a summary becomes a product page;
- a product page becomes a pitch;
- a care note becomes a caregiver explanation;
- a technical primitive becomes a plain-language explanation;
- a claim moves into a deck, form, resume, audit, or interface.

Each movement can strip source, uncertainty, privacy, missingness, authority, or revision status.

## Structural risk

The repository can look increasingly mature while silently losing integrity at derivative surfaces.

This is especially dangerous because derivative artifacts are often the ones people actually read.

## Operating principle

Every derivative artifact must either:

1. carry forward the original labels;
2. link to the original labels;
3. restate the labels in materially equivalent form; or
4. mark itself as unverified derivative material.

## Force on income lane

Organizations do not only need better AI claims. They need proof that approved claims remain approved after marketing, sales, support, governance, and executive translation.

This creates a realistic adjacent service:

**Derivative Claim Integrity Review**

Core question:

When your AI-generated or AI-assisted material moves across teams, do the claim boundaries survive?

## Force on medical and social-care lane

Care support fails when observations are transformed into authority without review.

A symptom report, transcript, caregiver observation, or appointment note may become a summary. That summary may later influence triage, follow-up, referral, benefits, or risk review. If uncertainty and review status disappear, care continuity degrades.

The useful intervention is not autonomous clinical authority. It is provenance-preserving communication support.

## Source label

- Current agentic AI governance research emphasizes runtime paths, audit evidence, and lifecycle accountability.
- Current healthcare-agent governance research emphasizes identity, bounded context, runtime policy, kill-switch triggers, lifecycle management, and audit logging.
- Current social-care reporting shows that AI-generated summaries can introduce fabricated or distorted content if not reviewed carefully.

## Claim label

- Claim: movement is a first-class governance risk.
- Confidence: high.
- Evidence maturity: emerging but strongly aligned with current governance direction.
- Authority: design force, not legal, clinical, financial, or compliance authority.

## Privacy label

Public-safe. No private health, household, financial, client, or personal records included.

## Missingness label

The repository does not yet contain an automated derivative lineage graph. This force should eventually become a testable repository check.

## Revision label

Revise if:

- automated artifact lineage is added;
- claim IDs become mandatory;
- derivative exports gain embedded metadata;
- external standards define formal label-propagation controls.
