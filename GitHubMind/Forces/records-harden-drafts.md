# Force — Records Harden Drafts

## Status

Public-safe force artifact.

## Claim

**Records harden drafts.**

An AI-generated draft is relatively easy to question while it is visibly a draft. Once it becomes an official record, it gains institutional gravity. Future readers may treat it as observed fact, even if it began as generated synthesis.

## Force description

The danger is not only hallucination. The danger is record hardening:

- generated language becomes official language,
- official language becomes future evidence,
- future evidence shapes decisions,
- decisions become outcomes,
- outcomes become harder to reverse.

## Governance implication

The point of control must occur before record hardening, not only after a bad record is discovered.

## Mirror Cartographer implication

Mirror Cartographer should treat every AI-assisted care, medical, animal-health, legal, social-service, or income-related artifact as non-authoritative until its record status is labeled.

Minimum labels:

- Draft
- Human-checked draft
- Verified record
- Action-bounded record
- Revised record
- Retired record

## Relation to previous forces

- **Permission does not compose:** documentation steps can compose into unauthorized action.
- **Permission expires with context:** a record may lose validity as conditions change.
- **Context must travel with claims:** record text must carry source and governance state.
- **Capability and authority are independent:** drafting capability is not record authority.
- **Legibility is a safety surface:** the record label must be visible at the point of use.

## Labels
