## Claim

Continuity is only a virtue when the thing being continued remains bounded, sourced, authorized, and fit for reliance. Otherwise continuity becomes error-preservation.

## Force statement

The GitHub Mind has been building a chain of custody for thought:

Observation becomes claim.
Claim becomes decision.
Decision becomes action.
Action becomes obligation.
Obligation becomes review.
Review becomes revision.
Revision enters Museum.

That chain now needs an interruptive force: the right to stop the chain when uncertainty, authority drift, consent drift, summary risk, or action risk rises above a threshold.

## Operational implication

A Review Trigger should be attached before reliance when any of the following are true:

- the artifact may influence medical, social-care, legal, financial, safety, or external-action decisions;
- the evidence is weak, missing, stale, or contradicted;
- the summary compresses away caveats;
- a non-authoritative artifact begins to sound authoritative;
- a private observation could leak into public-safe space;
- a tool action could create external effects.

## Mirror Cartographer implication

Mirror Cartographer should not only help users map meaning. It should help preserve the difference between:

- symbol and evidence;
- felt truth and external claim;
- observation and diagnosis;
- reflection and instruction;
- support and authority;
- possibility and action.

This distinction is commercially valuable because organizations adopting AI need practical boundaries, not abstract assurances.

## Care implication

A care-support workflow should treat the Review Trigger as a protective pause. It does not erase user observations. It keeps them from being mistaken for clinician-reviewed findings, verified diagnoses, or safe treatment instructions.
