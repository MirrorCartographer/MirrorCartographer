# Force — Explanation Can Change Force

## Status labels

- Source: public research/news synthesis plus repository comparison.
- Claim: explanation layers can alter the force of a claim even when the surface words appear compatible.
- Privacy: public-safe.
- Missingness: missing continuity around the previously reported explanatory-layer artifact increases the need for explicit drift detection.
- Revision: revise when repository claim IDs and explanation layers become machine-checkable.
- Confidence: medium-high.
- Evidence maturity: conceptual primitive grounded in current governance and care-documentation risks.

## Force statement

**An explanation is not neutral. It changes force.**

A claim can be technically bounded in one layer and practically dangerous in another.

Example pattern:

- Kernel layer: `Observation only; insufficient evidence for diagnosis.`
- Human layer: `This may help explain what is happening.`
- Executive layer: `System identifies likely cause.`
- Care layer: `Possible diagnosis.`

The executive and care layers have changed the force of the claim.

## Why this matters

Mirror Cartographer relies on symbolic, emotional, operational, scientific, and practical translations. That is one of its strengths. It lets different users approach complexity through different doors.

But every door must open into the same room.

If one layer becomes more certain, more authoritative, more actionable, or more private than the underlying claim allows, the explanation is no longer a translation. It is an escalation.

## Pressure points

Layer drift is most likely when:

1. A public artifact is made more compelling for attention.
2. A technical claim is converted into a sales page.
3. A care note is shortened for convenience.
4. A reviewer receives only an executive summary.
5. A symbolic interpretation is reused as factual evidence.
6. A missingness label is removed because it feels awkward.
7. A claim is exported outside its original context.

## Counterforce

The Layer Drift Sentinel adds friction exactly where persuasion, compression, or convenience would otherwise outrun evidence.

Required counterforce:

- same claim ID across layers;
- same authority boundary;
- same evidence maturity;
- same action ceiling;
- same revision trigger;
- visible missingness;
- drift result before export.

## Practical lane 1: income

Force-aware documentation review can be sold as a narrow, realistic service:

**AI Claim Consistency Review**

The buyer receives a table showing where product copy, internal governance docs, user help text, risk reports, and executive summaries diverge.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care support

Force-aware care summaries can reduce communication harm by keeping observation, inference, concern, recommendation, and diagnosis separate.

Mirror Cartographer should never turn a symptom story into a diagnosis. It can help preserve what was observed, what is uncertain, what needs review, and what should be brought to a qualified professional.

## Revision label

This force should be superseded only if a stronger primitive handles semantic equivalence, audience translation, and action-boundary preservation across all public artifacts.
