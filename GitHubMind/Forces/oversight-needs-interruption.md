# Force — Oversight Needs Interruption

## Force statement

Oversight without interruption is theater.

A human reviewer, clinician, operator, manager, or institution cannot meaningfully supervise an AI-assisted workflow if the system never defines the condition that pauses movement and routes the artifact to review.

## Pressure

As AI systems become more agentic, risk increasingly appears during execution:

- a tool receives broader access than intended;
- a summary becomes a record;
- a weak claim is reused in a stronger context;
- a user correction fails to propagate;
- a recommendation crosses into a medical, legal, financial, or operational action;
- a delegated authority persists past its context lease;
- telemetry shows anomaly but no pause path exists;
- humans are named as overseers but only after the system has already acted.

## Counterforce

Organizations often prefer broad policy statements because they are easier to write than interruption logic. But broad policies do not decide when to stop.

## Repository pressure point

Mirror Cartographer already has public-safe primitives for assurance, delegation, portability, authority, draft-to-record conversion, and reversibility.

This force adds the missing pressure:

A claim or workflow must expose its interruption threshold before it crosses an action boundary.

## Operational implication

Every high-impact workflow should answer:

1. What makes this unsafe to continue?
2. Who or what detects that condition?
3. What stops immediately?
4. Who reviews it?
5. What is shown to the user while paused?
6. What evidence releases, downgrades, or retires it?
7. Where is that decision logged?

## Practical lane 1: income

This force supports a concrete consulting offer:

**AI Interruption Threshold Audit**

Useful for teams that have AI workflows but lack:

- pause triggers;
- approval gates;
- incident thresholds;
- release criteria;
- reviewer routing;
- rollback documentation.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

This force supports:

**Care Pause Trigger Map**

Care-related AI should not merely say "ask a clinician." It should preserve a structured pause condition: what observation, contradiction, missing source, privacy boundary, or escalation signal requires human review before the note or plan travels further.
