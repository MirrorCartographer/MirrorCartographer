## Claim

Readiness is not a label. Readiness is a proved operating condition.

## Force statement

**A workflow is not ready because it is documented. It is ready only when its controls have evidence.**

## Pressure

AI systems increasingly produce effects beyond text:

- tool calls;
- record changes;
- emails or messages;
- care summaries;
- routing decisions;
- recommendations;
- intake classifications;
- delegated actions.

When effects cross into the world, static assurance becomes too weak. The system needs proof that its governance primitives can operate during use.

## Tension

Documentation says: "This should be safe."

Deployment proof asks: "Show where the safety behavior happened."

## Repository consequence

Every major GitHub Mind primitive should eventually be testable:

- Capability vs. Authority: prove the system cannot exceed authorized scope.
- Delegation Lineage Ledger: prove the authority path is reconstructable.
- Assurance Interface Contract: prove the user sees the right labels at the reliance point.
- Interruption Threshold Register: prove the system pauses when a trigger appears.
- Recovery Drill Ledger: prove rollback has been rehearsed.
- Reversibility Anchor: prove correction can propagate.
- Failover Custody Protocol: prove responsibility survives interruption.
- Governance State Machine: prove state transitions are explicit and lawful.

## Medical / social-care implication

Care-facing AI support should never be promoted as treatment authority. Its safer contribution is making observations more durable, reviewable, and correctable.

A care support artifact should not say:

"This is what is wrong."

It should say:

"Here is what was observed, what is missing, who reviewed it, what it may affect, what requires clinician review, and how to correct it."
