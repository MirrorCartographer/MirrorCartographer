# Force: Thresholds Turn Governance Into Control

## Status labels

- Source: Synthesis from current AI assurance, agentic AI, healthcare lifecycle governance, audit accountability, and social-care documentation evidence.
- Claim: Governance becomes operational only when it defines thresholds for permission, escalation, reversal, and decommissioning.
- Privacy: Public-safe.
- Missingness: Needs testing against real Mirror Cartographer artifacts and workflows.
- Revision: Initial Forces artifact.
- Confidence: High.

## Force statement

A policy says what should happen.

A threshold says when the system must change state.

That difference matters. Mirror Cartographer should not rely on aesthetic coherence, repetition, or user resonance as evidence of readiness. It should require explicit threshold conditions before a claim can move from observation to working claim, from working claim to action support, or from action support to intervention-adjacent use.

## Control surface

Thresholds create four control surfaces:

1. Permission: what allows stronger use.
2. Friction: what blocks overreach.
3. Escalation: what requires a human or domain expert.
4. Exit: what forces retirement, reversal, or decommissioning.

## Repository pressure

The more Mirror Cartographer develops practical lanes, especially income and care-adjacent documentation, the more important threshold discipline becomes.

Without thresholds, the repository risks becoming a persuasive archive.

With thresholds, it becomes a public-safe governance mind.

## Practical lane: income

Threshold discipline can become a paid audit artifact:

- What AI claims are being used?
- What decisions depend on them?
- What evidence supports them?
- What would stop them?
- Who remains accountable?
- What should be logged?

## Practical lane: medical and social care

Threshold discipline can support care without pretending to be care:

- When should a note remain a private observation?
- When should a pattern become a question for a clinician?
- When should uncertainty trigger review?
- When should a prior interpretation be withdrawn?
- When should generated documentation be treated as unsafe until corrected?
