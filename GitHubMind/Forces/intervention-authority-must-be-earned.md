## Force statement

Intervention authority must be earned separately from claim clarity.

A claim can be coherent, sourced, emotionally resonant, and useful for reflection while still being unauthorized for diagnosis, treatment, denial, escalation, payment, legal action, or social-care decisions.

## Why this force matters

Mirror Cartographer is strongest when it preserves continuity and makes reasoning inspectable. It becomes unsafe if inspectable reasoning is mistaken for licensed authority.

The living workspace should therefore ask two different questions every time:

1. Is the claim justified?
2. What is the claim allowed to do?

## Boundary tests

A claim should not move into action unless the trace answers:

- What is the source?
- What was transformed?
- What is missing?
- What would reverse the claim?
- Who reviewed it?
- Who remains accountable?
- What action is being proposed?
- What harm could follow if it is wrong?
- What professional or institutional authority is required?
