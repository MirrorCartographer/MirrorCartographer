# Force — Reliance Requires a Repair Path

## Force statement

A claim that can influence action must have a visible repair path.

## Why this force matters

AI-supported work often fails quietly at the boundary between draft, summary, memory, record, and action. A claim may begin as a cautious hypothesis, then become copied into a plan, sent to another person, summarized into a record, or used as the premise for a decision.

If the original uncertainty, source, missingness, and correction channel disappear, the claim becomes harder to repair than it was to create.

## Pressure pattern

The system is pressured by:

- fast generation;
- multi-step agent workflows;
- tool use;
- exported summaries;
- stale context;
- human over-trust;
- record hardening;
- memory persistence;
- delegated authority;
- weak decommissioning.

## Counterforce

The counterforce is **reversibility by design**.

Every artifact that could travel should preserve:

- who or what generated it;
- what evidence supported it;
- what was missing;
- what it was allowed to influence;
- where a correction can be entered;
- where the correction propagates;
- what cannot be undone.

## Practical lane 1: income

Commercial version:

**AI Reversibility Audit**

This force can become a paid review for organizations that use AI-generated summaries, agents, documentation systems, or decision-support workflows.

Audit questions:

- Where do AI outputs become records?
- Where do records become actions?
- Who can correct the output?
- What happens when a source is wrong?
- Can a bad claim be traced across exports?
- Can the workflow downgrade a claim instead of only adding more notes?

## Practical lane 2: medical/social-care

Care-support version:

**Care Record Correction Map**

This helps separate:

- raw observations;
- AI-assisted summaries;
- clinician-reviewed interpretations;
- caregiver concerns;
- disputed claims;
- corrected claims;
- superseded notes.

It protects continuity without claiming medical authority.

## Labels

- **Source:** Repository comparison and fresh public research on agent governance, runtime guardrails, healthcare lifecycle controls, and trace assurance.
- **Claim:** Reliance requires a visible correction and rollback path.
- **Privacy:** Public-safe.
- **Missingness:** Needs testing against real export, record, and handoff workflows.
- **Revision:** Initial Force artifact.
- **Confidence:** Medium-high.
- **Evidence maturity:** Moderate.
- **Authority:** Conceptual governance design only.
- **Action ceiling:** May guide documentation and review structure; cannot alter official records or authorize regulated decisions.
- **Validation status:** Proposed.
