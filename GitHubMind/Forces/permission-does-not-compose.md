# Force — Permission Does Not Compose

## Status labels

- Source: Fresh public research synthesis, 2026-06-30
- Claim: In agentic or AI-assisted workflows, individually permitted actions cannot be assumed to create an authorized sequence.
- Privacy: Public-safe.
- Missingness: Needs concrete examples from Mirror Cartographer artifacts and adjacent consulting workflows.
- Revision: Initial force artifact.
- Confidence: High.

## Force statement

**Permission does not compose.**

A system may be allowed to read.
A system may be allowed to summarize.
A system may be allowed to draft.
A system may be allowed to recommend.
A system may be allowed to update a record.

That does not mean the combined read → summarize → draft → recommend → update workflow has been authorized.

## Why this force matters

Sequence risk is easy to miss because each step can appear reasonable inside its own local frame. The danger appears only when the steps are viewed as a chain.

This is especially important for:

- AI agents with tools,
- care documentation,
- social-care summaries,
- job or income workflows,
- public artifacts,
- governance reports,
- systems that move claims between private reflection and public evidence.

## Mirror Cartographer implication

Mirror Cartographer should preserve not only the state of a claim, but also the chain of actions that claim has enabled.

A claim's governance state must travel with the claim, and the sequence must be reviewed when the claim crosses into a new use context.

## Operating question

Before advancing a workflow, ask:

> Did the whole sequence receive authority, or only the individual steps?

## Source anchors

- Runtime governance research identifies execution-time intervention as necessary when risks emerge during agent trajectories.
- Production-agent governance research describes sequences of individually permitted actions transforming business processes without authorization.
- Healthcare lifecycle governance research emphasizes kill-switch triggers, credential revocation, audit logging, and decommissioning for agent fleets.
- Audit guidance reinforces that accountability remains with human or institutional actors using AI; responsibility cannot be transferred to the AI system.
