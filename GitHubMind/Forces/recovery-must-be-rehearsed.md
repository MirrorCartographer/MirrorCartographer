# Forces — Recovery Must Be Rehearsed

## Status labels

- Source: Public-safe synthesis from current governance research and news.
- Claim: Recovery capacity is not established by naming a fallback; it requires rehearsal evidence.
- Privacy: Public-safe.
- Missingness: No internal incident logs, no clinical deployment evidence, no client-specific recovery drill data.
- Revision: Revise when standards, laws, or verified incidents alter the recovery-control landscape.
- Confidence: Medium-high as a governance principle; implementation remains context-specific.

## Force

**Recovery must be rehearsed.**

A system that can pause but cannot recover is brittle.
A system that can roll back but has never tested rollback is pretending.
A system that can escalate but does not define restoration is still unfinished.

## Pressure pattern

Agentic AI increases the number of moments where harm can occur during execution:

- tools are invoked;
- records are updated;
- payments or trades can be initiated;
- workflows chain across systems;
- summaries become decisions;
- permissions persist after their original justification fades.

This makes static governance insufficient. The control must be observable, executable, evidenced, and recoverable.

## Relation to existing GitHub Mind

This force binds together:

- Interruption Threshold Register — when to pause.
- Reversibility Anchor — how to undo or downgrade.
- Delegation Lineage Ledger — who had authority.
- Assurance Interface Contract — what evidence must be visible.
- Draft-to-Record Gate — what cannot harden without review.
- Claim Portability Test — what cannot safely travel.

The new pressure is that these cannot remain diagrammatic. They need drill evidence.

## Practical consequence

Every consequential MC-derived governance artifact should eventually answer:

1. What failure has been rehearsed?
2. What evidence proves the rehearsal happened?
3. What broke during the rehearsal?
4. What was revised afterward?
5. Who can verify recovery before authority is restored?

## Income implication

A paid audit can be framed around one concrete question:

**Can your AI workflow recover from the failure your policy says it can handle?**

This is sharper than a broad “AI governance review” because it produces a visible test artifact.

## Care/social-care implication

Care systems should not only ask whether an AI note was checked. They should ask:

**When a wrong note is discovered, how is reliance stopped, corrected, propagated, and reviewed?**

This matters because documentation errors can become assessment errors, service-allocation errors, risk-label errors, or clinician-communication errors.
