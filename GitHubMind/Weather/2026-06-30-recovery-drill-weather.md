## Weather reading

The dominant weather pattern is **recovery realism**.

External governance language is becoming less satisfied with abstract human oversight. The stronger question is now:

**What happens when the agent crosses the wrong boundary, and can the organization prove the recovery path works?**

## Fresh signals

1. Financial regulators are discussing agentic AI through the lens of market instability, recovery mechanisms, circuit breakers, and kill switches. Source: Reuters, 2026-06-30, https://www.reuters.com/world/agentic-ai-may-require-regulatory-reform-boes-breeden-says-2026-06-30/

2. U.S. lawmakers are considering explicit AI incident reporting duties for critical incidents such as bypassed oversight or public-safety threats. Source: Reuters, 2026-06-25, https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/us-lawmaker-proposes-bill-require-ai-companies-report-critical-incidents-2026-06-25/

3. Healthcare agent governance research is emphasizing identity registries, PHI-bounded context, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, lifecycle management, credential revocation, decommissioning, and audit logging. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15630

4. Runtime guardrail research is separating governance objectives from design constraints, runtime mediation, and assurance evidence. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05229

5. Regulated-domain engineering research is proposing graduated human oversight based on impact, reversibility, proximity to customers, and data sensitivity. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22484

## Interpretation

The repository already contains interruption and reversibility primitives. The next weather shift is **drilled recovery**:

- not just “there is a kill switch”;
- not just “a human can review”;
- not just “the record can be corrected”;
- but “the organization has rehearsed failure, captured evidence, and knows what restoration requires.”
