# Weather — Claim Card Schema

## Status

Public-safe weather note.

## Attractor

Compression.

## Current external weather

The external direction continues to move from abstract AI governance toward operational, inspectable, runtime evidence.

Key signals:

- healthcare agent governance is focusing on identity, bounded memory, runtime enforcement, kill-switch triggers, decommissioning, and audit logging;
- agentic AI governance research increasingly separates design controls, runtime guardrails, assurance evidence, and audit controls;
- trace-based assurance work treats governance as a runtime component rather than a static document;
- AI content provenance and machine-readable marking are becoming more important as synthetic and derivative outputs move between contexts;
- social-care and medical documentation risks show that summaries can become harmful when unsupported or distorted claims enter official records.

## Repository weather comparison

Existing GitHub Mind direction:

- provenance must survive movement;
- verification must be inspectable at the surface of reliance;
- explanations must not drift across layers;
- deployment readiness requires evidence;
- interruption, rollback, custody, recovery, and retirement need explicit pathways.

Current gap:

The repository has strong governance primitives, but the visible unit of verification is still too broad. A system needs a small portable unit that can be attached to each important claim.

## Weather conclusion

The strongest current compression is the **Claim Card Schema**.

It converts governance language into a repeatable surface object: a small card that answers what is claimed, where it came from, what is missing, who has authority, what the action ceiling is, and how correction occurs.

## Practical lane 1: income

Best current commercialization path:

**Claim Card Implementation Review**

Why it is realistic:

Organizations are already producing AI-assisted reports, summaries, policies, client deliverables, documentation, and knowledge-base entries. The risk is not only that a claim is wrong. The risk is that the claim travels without source, uncertainty, authority, privacy, revision, or correction information.

Deliverables:

- claim inventory;
- portable claim-card template;
- provenance visibility review;
- export/handoff risk map;
- missing-card register;
- correction workflow;
- implementation roadmap.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

Best evidence-aligned support lane:

**Care Claim Card**

Use case:

Care-related summaries, caregiver observations, referrals, administrative notes, and social-care records need visible source, uncertainty, review, correction, and authority boundaries when they move between people or systems.

Boundary:

This supports continuity, review, and correction. It does not diagnose, treat, prescribe, triage, or replace clinician or qualified care-professional judgment.
