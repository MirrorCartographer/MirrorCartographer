# Weather: Exit Authority and Continuous Assurance

## Current weather

The public AI governance environment is moving from permission-to-use toward permission-to-continue.

The recurring external pattern is no longer only approval, validation, or documentation. It is continuous risk assessment, runtime enforcement, human override, corrective action, and decommissioning.

## Observed signals

1. Financial AI governance is emphasizing ongoing risk review, independent validation, human oversight for automated decision-making, and the ability to apply enhanced controls, limit use, or decommission models when risk becomes excessive.

2. Healthcare-agent governance is emphasizing runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, audit logging, credential revocation, and lifecycle management.

3. Medical-agent ethics work is emphasizing explicit human override conditions and oversight tooling.

4. Social-care documentation reporting shows that AI-generated records can become harmful when drafts are trusted too quickly, checked too lightly, or converted into official records without sufficient human review.

5. Enterprise AI assurance research is shifting away from proof of correctness and toward continuous risk reduction.

## Repository implication

Mirror Cartographer should treat continuation itself as a governed action.

A claim, workflow, or artifact should not only ask:

- Is this supported?
- Is this traceable?
- Is this actionable?

It should also ask:

- Who can stop this?
- What evidence forces review?
- What condition reverses authority?
- What makes decommissioning mandatory?
