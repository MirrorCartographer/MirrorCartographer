## Current weather

Recent public evidence points in the same direction across domains:

- Financial regulators are moving toward ongoing AI risk assessment, board-approved frameworks, independent validation, enhanced controls, limiting use, and decommissioning when risk exceeds tolerance.
- Agentic healthcare AI research is treating identity, permissions, PHI-bounded context, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, credential revocation, and audit logging as lifecycle necessities.
- Runtime governance research is turning policy from advisory text into execution conditions, proof artifacts, logs, and shutdown paths.
- Social-care AI documentation failures show the cost of treating generated records as usable before review thresholds are met.
- Audit and assurance reporting continues to emphasize that humans and institutions remain accountable for AI-assisted outputs.

## Repository implication

Mirror Cartographer should keep evolving from general symbolic reflection toward bounded claim governance:

- What is the claim?
- What is the source?
- What is missing?
- What can this claim currently do?
- What threshold would allow it to do more?
- What threshold would force review, pause, or retirement?

## Practical lane: income

Weather supports a concrete service lane:

**AI Governance Threshold Review**

This can be positioned for small teams, researchers, agencies, and creators who use AI outputs but need a defensible map of claim status, evidence maturity, missingness, and action authority.

## Practical lane: medical and social care

The relevant opportunity is not diagnosis. It is safer documentation continuity:

- reviewable observations;
- clear uncertainty;
- source labels;
- escalation thresholds;
- revision history;
- separation between reflection, documentation, decision support, and intervention authority.
