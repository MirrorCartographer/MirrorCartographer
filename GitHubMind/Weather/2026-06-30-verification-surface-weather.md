# Weather — Verification Surface

## Status

Public-safe external signal scan.

## Attractor

Discovery.

## Weather summary

The external field is moving from abstract provenance language toward inspectable provenance surfaces.

Three pressures are converging:

1. Agentic systems increasingly act through tools, connectors, and delegated authority, so governance has to be visible inside workflows rather than held only in policy documents.
2. Synthetic content systems are adopting metadata, watermarking, and content credentials, but those mechanisms remain fragile when metadata is stripped or content is transformed.
3. Medical and social-care documentation risks show that summaries can become operational records even when their uncertainty, source, or review status is unclear.

## Signals

### Runtime governance

Recent agent-governance research emphasizes runtime mediation, audit evidence, stop-anywhere controls, finite-state conformance, and assurance feedback. This supports the GitHub Mind's shift toward operational controls rather than static declarations.

### Content provenance

Recent content-authenticity work and industry reporting emphasize metadata, content credentials, watermarking, public verification portals, and the limitation that metadata may be removed. This creates a need for redundant verification surfaces rather than single fragile provenance markers.

### Care and social-care documentation

Reports on AI transcription and summary errors in social care show how generated text can distort official records. Healthcare AI liability concerns show that accountability must be clear when AI outputs influence clinical or administrative decisions.

## Practical implication

Mirror Cartographer should treat every derivative artifact as needing a **verification surface**: a small readable or machine-readable boundary card that travels with the artifact and tells the next recipient what the claim is, where it came from, what is missing, who has authority, and how to correct it.

## Practical lane 1: income

Potential offering:

**AI Verification Surface Review**

Use case:

Organizations using AI to create executive summaries, governance reports, policy drafts, product claims, sales copy, client deliverables, knowledge-base content, or care-adjacent documentation need a way to prove that provenance and uncertainty remain inspectable after transformation.

Deliverables:

- artifact surface map;
- provenance visibility audit;
- missing-verification register;
- derivative-risk classification;
- claim card template;
- correction and supersession pathway;
- implementation roadmap.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

Potential support artifact:

**Care Summary Verification Card**

Purpose:

Expose observation source, review status, uncertainty, correction history, and authority limits on patient-facing, caregiver-facing, clinician-facing, and administrative summaries.

Boundary:

This supports communication, continuity, and review. It does not diagnose, treat, triage, or replace clinician or qualified care-professional judgment.
