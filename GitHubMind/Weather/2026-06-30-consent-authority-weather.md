## Current weather

The outside field is converging on a governance problem that is sharper than generic safety: AI systems are beginning to act, transact, summarize, coordinate, and write into records. This makes permission boundaries operational, not decorative.

The relevant weather pattern:

- healthcare agent governance is moving toward identity registries, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, decommissioning, credential revocation, and audit logging;
- trace-based assurance research treats side effects such as API calls, database writes, and message sends as governance objects;
- regulated-domain oversight work is moving toward tiered human review based on regulatory impact, reversibility, customer proximity, and data sensitivity;
- healthcare agent security work emphasizes least privilege, prompt integrity, untrusted-content labeling, credential protection, and egress boundaries;
- social-care documentation reports show that AI-generated summaries can introduce harmful misrepresentations into official records if unchecked;
- agentic commerce raises authorization, liability, consumer-protection, and fraud questions when AI agents can purchase or transact on behalf of people.

**AI Consent Boundary Review**

## Practical lane 2 — care/social-care signal

Evidence-aligned lane:

**Care Consent Boundary Card**

This fits AI scribes, care summaries, social-care notes, caregiver summaries, and patient/caregiver-facing continuity documents.

The intervention is not diagnosis. It is safer record movement:

- draft versus reviewed note;
- observation versus assessment;
- consented reuse versus non-consented reuse;
- person-supplied statement versus AI-generated inference;
- clinician/social-worker-reviewed versus unreviewed.
