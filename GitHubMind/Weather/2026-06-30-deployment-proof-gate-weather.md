## Claim

The weather is shifting from static policy toward deployment evidence.

## Current external pattern

Recent work repeatedly emphasizes:

- lifecycle governance;
- runtime policy enforcement;
- trace-based assurance;
- action mediation;
- interruptibility;
- human escalation;
- rollback and decommissioning;
- auditable evidence.

The recurring pressure is practical: organizations cannot safely rely on autonomous or semi-autonomous AI systems unless controls can be observed, tested, and revised during real operation.

## Interpretation

The GitHub Mind should keep moving from symbolic description into operational proof.

A good governance artifact now needs to answer:

1. What state is this workflow in?
2. What can it do?
3. Who authorized that?
4. What happens when it fails?
5. How do we know the control actually worked?
6. What evidence survives export, handoff, or review?

The safe opportunity is not diagnosis. It is continuity infrastructure:
