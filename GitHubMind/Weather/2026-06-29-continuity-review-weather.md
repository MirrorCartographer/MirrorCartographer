## Weather pattern
AI governance is shifting from static approval into recurring operational assurance.

The strongest current signals:

1. Healthcare agent governance work is emphasizing identity registries, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, audit logging, and lifecycle decommissioning.
2. Runtime guardrail research is separating governance objectives, design constraints, runtime mediation, and assurance feedback.
3. Oversight-by-design research treats human review, escalation thresholds, risk signalling, and audit logs as interface architecture rather than after-the-fact cleanup.
4. Enterprise AI assurance research frames testing as continuous risk reduction rather than final correctness verification.
5. Social-care documentation failures show that AI-generated records can become dangerous when weak transcripts are treated as stable facts.
6. Medical liability reporting shows that clinical AI use requires clearer accountability, review, and responsibility boundaries.
7. Market movement in AI assurance staffing suggests that auditability is becoming an economic lane, not just a research concern.

## Claim
The repository should treat continuity as an active control surface.

A claim that was safe yesterday can become unsafe today if its context, evidence, authority, or downstream use changes.
