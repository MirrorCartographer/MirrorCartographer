# Weather — Draft-to-Record Gate

## Status

Public-safe weather artifact.

## Attractor

Contradiction.

## Current weather

The external environment is converging on a practical governance problem: AI systems are increasingly used to produce documents that outlive the interaction that created them. In healthcare and social care, these documents can become operational memory. That makes record integrity a safety surface.

## Signals

1. Healthcare agent lifecycle research now emphasizes identity, bounded context, memory control, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, decommissioning, credential revocation, and audit logging.
2. Runtime guardrail research argues that standards-derived governance goals need translation into design constraints, runtime mediation, and assurance evidence.
3. Medical AI ethics proposals emphasize human override conditions, auditable reasoning, patient preference structures, oversight tools, benchmarking, and sandboxes.
4. Agentic AI assurance work emphasizes multi-step toolchains, dynamic authorization, semantic telemetry, interruptibility, provenance, and audit controls.
5. Social-care reporting shows a concrete failure mode: AI-produced summaries and transcripts can insert fabricated or distorted content into official care records.

## Interpretation

The record is becoming a governance object, not just a storage object. Once AI-assisted text enters an official record, the burden shifts from checking a draft to repairing institutional memory.

Low-friction offer:

- staff-facing guide.

This can be sold as risk reduction and workflow clarity without requiring new software.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care support

Evidence-based support should focus on continuity, provenance, and correction paths. The record should separate:

- direct observation,
- direct quote,
- generated summary,
- human interpretation,
- verified fact,
- unresolved uncertainty,
- recommended next review.

This keeps AI in the documentation-support lane and prevents accidental slide into diagnostic or decision authority.
