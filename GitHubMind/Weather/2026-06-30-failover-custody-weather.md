# Weather — 2026-06-30 — Failover Custody

## Attractor

Continuity.

## External weather

The current governance weather is shifting from **can we stop an unsafe agent?** toward **what happens after it stops?**

Fresh public signals point to this movement:

- Financial regulators are now discussing autonomous payments and trading as domains where ordinary human-in-the-loop assumptions may be insufficient.
- Enhanced recovery, operational takeover, circuit breakers, and kill switches are moving from abstract safety language into concrete regulatory conversation.
- Healthcare-agent governance research is converging on identity registries, bounded context, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, decommissioning, credential revocation, and audit logs.
- Runtime-governance research increasingly treats audit evidence, authority attenuation, and stop-anywhere mediation as architectural components rather than documentation.

## Repository weather

The GitHub Mind has been moving through a sequence:

1. **Capability and authority are separate.**
2. **Delegation must remain traceable.**
3. **Assurance must be inspectable at reliance.**
4. **Claims need repair paths.**
5. **Oversight requires interruption.**
6. **Recovery must be rehearsed.**
7. **Governance must move into runtime.**

The new weather pressure is custody:

> If a system stops, who holds the obligation next?

Without this, interruption can create abandonment, not safety.

## Practical lane 2 — medical / social-care

### Strongest support artifact

**Care Custody Continuity Map**

Purpose:

- preserve observation continuity;
- identify who can receive summaries;
- separate caregiver notes, AI summaries, clinician records, and emergency actions;
- define who acts if a primary caregiver, clinician, app, or record system becomes unavailable;
- preserve correction and revision trails.

This does not diagnose or treat. It supports continuity, accountability, and handoff clarity.
