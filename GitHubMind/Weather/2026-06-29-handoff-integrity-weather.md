# Weather: Handoff Integrity

## Status

Weather artifact. Public-safe. Initial version.

## Attractor

Contradiction.

## External weather

Current AI governance signals point toward transfer-safe accountability.

The pattern:

1. AI outputs move between tools, teams, documents, and decision contexts.
2. Downstream readers need to know what a claim is allowed to support.
3. Static policy is not enough when source, authority, and review context are missing.
4. Auditability is becoming a lifecycle property.
5. Human or institutional accountability remains attached even when AI contributes to the work.

## Source observations

- 2026 LLM audit-trail research emphasizes durable, context-rich lifecycle records that allow later reconstruction of what changed, when, why, and under whose authorization.
- 2026 healthcare agent-governance research emphasizes lifecycle controls, agent identity, runtime policy enforcement, stop triggers, decommissioning, credential revocation, and audit logging.
- 2026 enterprise governance research argues that organizations cannot govern what they cannot see, and that compliance is shifting toward telemetry evidence and architecture-backed proof.
- 2026 reporting on AI-assisted professional documentation reinforces the risk of unlabeled AI-generated errors entering later records.
- 2026 audit-sector guidance reinforces that responsibility cannot be displaced onto AI systems.

## Weather conclusion

The external environment supports a GitHub Mind primitive called Handoff Integrity.

A claim should not cross a boundary unless its source, evidence, missingness, authority, allowed use, review trigger, and accountability holder cross with it.

## Practical lane 1: income

Most realistic near-term revenue path:

AI Claim Handoff Audit.

Potential buyers:

- small organizations adopting AI note-taking,
- consultants using AI-generated reports,
- documentation-heavy teams,
- nonprofits using AI for program records,
- founders needing trustworthy AI governance language before selling into regulated environments.

Starter deliverable:

A short Handoff Integrity report identifying orphaned claims and attaching Handoff Cards to the highest-risk outputs.

## Practical lane 2: support and documentation

The evidence-based support lane is continuity documentation, not diagnosis or professional decision authority.

A safe Mirror Cartographer care-adjacent record would preserve:

- direct observation,
- source status,
- user correction,
- externally verified fact,
- AI-assisted interpretation,
- missingness,
- review trigger,
- escalation boundary,
- final human decision.

This reduces the chance that a reflective or AI-generated interpretation becomes an unlabeled fact in a future handoff.
