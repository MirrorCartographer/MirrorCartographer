# Weather — Interruption Thresholds

## Attractor

Contradiction.

## Current external weather

The strongest public signal this cycle is that agentic AI governance is moving from abstract oversight language toward concrete interruption, incident, and recovery mechanisms.

Recent reporting says the Bank of England is considering whether agentic AI may require new regulatory approaches, including circuit breakers or kill switches for high-impact financial systems. A separate June 25, 2026 Reuters report describes a proposed U.S. AI Incident Reporting Act that would require companies to report critical incidents, including systems bypassing human oversight or posing public-safety threats.

Research also points in the same direction. Healthcare agent lifecycle work emphasizes runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, credential revocation, audit logging, and decommissioning. Runtime guardrail research separates governance objectives from enforceable controls and argues that some controls belong specifically at execution time when risks are observable and time-sensitive. Agentic assurance frameworks emphasize telemetry, dynamic authorization, anomaly detection, interruptibility, provenance, and accountability.

The missing weather pattern is **pre-reliance interruption**: the threshold where a claim, summary, tool action, export, or workflow must pause before it becomes a record, recommendation, financial action, clinical artifact, or social-care decision support object.

## Weather claim

The governance frontier is shifting from:

"Who is responsible?"

to:

"What exact signal stops the system before responsibility becomes retrospective blame?"

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

The evidence-supported care lane is **Care Pause Trigger Map**.

This is not diagnosis. It is a continuity and communication structure for marking when observations, AI summaries, or care notes should stop and be reviewed because the information is incomplete, contradictory, privacy-sensitive, urgent, or outside the tool's authority.
