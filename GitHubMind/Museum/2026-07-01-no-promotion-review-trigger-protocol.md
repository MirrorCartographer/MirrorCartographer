## Claim

The Review Trigger Protocol should be preserved as a live primitive, but it should not yet be promoted as a validated system. It is a conceptual governance artifact, not executable assurance software.

## Why no promotion

Promotion is withheld because:

- thresholds are not calibrated;
- no schema validator exists yet;
- no test cases have been run;
- no expert medical, legal, social-care, or compliance review has occurred;
- the repository index did not expose enough existing GitHubMind context to verify all predecessor artifacts.

## What survives

The following concepts are worth carrying forward:

- continuity can preserve errors;
- high-stakes summaries require review triggers;
- action boundaries need interrupt conditions;
- authority and consent drift should pause reliance;
- care-support artifacts must preserve uncertainty.

## Practical lane 1: income

Preserve as candidate offer:

Review Trigger Audit.

Do not market as certified compliance, legal advice, medical safety certification, or validated risk scoring.

Public-safe description:

A structured review of where AI-assisted workflows should pause, downgrade, escalate, or route to human review before producing external effects.

## Practical lane 2: care support

Preserve as candidate artifact:

Care Review Trigger Card.

Do not describe as diagnosis, triage authority, treatment plan, emergency guidance, or clinician replacement.

Public-safe description:

A documentation boundary card that identifies when a care-related observation or summary needs professional review before reliance.
