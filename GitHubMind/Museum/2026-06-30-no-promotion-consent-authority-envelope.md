# Museum — No-Promotion Record: Consent Authority Envelope

## Artifact under review

`GitHubMind/Genesis/consent-authority-envelope.md`

## Museum purpose

Preserve the artifact without promoting it into product promise, clinical claim, compliance certification, legal advice, or implementation proof.

## Why quarantine is needed

Consent and authority language can sound stronger than it is. The current primitive is a design boundary, not a verified compliance system.

The artifact should not be marketed as:

- HIPAA compliance;
- EU AI Act compliance;
- clinical safety validation;
- social-care regulatory approval;
- legal consent sufficiency;
- autonomous-agent safety certification;
- proof that Mirror Cartographer can govern live healthcare workflows.

## Safe interpretation

The artifact may be used as:

- a public-safe governance concept;
- a checklist seed;
- a claim-boundary design primitive;
- an internal audit scaffold;
- a care-support documentation boundary;
- a research direction for permission-aware AI interfaces.

## Unsafe interpretation

Do not claim that the artifact:

- makes AI-generated care notes safe;
- authorizes medical or social-care action;
- solves hallucination or documentation risk;
- replaces professional review;
- creates valid legal consent;
- determines who has authority in real cases;
- permits AI agents to transact, message, write records, or make decisions without human governance.

## Practical lane 1 — income quarantine

The income path must be framed as **review and mapping**, not certification.

Acceptable phrase:

**AI Consent Boundary Review**

Avoid:

- legally valid consent engine;
- healthcare-ready agent governance;
- autonomous compliance layer.

## Practical lane 2 — care/social-care quarantine

The care lane must remain documentation-supportive.

Acceptable phrase:

Avoid:

- treatment authorization tool;
- social-care decision system;
- clinician replacement;
- official record validator.
