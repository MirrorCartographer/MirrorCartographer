## Museum decision

**No promotion yet.**

The Recovery Drill Ledger is structurally aligned with the current GitHub Mind, but it should remain in observation until it proves value across multiple runs.

## Why not promoted

It could become too performative if treated as a checklist instead of a real rehearsal record.

Bad version:

- “We have a recovery plan.”
- “A human can intervene.”
- “The system can roll back.”

Good version:

- “We simulated the failure.”
- “The pause triggered.”
- “The fallback preserved data integrity.”
- “The restoration condition was met.”
- “The drill exposed two missing escalation contacts.”
- “The artifact was revised.”

## Promotion criteria

Promote later if the primitive can reliably produce:

1. A concrete failure scenario.
2. A visible trigger.
3. A tested pause/fallback.
4. A human restoration authority.
5. Evidence of what happened.
6. A revision after the drill.
7. A clear residual-risk statement.

## Relation to beauty

The beautiful version is not a thick compliance packet.

The beautiful version is a compact artifact that lets someone see, quickly:

- what failed;
- what stopped;
- what recovered;
- what remained damaged;
- what changed afterward.

## Public-safe boundary

No real patient, user, client, financial, or operational incident data belongs in this artifact unless explicitly sanitized, permissioned, and de-identified.
