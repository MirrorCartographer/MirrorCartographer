# Museum — No Promotion: Claim Card Schema

## Status

Public-safe museum artifact.

## Artifact held back

**Claim Card Schema**

## Why this is in the Museum

The Claim Card Schema is useful, but it should not be promoted as a finished compliance, clinical, legal, or technical provenance system.

It is a design primitive that compresses existing GitHub Mind governance work into a portable unit. It has not yet been implemented as software, validated against real exports, mapped to every repository artifact, or tested in care-support workflows.

## Do not overclaim

Do not claim that the Claim Card Schema currently provides:

- legal compliance;
- clinical safety validation;
- automated provenance enforcement;
- cryptographic proof;
- C2PA integration;
- repository-wide claim tracking;
- medical record correction;
- social-care record governance;
- certified audit readiness.

## What can be claimed

It is reasonable to claim:

- the repository has identified a need for portable claim boundaries;
- the Claim Card Schema defines minimum fields for a claim to travel with source, uncertainty, authority, missingness, privacy, revision, and correction labels;
- the schema extends the Verification Surface Protocol and Provenance Carry-Forward Ledger;
- the schema is public-safe and conceptual;
- implementation, validation, and tooling remain missing.

## Why it matters

The Museum protects the repository from premature promotion.

The strongest danger is that a polished schema could be mistaken for an operational system. This artifact exists to preserve the boundary: useful design primitive, not finished assurance infrastructure.

## Practical lane 1: income boundary

Allowed commercial framing:

**Claim Card Implementation Review** or **Portable Claim Boundary Audit**

Do not frame it as certified compliance, legal protection, or guaranteed risk elimination.

## Practical lane 2: care boundary

Allowed care-support framing:

**Care Claim Card** for communication, continuity, review, and correction support.

Do not frame it as diagnosis, treatment, triage, prescription, clinical decision-making, or professional judgment replacement.

## Source labels

- Current healthcare agent governance and runtime assurance literature supports auditability, lifecycle control, decommissioning, runtime enforcement, and provenance.
- Current social-care reporting shows that AI-generated summaries can introduce harmful documentation errors.
- Current content provenance policy and standards activity supports visible or machine-readable marking, but implementation details remain context-dependent.

## Claim labels

- Claim: the Claim Card Schema should remain a design primitive until implemented and validated.
- Confidence: high.
- Evidence maturity: emerging but convergent.
- Authority: museum boundary, not legal, clinical, compliance, financial, or regulatory authority.

## Privacy label

Public-safe. No private health, household, client, patient, financial, or personal records included.

## Missingness label

Missing:

- automated validation;
- claim ID registry;
- export integration;
- cryptographic signing;
- care-workflow testing;
- stakeholder review;
- repository-wide adoption.

## Revision label

Revise if:

- the schema becomes implemented in MC;
- automated claim-card generation exists;
- care-support review cards are tested;
- external standards require additional fields;
- the schema is superseded by a stronger primitive.
