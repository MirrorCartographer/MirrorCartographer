# Museum — No-Promotion Note: Provenance Carry-Forward

## Why this is in the Museum

The Provenance Carry-Forward Ledger is preserved as a governance primitive, not as proof that Mirror Cartographer is already enterprise-ready, clinically validated, legally compliant, or commercially adopted.

## What this artifact prevents

It prevents a common overclaim:

"This system has source, claim, privacy, missingness, and revision labels, so its outputs are safe to reuse."

Corrected claim:

"The system is developing a primitive for checking whether labels survive reuse. It still needs implementation, testing, and review before it can be treated as operational assurance."

## What may be promoted

Public-safe claim:

Mirror Cartographer is developing a provenance carry-forward primitive for preserving source, claim, privacy, missingness, revision, and authority labels when artifacts are summarized, exported, renamed, layered, or reused.

## What may not be promoted

Do not claim:

- the repository has completed automated provenance tracking;
- the primitive provides legal compliance;
- the primitive validates medical or social-care summaries;
- the primitive prevents hallucinations by itself;
- the primitive proves enterprise readiness;
- the primitive replaces human review.

## Income lane boundary

The commercial idea may be explored as a review service:

**Provenance Carry-Forward Audit**

But it must be described as a human-led or tool-assisted review of derivative claim integrity, not a certified compliance product unless such certification is actually obtained.

## Medical and social-care boundary

The care-support idea may be explored as:

**Care Provenance Carry-Forward Map**

It must be described as communication and continuity support. It must not be described as diagnosis, treatment, triage authority, or clinical decision-making.

- Current social-care reporting supports concern about AI-generated documentation errors.

## Claim label

Public-safe. No private health, household, financial, client, or personal records included.
