The repository has many related concepts, but their shared operational unit is now named:

**Minimum Viable Trace**

## Comparison against existing mind

Recent artifacts have expanded the system in multiple directions:

- Evidence Chain: preserves changes over time.
- Claim Custody Chain: follows a claim across transformations.
- Evidence Readiness Gate: decides whether evidence is mature enough to proceed.
- Action Authority Ladder: decides what a claim is allowed to do.
- Nondelegable Accountability Boundary: prevents authority from becoming accountability transfer.
- Context Lineage: records where context came from and how it shaped the output.

Minimum Viable Trace compresses these into one testable record:

**source -> claim -> transformation -> authority -> action boundary -> outcome/revision**

## Weather

Observed public trend:

AI governance is moving from static documentation toward trace-based operational assurance. Current sources emphasize lifecycle audit trails, message-action traces, runtime enforcement, observability telemetry, and healthcare-specific lifecycle control planes.

## Forces

Dominant force:

**Trace pressure.**

Organizations increasingly need to reconstruct what AI systems did, why they did it, what evidence they used, what controls applied, and who remained accountable.

## Practical lane 1: income

Most realistic near-term opportunity:

**AI Evidence Hygiene Review**

This is a narrow, manual service based on Minimum Viable Trace. It does not require claiming to be a certified auditor or full AI governance platform.

Deliverables:

- Minimum Viable Trace map,
- missingness report,
- action-authority rating,
- fluent ambiguity risk notes,
- revision recommendations,
- executive summary.

Commercial hypothesis:

Small teams using AI in consequential documents may need lightweight evidence hygiene before they need enterprise assurance tooling.

Validation needed:

- identify one test user or sample workflow,
- perform a free or low-cost pilot,
- measure whether the report is understandable, useful, and worth paying for.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social care

Evidence-based direction:

Do not position MC as diagnosis, treatment, therapy, or clinical decision-making.

Position it as continuity and documentation support:

- preserve observations,
- label uncertainty,
- distinguish report from interpretation,
- maintain revision history,
- flag clinician-review boundaries,
- make missingness explicit.

This aligns with healthcare AI governance emphasis on lifecycle management, audit logging, human oversight, and PHI-bounded context.
