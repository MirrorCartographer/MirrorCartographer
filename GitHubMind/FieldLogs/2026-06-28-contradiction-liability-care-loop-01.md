## Claim labels

Claim 1: The strongest contradiction is that enterprises want AI agents to act, while governance evidence increasingly requires proof of what the agent changed.
Confidence: High.
Reason: Recent agent-governance literature emphasizes telemetry, provenance, runtime policy enforcement, audit controls, and human oversight.

Claim 2: Healthcare and social care expose the contradiction more sharply than generic enterprise workflows.
Confidence: Medium-high.
Reason: When AI contributes to triage, diagnosis, documentation, eligibility, fraud detection, care navigation, or resource allocation, the cost of bad transformation is not just inefficiency. It can become liability, denial of care, delayed care, or loss of trust.

Claim 3: Mirror Cartographer should treat every AI-assisted care or work episode as a transformation-liability-care loop.
Confidence: Medium-high.
Reason: The prior GitHub Mind already compressed MC into a Transformation Record. This run adds the next layer: who or what becomes accountable after a transformation enters the physical world.

## Genesis update

New candidate concept:

The Liability Surface.

Definition:
The Liability Surface is the set of people, organizations, tools, claims, and decisions that may carry consequence after AI changes an artifact or action path.

Early examples:
- clinician using AI-assisted diagnosis,
- nonprofit using AI for eligibility screening,
- company using an agent for customer action,
- person using AI to interpret medical records,
- developer using AI-generated code in production,
- agency using AI to detect fraud or prioritize audits.

## Practical lane 2 — Medical and social-care support path

Care-support candidate: Care Timeline and Liability Surface Audit

Use case:
A person or caregiver has scattered symptoms, appointments, labs, messages, pet-health notes, social-service needs, or family-care responsibilities.

Deliverable:
A public-safe template that organizes:
- timeline,
- symptom or event cluster,
- evidence source,
- uncertainty,
- care question,
- risk if missed,
- next professional to ask,
- social support needed,
- privacy boundary.

Evidence-based boundary:
This is not diagnosis or treatment.
It is care coordination, record organization, communication support, and uncertainty tracking.

Claim:
The medical/social-care lane should focus on helping people prepare better evidence for professionals, not replacing professionals.

Missingness:
Needs review against patient-safety literature, care-coordination research, and health-literacy evidence.

Revision:
This moves the lane away from speculative cure claims and toward actionable support that can coexist with clinical care.

## Field Log conclusion

The contradiction is the product signal:

AI is being asked to act like labor.
Governance still treats it like software.
Healthcare treats consequence like human responsibility.
Users experience it as confusing power.

Mirror Cartographer can become useful at the seam:

not by claiming authority,
but by showing exactly where transformation, evidence, consequence, and care separate from one another.
