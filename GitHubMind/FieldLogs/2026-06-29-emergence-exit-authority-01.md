## Practical lane 1: income

The strongest realistic income path remains a narrow service package, not a broad platform.

Updated service framing:

AI Governance Exit Review

Deliverables:

- governed trace reconstruction,
- action authority assessment,
- exit authority assessment,
- stop/reversal/decommission condition map,
- missingness and overreach report,
- executive risk summary.

Likely buyer category:

Small organizations using AI for documentation, client intake, knowledge management, internal decision support, or workflow automation but lacking mature governance staff.

Why it is realistic:

The external market is expanding around AI assurance and auditability, while many organizations are adopting AI faster than they are building governance capacity.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social care

Potential useful contribution:

Care Continuity Trace with Exit Authority

This would not diagnose, treat, or replace clinical judgment. It would help maintain a structured record of:

- observation,
- source,
- uncertainty,
- human decision,
- revision trigger,
- escalation condition,
- and stop condition.

Medical/social-care relevance:

Current public sources show that AI tools in care contexts need explicit human oversight, audit trails, override conditions, and safeguards against documentation errors becoming official truth too quickly.
