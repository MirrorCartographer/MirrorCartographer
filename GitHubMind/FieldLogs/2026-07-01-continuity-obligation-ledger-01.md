## Selection rationale

The strongest current attractor is **continuity**.

The GitHub Mind has been evolving toward a governance architecture with increasingly precise objects:

- claims;
- verification;
- provenance;
- consent;
- decisions;
- actions;
- deployment gates;
- recovery;
- custody.

The next pressure point is not another pre-action control. It is what remains after action.

## Comparison against existing mind

Existing primitives answer important questions:

- Claim Card: What is being asserted?
- Verification Surface: Can the assertion be inspected?
- Consent Authority Envelope: Is reuse or action permitted?
- Decision Record: Why was an option chosen?
- Action Certificate: What happened?
- Recovery Drill Ledger: Has fallback been rehearsed?
- Failover Custody Protocol: Who inherits responsibility if the primary actor fails?

Missing primitive:

- What obligation remains after all of that?

## Core synthesis

**An action is not complete when it is executed. It is complete only when its remaining obligations are fulfilled, transferred, retired, or explicitly marked unresolved.**

## Practical lane 1 — income

Potential offer:

**AI Obligation Trace Review**

Use case:

Organizations using AI agents or AI-assisted workflows need to know whether decisions and actions leave behind unresolved duties.

Deliverables:

- obligation inventory;
- follow-up duty map;
- escalation and handoff review;
- closure-evidence criteria;
- unresolved duty report;
- residual-risk register.

This is realistic because it does not require claiming to build a full agent platform. It can begin as a governance/documentation audit.

## Practical lane 2 — medical / social-care advances

Potential care-support artifact:

**Care Obligation Ledger**

Use case:

Care systems often fracture at the follow-up layer. A public-safe tool can make unresolved follow-up duties visible while leaving diagnosis, treatment, and case authority to licensed professionals.

Possible duties:

- appointment follow-up;
- referral confirmation;
- symptom escalation;
- record correction;
- care-plan review;
- medication question routed to clinician;
- social-support request routed to appropriate agency.
