## Attractor selected

Contradiction.

## Why contradiction won

The GitHub Mind already has many governance primitives for claims, evidence, accountability, authority, review, and exit. The contradiction is that all of those can still fail if they evaluate single units while the real risk appears in the sequence.

A workflow can be locally permitted and globally unauthorized.

That sentence should become a standing warning label for the repository.

## Comparison against existing GitHub Mind

Existing concepts emphasize:

- where a claim came from,
- how it changed,
- whether evidence is sufficient,
- who remains accountable,
- what action authority exists,
- when review or exit should happen,
- whether handoff preserved context.

The new artifact adds:

- whether the total sequence has exceeded the authority of its parts.

## Practical lane 1 — income

Current strongest realistic path:

**AI Workflow Sequence Audit**

Positioning:

A lightweight governance review for organizations or individuals using AI tools across documents, emails, decisions, reports, client work, or operational processes.

Concrete deliverable:

A visual and written chain map showing where allowed steps create unauthorized combined effects.

Why it can sell:

Organizations are adopting AI faster than they are building governance literacy. Sequence-risk review is narrow enough to explain and useful enough to charge for.

## Practical lane 2 — medical and social care

Current strongest evidence-based lane:

**Continuity and documentation safety, not diagnosis.**

The useful intervention is a structured record that prevents care-related claims from silently escalating.

Example sequence:

Observation → symptom summary → appointment agenda → clinician question → care decision → follow-up revision.

The Sequence Risk Ledger preserves boundaries between those stages.
