# Field Log: Emergence — Intervention Boundary Matrix

## Attractor

Emergence

## Why this attractor won

The strongest pattern in the current public signal is not merely governance, audit, or traceability. It is the emergence of a boundary problem:

A system can produce useful documentation and still be unsafe if users mistake documentation support for intervention authority.

This matters for Mirror Cartographer because the GitHub Mind has been accumulating trace, evidence, custody, sufficiency, readiness, authority, and accountability concepts. The next useful step is to bind them into a practical boundary around real-world action.

Observed gap:

The prior artifacts explain how a claim is sourced, traced, matured, and permissioned. They do not yet make the medical/social-care distinction explicit enough:

- documentation support
- decision support
- intervention authority

The new artifact fills that gap.

## New artifact created

GitHubMind/Genesis/intervention-boundary-matrix.md

## Claim added

Mirror Cartographer should distinguish documentation support, decision support, and intervention authority as separate layers.

1. RBI draft AI/ML risk guidance, reported by Reuters on 2026-06-24, requires ongoing risk assessment, independent validation, enhanced controls, possible model-use limits or decommissioning, and human oversight for automated decision-making.

Agentic AI Governance and Lifecycle Management in Healthcare, arXiv 2026-01-22, proposes operational healthcare controls including identity/persona registry, PHI-bounded memory/context, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, lifecycle management, credential revocation, and audit logging.
3. The Guardian, 2026-02-11, reported Ada Lovelace Institute findings on social-work AI transcription/summarization errors that can distort official care records while also acknowledging time-saving and relational benefits.
4. Human Oversight-by-Design for Accessible Generative IUIs, arXiv 2026-02-14, frames escalation policies, risk signals, intervention controls, and traceable audit logs as necessary for high-stakes generated interfaces.
5. Six Interventions for the Responsible and Ethical Implementation of Medical AI Agents, arXiv 2026-03-14, proposes human override conditions, auditable ethical reasoning, preference profiles, ethics oversight tools, benchmarks, and regulatory sandboxes.

## Weather

The weather remains runtime assurance: governance is moving from static policy statements into monitored execution, escalation thresholds, audit logs, lifecycle controls, and human override mechanisms.

## Forces

Dominant force: intervention authority must be earned separately from claim clarity.

A beautiful or coherent summary does not become safe because it is legible. It becomes safer only when source fidelity, uncertainty, review authority, and accountability are preserved.

## Practical lane 1: income

Updated realistic offer:

AI Documentation Risk and Evidence Review

This is more concrete than selling Mirror Cartographer as a general symbolic tool. It sells a narrow, useful boundary check:

- Is the record faithful to sources?
- Where did AI transform meaning?
- Which claims are unsupported or overconfident?
- What actions are not authorized by the evidence?
- What should require human review?

This could apply to AI scribes, intake notes, case summaries, advocacy records, internal governance documentation, and startup compliance materials.

## Practical lane 2: medical/social-care support
