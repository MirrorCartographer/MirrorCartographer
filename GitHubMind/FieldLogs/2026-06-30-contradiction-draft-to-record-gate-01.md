# Field Log — Contradiction: Draft-to-Record Gate

## Observation

The repository has been building a governance vocabulary around authority, context, portability, handoff, sequence risk, and control placement. The next missing boundary is the point where generated language becomes durable institutional memory.

## Attractor selected

Contradiction.

## Why this attractor won

The strongest external signal is that AI documentation tools can be both genuinely useful and genuinely dangerous. In social care, AI-generated transcripts and summaries can save time and support relational work, but reporting from February 2026 describes fabricated or distorted content entering official records. In healthcare AI governance, recent work emphasizes lifecycle control, runtime enforcement, audit logging, override conditions, and provenance.

## Repository comparison

Existing primitives already cover nearby territory:

- Handoff Integrity: claims must carry context.
- Claim Portability Test: claims must earn travel.
- Context Lease: permission expires with context.
- Sequence Risk Ledger: small authorized steps can combine into unauthorized workflows.
- Control Placement Matrix: safeguards must sit at the right layer.
- Autonomy/Access Separation: capability and authority are independent.

The missing primitive was a specific conversion gate for AI-assisted documentation.

## Artifact added

`GitHubMind/Genesis/draft-to-record-gate.md`

Core invariant:

**A draft has no record authority until a responsible human verifies source fidelity, missingness, interpretation boundaries, and action consequences.**

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

Best evidence-supported intervention lane:

AI should support continuity documentation, source fidelity, uncertainty tracking, and correction paths. It should not be presented as diagnostic authority or autonomous care decision-maker.
