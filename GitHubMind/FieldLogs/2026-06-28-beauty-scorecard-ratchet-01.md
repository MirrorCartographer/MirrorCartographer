## Core upgrade

Mirror Cartographer now has a measurable review object for a recurring AI failure mode:

Fluent Ambiguity: when output sounds organized and helpful while hiding missing evidence, missing source links, softened uncertainty, unlabeled inference, or consequence-bearing interpretation.

The scorecard uses 8 dimensions:

1. Source traceability.
2. Claim specificity.
3. Evidence preservation.
4. Missingness visibility.
5. Inference labeling.
6. Consequence awareness.
7. Privacy and exposure boundary.
8. Revision path.

Each dimension is scored 0 to 3.

Total score: 24.

## Genesis update

New candidate concept:

Consequence-Weighted Missingness.

Definition:

Missing information should be weighted by downstream consequence, not merely counted.

A missing date in a casual brainstorm is low impact.
A missing date in a care escalation, benefits record, safety report, legal memo, code deployment explanation, or AI-agent action trace may be high impact.

Genesis label:
- Source: Scorecard design.
- Claim: Missingness becomes more important when an output is likely to affect care, money, safety, eligibility, reputation, legal exposure, or system behavior.
- Privacy: PUBLIC-SAFE.
- Missingness: Needs domain-specific weighting examples.
- Revision: New concept.
