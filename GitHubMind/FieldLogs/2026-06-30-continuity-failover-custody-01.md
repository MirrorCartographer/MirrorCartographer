## Why this attractor

The strongest current pattern is not another isolated safety label. The repository now has a growing governance stack: authority, delegation, assurance, reversibility, interruption, recovery, and runtime kernel. The continuity gap appears after interruption: when normal operation fails, someone or something still has to hold responsibility.

## Fresh research signal

Current public sources increasingly emphasize autonomous-agent disruption, kill switches, enhanced recovery, runtime controls, audit trails, decommissioning, and stateful governance.

Key signals:

- Reuters reported on 2026-06-30 that the Bank of England is considering whether agentic AI in finance may require bespoke rules, including enhanced recovery and circuit breakers or kill switches for autonomous payments and trading.
- Healthcare-agent lifecycle research proposes identity/persona registries, PHI-bounded context and memory, runtime policy enforcement with kill-switch triggers, lifecycle management, credential revocation, decommissioning, and audit logging.
- Runtime-governance research proposes stop-anywhere mediation, composite principals, capability attenuation, and audit as structured evidence substrate.
- Trace-based assurance research frames external side effects, replay, service faults, memory faults, and runtime governance as testable properties of agentic orchestration.

## Repository comparison

Existing primitives covered:
