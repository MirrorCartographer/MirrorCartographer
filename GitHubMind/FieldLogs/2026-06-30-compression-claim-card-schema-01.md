## Why this attractor

The strongest current need is not another broad governance primitive. The repository now contains many controls and boundaries: provenance carry-forward, verification surface, layer drift, deployment proof, reversibility, recovery, interruption, custody, and state-machine thinking.

The compression need is to define the smallest reusable unit that can carry those boundaries with a claim.

## Repository comparison

- The Verification Surface Protocol says provenance must be inspectable where reliance occurs.
- The Provenance Carry-Forward Ledger says labels must survive movement.

Gap found:

## Core upgrade

**A claim that cannot carry its minimum verification boundary should not be allowed to travel alone.**

## New primitive

**Claim Card Schema**

Purpose:

Compress source, claim, evidence maturity, confidence, privacy, missingness, authority, revision, supersession, derivative status, verification method, correction path, and action ceiling into a portable unit.

## Practical lane 1: income

Best current commercialization path:

**Claim Card Implementation Review**

Use case:

Organizations producing AI-assisted reports, summaries, internal policies, product claims, client deliverables, knowledge-base entries, research notes, governance documents, or care-adjacent summaries need a way to prevent claims from traveling without visible provenance and correction boundaries.

Deliverables:

- claim inventory;
- portable claim-card template;
- provenance visibility review;
- export and handoff risk map;
- missing-card register;
- correction workflow;
- implementation roadmap.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care

Best evidence-aligned support lane:

**Care Claim Card**

Purpose:

Preserve observation source, review state, uncertainty, authority boundary, privacy boundary, correction history, missingness, and supersession state when care-related information moves between patient, caregiver, clinician, administrative, referral, and social-care contexts.

Boundary:

This supports communication, continuity, review, and correction. It does not diagnose, treat, triage, prescribe, or replace clinician or qualified care-professional judgment.
