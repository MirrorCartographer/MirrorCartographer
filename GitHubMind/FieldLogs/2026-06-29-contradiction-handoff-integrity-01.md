## Status

Field Log. Public-safe. Initial version.

## Attractor

Contradiction.

## Observation

The GitHub Mind has become strong at labeling claim status, evidence status, authority boundaries, review loops, and thresholds. The next weakness appears when a claim moves.

A claim can be well-governed in one document and poorly governed in the next if its labels do not travel with it.

## Comparison against existing GitHub Mind

Existing README language already emphasizes that Mirror Cartographer is bounded, not diagnostic, not an oracle, and designed to preserve the boundary between symbol and evidence. The system also tracks source status, claim status, audit labels, health-adjacent boundary flags, evidence boundaries, grounded next steps, and feedback loops.

Handoff Integrity does not replace those labels. It defines the rule for transferring them.

## New synthesis

Handoff Integrity turns claim governance into a portability problem.

The core question:

Can the claim survive movement without becoming stronger, vaguer, or more authoritative than intended?

## Practical lane 1: income

Strongest realistic income path identified this cycle:

AI Claim Handoff Audit.

Minimum sellable version:

- review one AI-assisted report, memo, care-adjacent note, or client-facing document,
- identify orphaned claims,
- classify source status and authority boundary,
- attach Handoff Cards to the top five highest-risk claims,
- deliver a concise revision plan.

This is more realistic than selling a full platform immediately because it can be delivered manually, improves trust, and creates product evidence.

## Practical lane 2: evidence-based support

Strongest support direction:

Use Mirror Cartographer as continuity infrastructure for observations, uncertainty, source labels, and revision triggers.

Do not position it as diagnosis, treatment, or professional decision replacement.

The useful intervention is documentation quality: preserving enough context that future review is safer, clearer, and less likely to confuse reflection with fact.
