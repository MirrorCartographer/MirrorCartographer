## Genesis

Added:

- Action Authority Ladder

Definition:

A ladder that records what a claim is allowed to do at each maturity level, from Park through Museum Candidate.

## Weather

Current weather pattern:

AI governance continues moving from static policy toward runtime evidence, observability, audit trails, custody, and lifecycle controls.

Healthcare AI adds liability pressure because AI-influenced clinical errors may create legal and professional consequences.

AI assurance is also becoming a visible market category.

## Forces

Dominant force:

Action without authority is becoming the core risk.

Evidence alone is not enough. A system needs to know whether evidence is allowed to become a draft, route-to-review note, controlled-use decision, audit-ready artifact, or durable reference.

## Field interpretation

The GitHub Mind is moving from:

- What changed?
- What evidence supports it?
- Who touched the claim?

Toward:

- What is this claim allowed to do now?

That question connects governance, income, and care.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social-care support

Safe intervention area:

Longitudinal continuity records that keep observations, uncertainty, review needs, and revisions visible.

Possible support contexts:

- appointment preparation
- symptom timeline organization
- caregiver notes
- disability or case-management preparation
- family communication summaries

Boundary:

This is documentation and review-routing support. It is not diagnosis, prescription, or replacement for professional care.
