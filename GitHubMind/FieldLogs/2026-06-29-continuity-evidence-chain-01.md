## Summary

This pass compares the current GitHub Mind direction against fresh public research and identifies the strongest missing layer: continuity across transformation records.

The recent repository direction has focused on:

- Transformation Records
- Transformation-Liability-Care Loops
- Fluent Ambiguity
- Audit templates
- Scorecards

These are useful, but most are single-artifact objects. Current research and market signals point toward evidence that persists across time: traces, telemetry, provenance, audit trails, runtime controls, and accountability ledgers.

## Strongest attractor selected

Continuity.

Reason:

The strongest current pattern is not a new isolated concept. It is the need to connect every concept already built into a durable record of change over time.

## Fresh source scan

### Source 1: Trace-Based Assurance for Agentic AI

URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18096
Date: March 18, 2026

Relevant signal:

Agentic AI failures arise from long-horizon interaction, stochastic decisions, external side effects, unsupported claim propagation, role drift, and attacks through untrusted context. The paper proposes Message-Action Traces, contracts, replay, runtime governance, and action mediation.

Mirror Cartographer comparison:

MC already tracks transformation, missingness, and revision. It should now connect those records into chains so drift and consequences are visible across time.

### Source 2: AGENTSAFE

URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.03180
Date: December 2, 2025

Relevant signal:

Agentic governance requires design, runtime, and audit controls, including semantic telemetry, dynamic authorization, anomaly detection, interruptibility, provenance, accountability, and auditable lifecycle assurance.

Mirror Cartographer comparison:

MC should treat its recurring Source, Claim, Privacy, Missingness, and Revision labels as proto-telemetry for human-facing knowledge work.

### Source 3: Audit Trails for Accountability in LLMs

URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20727
Date: January 28, 2026

Relevant signal:

LLM audit trails are described as chronological, tamper-evident, context-rich ledgers linking technical provenance with governance records so organizations can reconstruct what changed, when, and who authorized it.

Mirror Cartographer comparison:

This strongly supports the shift from isolated audits to Evidence Chains.

### Source 4: Healthcare AI liability reporting

Medical Protection Society warned that doctors and NHS organizations could face liability for mistakes made by AI tools, especially if diagnostic or treatment errors cause harm.

## Genesis

Added:

Evidence Chain.

Definition:

A continuity structure linking source, claim, transformation, evidence, missingness, privacy, consequence, revision, and later validation across time.

## Weather

Current public environment:

- Agentic AI governance is shifting toward runtime evidence, not just static policy.
- Enterprises need traceability when AI systems act across tools, memory, repositories, databases, or external services.
- Healthcare AI liability concerns are becoming concrete and public.
- High-stakes professional AI products are being marketed around accuracy, accountability, and fiduciary-grade trust.

## Forces

Dominant force:

Runtime accountability.

Secondary forces:

- AI systems increasingly act rather than merely answer.
- Acting creates consequence.
- Consequence creates liability.
- Liability creates demand for traceable evidence.
- Traceable evidence creates a market for transformation records and evidence chains.

## Practical lane 1: Income

Strongest realistic offer:

AI Transformation Audit plus Evidence Chain.

Entry-level service:

- Audit one AI-assisted artifact.
- Identify claims, unsupported inferences, missing evidence, risk surfaces, privacy exposure, and revision path.

Higher-value service:

- Maintain evidence chains across multiple outputs, revisions, approvals, and downstream decisions.

Likely initial buyer types:

- small AI tool builders
- consultants using AI in client work
- healthcare-adjacent documentation startups
- legal-tech or compliance-adjacent teams
- founders preparing investor, grant, regulatory, or enterprise-facing materials

Caution:

Do not sell this as certification, legal compliance, clinical safety, or guaranteed risk reduction. Sell it as structured review evidence and decision-support documentation.

## Practical lane 2: Medical and social care

Strongest evidence-based lane:

Care continuity documentation.

Mirror Cartographer should not make clinical claims. Its safe and potentially useful role is:

- organize observations over time
- separate observation from interpretation
- preserve uncertainty
- identify missing data
- prepare clearer questions for professionals
- reduce loss of nuance in summaries
- track changes after visits, interventions, or new evidence

Care claim label:

Claim: Better structured documentation and preserved uncertainty can support communication and continuity of care.
Confidence: Medium
Privacy: Public-safe only in this artifact
Missingness: Need specific clinical-context validation and professional review before claiming outcome improvements.
