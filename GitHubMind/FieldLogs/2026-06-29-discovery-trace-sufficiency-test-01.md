Date: 2026-06-29
Attractor: Discovery
Privacy: Public-safe

## Run finding

The current GitHub Mind has developed a strong trace architecture, especially the Minimum Viable Trace. The next useful discovery is that trace existence is not enough. A trace can be present, structured, and still too weak for the action it is asked to support.

New artifact added:

- GitHubMind/Genesis/trace-sufficiency-test.md

## Museum

No promotion this cycle.

Reason: Minimum Viable Trace is strong, but the repository still needs applied examples and failure cases before any trace-related concept becomes stable enough for Museum status.

## Genesis

Added:

**Trace Sufficiency Test**

Core compression:

- Minimum Viable Trace asks: what record must exist?
- Trace Sufficiency Test asks: is that record strong enough for the claim's intended authority and action boundary?

## Weather

Fresh external signals continue to converge on auditability, trace-based assurance, runtime guardrails, and lifecycle accountability.

Notable sources:

- LLM audit trails are being framed as durable ledgers that connect technical provenance with governance records.
- Agentic AI assurance work is using message-action traces, contracts, deterministic replay, and governance outcome distributions.
- Runtime guardrail research distinguishes governance objectives, technical controls, runtime mediation, and assurance evidence.
- Healthcare agent governance work emphasizes identity/persona registries, PHI-bounded memory, runtime policy enforcement, kill-switch triggers, decommissioning, credential revocation, and audit logging.
- Social-care reporting shows that AI-generated transcripts and summaries can distort records in ways that affect care decisions and professional accountability.

## Forces

Dominant force:

**Trace sufficiency over trace existence.**

The pressure is shifting from "do we have documentation?" to "can this documentation actually support reconstruction, challenge, correction, and accountability under the level of action being taken?"

## Practical lane 1: income

Refined offer:

**Trace Sufficiency Review**

A realistic near-term paid service for AI-assisted documents, workflows, notes, or decision paths.

Deliverables:

- Minimum Viable Trace reconstruction
- trace sufficiency score
- missingness severity map
- action-authority recommendation
- review checkpoints
- correction protocol
- public-safe executive summary

Market rationale:

AI adoption is running ahead of oversight, while AI assurance teams and audit-adjacent services are expanding. This suggests demand for small, concrete governance artifacts that help organizations see whether their AI-assisted processes can survive review.

## Practical lane 2: medical and social care

Care-safe application:

Use Trace Sufficiency Test to improve documentation continuity and reviewability, not to diagnose, prescribe, automate care decisions, or replace licensed judgment.

Most important care safeguard:

Any AI-generated note should preserve the boundary between:

- original speech or observation,
- human interpretation,
- AI transformation,
- unsupported addition,
- review status,
- correction history.

This matters because social-care AI documentation errors can enter official records and influence decisions about children, adults, practitioners, and services.
