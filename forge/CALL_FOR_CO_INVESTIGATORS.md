# Call for Co-Investigators

Mirror Cartographer is looking for people who recognize the same problem from different worlds.

The problem:

**Information can survive while understanding disappears.**

That happens in notes, records, research, code, art, care work, long projects, and conversations.

Mirror Cartographer is an attempt to build a public body of demonstrations showing how fragments become maps, how maps become action, and how understanding can be preserved without pretending uncertainty is gone.

## Who this is for

You may recognize the project if you are:

- a researcher interested in human-AI interaction
- a designer interested in reflective interfaces
- a developer interested in knowledge tools that are not boring
- an artist who thinks symbols are infrastructure
- a person who organizes complex archives
- a caretaker who knows records rarely tell the whole story
- a founder who wants a project to remember why it exists
- a writer or thinker who has lost ideas across tools

## What to bring

Bring one fragment.

A fragment can be:

- a research question
- a project that lost its center
- an archive that became too large to understand
- a symbol you keep returning to
- a care-history problem
- a creative system
- a tool failure that should become a feature
- a joke that reveals something true

## What we are building

Project 101: one hundred and one demonstrations that show Mirror Cartographer by making understanding visible.

The public repository is not meant to be a polished pitch. It is a research vessel.

## How to contribute

Open a discussion, issue, pull request, or fork with one of these:

- a demonstration idea
- a stronger boundary rule
- a better interface sketch
- a useful critique
- a public-safe example
- a collaboration proposal
- a proof that something is boring and should be rebuilt

## Standard

Do not make it vague.

Do not make it generic.

Do not make it look smarter by becoming less true.

Make the map better.
