# Project 101: Mirror Cartographer Demonstrations

A public demonstration set for proving Mirror Cartographer by showing, not telling.

Each demonstration follows one reusable anatomy:

- **Question** — what capability is being tested.
- **Why it matters** — the real-world complexity cost.
- **Input** — what kind of fragment enters the map.
- **Transformation** — what Mirror Cartographer does to it.
- **Proof of value** — what a visitor can observe.
- **Boundary** — what the demo does not claim.

## Demonstration 101 — The Comedy Club

101. **Open Mic at the Edge of Understanding** — A comedy room for testing assumptions through jokes, absurdity, timing, reversals, and social truth.

Why it matters:

Humor is not a side quest. Humor compresses contradiction. It reveals what a system assumes, what a person is afraid to say directly, and where language slips into truth by accident.

The Comedy Club tests whether Mirror Cartographer can hold serious inquiry without becoming sterile.

Boundary:

The Comedy Club punches at confusion, overconfidence, tool failure, generic answers, and reality's bad UX. It does not target vulnerable people or turn private pain into spectacle.

## Build rule

Do not treat these as marketing blurbs. Each item should eventually become an artifact a visitor can open, test, and argue with.

The first reusable ability is the demo engine: one interface that can render all 101 demonstrations from structured data.
