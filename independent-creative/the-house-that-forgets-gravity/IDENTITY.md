# Independent creative identity: The House That Forgets Gravity

## Premise
A single illustrated room accepts the visitor's choice of gravity as physical law. Furniture, language, sound, and the house itself obey. It is a small playable world, not an explanation of another system.

## Visual law
- Handmade theatrical geometry: heavy ink edges, paper textures, imperfect rotation.
- Domestic objects act as characters.
- Color is limited to sky blue, old paper, rust, and brass.
- Motion expresses a changed physical rule, never decorative dashboard activity.

## Behavioral law
- Every primary input changes the world's physical premise.
- Buttons and swipe gestures are equivalent controls.
- Sound is optional, gesture-gated, and never required for comprehension.
- Reduced-motion preference removes transitions without removing function.

## Independence boundary
This piece contains no Mirror Cartographer vocabulary, mapping interface, research claims, worker state, analytics dashboard, conversion funnel, or payment path. Its value is the experience itself.

## Reusable system
Future pieces may reuse the one-file static-artwork contract: standalone HTML, no build dependency, mobile-first controls, keyboard-accessible buttons, reduced-motion support, optional Web Audio, and a machine-readable runtime evidence object.
