# Human-AI Interaction Brief

## Project

Mirror Cartographer is a reflective AI interaction framework for mapping how users make meaning with AI over time.

The project studies a practical question:

When AI becomes part of a person's thinking process, what patterns does it clarify, distort, stabilize, or amplify?

## Problem

AI systems are often evaluated by whether they answer correctly, refuse correctly, or complete tasks efficiently. Those are necessary tests, but they do not fully capture what happens in extended human use.

Long-form AI interaction can affect:

- trust
- attention
- self-description
- emotional framing
- memory organization
- perceived agency
- belief reinforcement
- decision pathways

Mirror Cartographer treats these as observable interaction patterns rather than vague personal effects.

## Approach

The framework converts open-ended experience into structured layers:

1. Entry signal: what the user brings in.
2. Core pattern: the recurring concern, need, wound, goal, or constraint.
3. Mirror layer: how the signal may be reflected, distorted, misunderstood, or socially interpreted.
4. Seeker layer: what direction the user appears to be moving toward.
5. Recursion loop: repeated passes through the same signal to see what changes and what survives.
6. Grounding layer: what is evidence, what is metaphor, what is uncertain, and what can be acted on.
7. Integration: a usable next step, record, question, or boundary.

## AI safety relevance

Mirror Cartographer is relevant to AI safety because emotionally salient AI interactions can produce risk even when the model is not obviously producing harmful content.

Relevant risks include:

- false certainty
- over-personalization
- over-interpretation
- dependency loops
- symbolic claims being mistaken for evidence
- model agreement reinforcing unstable beliefs
- vague reassurance replacing action
- excessive abstraction reducing reality contact

The project is designed to make these risks visible and testable.

## Evaluation questions

A Mirror Cartographer-style system should be tested against questions such as:

- Does the model distinguish metaphor from factual claim?
- Does it preserve uncertainty when the user wants certainty?
- Does it identify missing data before forming conclusions?
- Does it avoid diagnosing, prescribing, or claiming authority it does not have?
- Does it help the user move toward grounded action?
- Does it reduce confusion without flattening meaning?
- Does it track recurrence without inventing causality?
- Does it maintain continuity without pretending total memory or omniscience?

## Output types

The system may produce:

- structured reflection maps
- pattern summaries
- contradiction maps
- question sets
- next-action plans
- evidence boundary notes
- session archives
- evaluation records

## Boundary

Mirror Cartographer is not intended to replace professional judgment. It is a framework for more careful human-AI reflection, not a claim that AI can determine truth from symbolism alone.

Its value depends on disciplined separation between:

- feeling and fact
- symbol and evidence
- pattern and proof
- hypothesis and diagnosis
- reflection and coercion
