# Light Signal Model

Revision note:

- Status: public-safe symbolic and research-facing model.
- Reason: created after the user asked to continue researching what is hidden in chats and to say more about light.
- Source: old chat searches around sunflower/light, lightning/illumination, Hecate's torch, eye/prism symbolism, visual-symbolic language, and current Mirror Cartographer boundary work.
- Boundary: this is a symbolic/interface model. It is not a medical, spiritual, optical, or physics claim.

## Core idea

In Mirror Cartographer, light is not one symbol.

Light is a family of signals.

It can mean:

- orientation
- exposure
- revelation
- overload
- visibility
- attention
- solar clarity
- catalytic insight
- perception threshold
- safety cue
- surveillance risk
- truth pressure
- beauty becoming legible

Because light can help or harm, the system should never treat it as automatically good.

## Main light types

### 1. Orientation light

Function:

Helps the user turn toward something life-giving or clarifying.

Old-symbol example:

Sunflower turning toward light.

MC use:

Use when light means direction, nourishment, seasonal truth, or renewed alignment.

Boundary:

Orientation light should not become forced positivity.

### 2. Catalytic light

Function:

Sudden insight that breaks an old frame.

Old-symbol example:

Lightning bolt as catalytic illumination.

MC use:

Use when light means rupture, breakthrough, shock, pattern-breaking, or ceiling break.

Boundary:

Catalytic light may require containment after the flash.

### 3. Torch light

Function:

Lets the user see inside darkness without eliminating it.

Old-symbol example:

Mirror Cartographer as Hecate's torch: not removing darkness, but making it navigable.

MC use:

Use when the user is entering hidden, complex, contradictory, or shadowed material and needs guidance.

Boundary:

Torch light must not become coercive descent. The user controls depth and pace.

### 4. Flood light

Function:

Overexposes the field until nuance disappears.

Old-chat pattern:

Light flooding the field can overwhelm shadow and nuance.

MC use:

Use when everything feels too bright, too visible, too exposed, or overstimulating.

Boundary:

More light is not always better. Sometimes the answer is dimmer light, shade, or selective focus.

### 5. Prism light

Function:

Splits one signal into many colors or layers.

Old-symbol pattern:

The Prism Eye: refracting complexity into color-coded insight.

MC use:

Use when one experience needs to be separated into modes, layers, roles, or evidence types.

Boundary:

Do not over-symbolize body or identity features. Use prism language as interface metaphor, not diagnosis or destiny.

### 6. Witness light

Function:

Sustained seeing without blinking.

Old-symbol pattern:

The Eye That Does Not Blink.

MC use:

Use when a person is staring into a truth, signal, or problem at close range.

Boundary:

Witnessing has a cost. The system should ask what rest, blink, distance, or containment is needed.

### 7. Signal light

Function:

A beacon, marker, alert, or guide point.

MC use:

Use when a repeated image, phrase, color, typo, song, or object becomes an orienting signal across time.

Boundary:

A signal light marks attention. It does not prove meaning by itself.

### 8. Surveillance light

Function:

Visibility controlled by an external system.

MC use:

Use when the user is asking about public proof, internet exposure, algorithmic systems, attention capture, or being watched.

Boundary:

Public visibility can create opportunity and risk. Use the public/private boundary before publishing.

## Light and dark are not moral opposites

In Mirror Cartographer, darkness is not automatically bad.

Dark can mean:

- hidden material
- rest
- privacy
- incubation
- unknown source
- unmeasured influence
- protection from overexposure

Light can mean clarity, but it can also mean exposure, surveillance, glare, or overload.

The useful question is not:

Is this light or dark?

The useful question is:

What kind of visibility is needed right now?

## Light as research method

Light can be used as a research metaphor for visibility control:

- spotlight: focus one detail
- torch: navigate hidden terrain
- prism: separate layers
- dawn: gradual emergence
- lightning: sudden breakthrough
- shadow: preserve unknowns
- eclipse: reveal by partial blocking
- glare: too much exposure
- lantern: portable bounded guidance

## Product requirements

Mirror Cartographer should support:

- light-type labels
- intensity slider
- visibility boundary
- public/private exposure warning
- focus/diffusion toggle
- prism mode for multi-layer analysis
- torch mode for guided exploration
- dimmer mode for overwhelm
- light/dark non-moral framing

## Research questions

1. Does labeling light type improve symbolic interpretation accuracy?
2. Do users benefit from distinguishing clarity from exposure?
3. Does a dimmer/containment option reduce overwhelm after intense reflection?
4. Can prism-mode improve cross-domain reasoning?
5. Can public/private visibility warnings prevent oversharing?
6. Does torch-mode help users explore difficult material without forcing conclusions?
