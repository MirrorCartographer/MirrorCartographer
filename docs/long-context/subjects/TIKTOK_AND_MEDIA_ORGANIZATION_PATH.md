Revision note:

- Status: AI-organized subject path from old conversations and library image/search results.
- Reason: created to preserve the saved-media organization thread as a practical Mirror Cartographer use case.
- Source: old conversations about organizing roughly 22,000 to 23,000 saved TikToks, the TikTok Cabinets mockup, and the five-category archive structure.

## Why this belongs in Mirror Cartographer

The saved TikTok/media problem was not just a content-management issue.

It revealed a deeper project need:

How can a huge archive of saved attention become searchable self-knowledge?

## Original practical problem

The user had tens of thousands of saved videos and wanted them organized without losing place, manually scrolling forever, or flattening the whole archive into generic folders.

Early categories included:

- music
- art
- fashion
- states
- food
- places
- ideas
- behavior
- trends

The five-category Mirror Cartographer archive then generalized this into:

- Body
- Style
- Build
- Map
- Signal

## Product idea: TikTok Cabinets

The TikTok Cabinets concept imagined saved videos organized into cabinets while preserving privacy and user control.

Important principles:

- nothing moves until the user approves
- AI can suggest categories
- saved media can become a research archive
- the user should not lose place
- categories should be browsable, searchable, and adjustable

## Mirror Cartographer translation

Saved media can be treated as signal.

A saved video may show:

- aesthetic preference
- emotional resonance
- repeated research topic
- future project seed
- body/health interest
- place or relocation interest
- humor style
- visual-symbolic language
- career or identity clue

## Product boundary

A saved item does not prove what a user wants or believes.

It is a low-confidence signal until confirmed by repetition, context, or user labeling.
