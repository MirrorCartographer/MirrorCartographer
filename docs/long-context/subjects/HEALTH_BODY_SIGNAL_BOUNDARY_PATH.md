# Health and Body-Signal Boundary Path

Revision note:

- Status: AI-organized subject path from old conversations and project memory.
- Reason: created to preserve the health/body-signal research path while keeping medical boundaries clear.
- Source: prior conversations about body symptoms, autonomic patterns, eye/neck/shoulder sensations, MGUS/ANA/HLA-B27 notes, doctor-prep language, and symbolic body mapping.
- Boundary: this is not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment guidance, or a replacement for professional care.

## Why this belongs in Mirror Cartographer

A major long-context thread involved body sensations that were hard to explain linearly.

Mirror Cartographer's role is not to diagnose them.

Its role is to help organize:

- what was felt
- where it was felt
- when it happened
- what changed it
- what repeated
- what needs professional review
- what should not be concluded yet

## Core principle

Symbol can organize attention.

Symbol cannot diagnose cause.

## Useful health-adjacent structure

### Observation

What happened, described as concretely as possible.

### Location

Where in the body the sensation or change appeared.

### Timing

When it happened, how long it lasted, and what came before it.

### Context

Sleep, food, heat, movement, stress, medication, travel, posture, period cycle, or environment.

### Change

What improved, worsened, moved, disappeared, or stayed.

### Boundary

What requires professional care, urgent care, veterinary care, or no interpretation yet.

## Product translation

Mirror Cartographer health-adjacent mode should produce:

- observation record
- body map
- timeline
- uncertainty label
- professional-care boundary
- questions for clinician/vet
- what not to conclude
- optional symbolic layer clearly marked as symbolic

## Old-chat insight retained

The user often described body and perception through symbolic or spatial language.

The system should not flatten that language into generic symptom checklists, but it also must not inflate it into medical certainty.
