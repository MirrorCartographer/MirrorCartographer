# Symbolic Field Engagement Workbook

This workbook turns Mirror Cartographer into a practice system.

It guides symbolic attention across five layers:

1. neural
2. somatic
3. field / signal
4. informational
5. mythic

The exercises are not presented as proof of external truth. They are structured reflection practices for noticing pattern, body response, attention, memory, and meaning.

## I. Neural layer: pattern anchor

Exercise:

1. Choose one symbol, such as Eye, Tree, Flame, Mirror, Cage, Bridge, or Sentinel.
2. Write down the first emotional memory it brings up.
3. Describe the pattern of thoughts associated with it.
4. Choose a short three-word phrase that combines the symbol and emotional pattern.
5. Repeat the phrase briefly as a focus cue.

Example:

Watch. Root. Rise.

## II. Somatic layer: symbol as gesture

Exercise:

1. Pick a body location that holds recurring tension, heaviness, heat, numbness, or pressure.
2. Assign it a symbol.
3. Create a small physical gesture that represents the symbol unlocking, softening, or transforming.
4. Repeat the gesture as a micro-ritual before rest, after overwhelm, or before a difficult task.

Example:

Chest = Cage. Gesture = opening arms while exhaling slowly.

## III. Field / signal layer: symbolic coherence ritual

Exercise:

1. Sit with feet grounded.
2. Visualize the chosen symbol.
3. Inhale while imagining the symbol expanding outward from the chest.
4. Exhale while drawing it back inward.
5. Repeat several times and record what changed in body sensation, emotion, or attention.

## IV. Informational layer: schema recode

Exercise:

1. Identify a default self-schema, such as "I am always behind."
2. Choose a symbol that contradicts or transforms it, such as Phoenix, Bridge, Compass, or Seed.
3. Write a short micro-myth from the symbol's perspective.
4. Read it aloud and record whether it changed posture, breath, mood, or action-readiness.

## V. Mythic layer: role invocation

Exercise:

1. Choose a role, such as Weaver, Oracle, Bridge, Sentinel, Cartographer, or Flame Holder.
2. Draw or describe yourself as that archetype.
3. Write one sentence that archetype would say to the current version of you.
4. Record both voices: who you are and who you are becoming.

## Safety boundary

These practices are for symbolic attention and reflection.

They do not prove causality, replace professional care, diagnose, or require belief in any metaphysical claim.

If an exercise increases distress, the system should shift toward grounding, rest, sensory orientation, or outside support.
